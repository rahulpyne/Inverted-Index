     <p>
      The
      <b>
       International Electrotechnical Commission
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       IEC
      </b>
      ; in
      <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
       French
      </a>
      :
      <i>
       Commission lectrotechnique internationale
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       CEI
      </i>
      )) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">
       non-profit
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">
       non-governmental
      </a>
      international
      <a href="/wiki/Standards_organization" title="Standards organization">
       standards organization
      </a>
      that prepares and publishes International Standards for all
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical" title="Electrical">
       electrical
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">
       electronic
      </a>
      and related technologies  collectively known as "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrotechnics" title="Electrotechnics">
       electrotechnology
      </a>
      ". IEC standards cover a vast range of technologies from power generation, transmission and distribution to home appliances and office equipment, semiconductors, fibre optics, batteries,
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar energy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">
       nanotechnology
      </a>
      and marine energy as well as many others. The IEC also manages three global conformity assessment systems that certify whether equipment, system or components conform to its International Standards.
     </p>
     <p>
      The IEC charter embraces all electrotechnologies including energy production and distribution, electronics, magnetics and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electromagnetics" title="Electromagnetics">
       electromagnetics
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electroacoustics" title="Electroacoustics">
       electroacoustics
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Multimedia" title="Multimedia">
       multimedia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">
       telecommunication
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_technology" title="Medical technology">
       medical technology
      </a>
      , as well as associated general disciplines such as terminology and symbols, electromagnetic compatibility (by its Advisory Committee on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_Compatibility" title="Electromagnetic Compatibility">
       Electromagnetic Compatibility
      </a>
      , ACEC), measurement and performance, dependability, design and development, safety and the environment.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Electrotechnical_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The IEC held its inaugural meeting on 26 June 1906, following discussions between the British
      <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Electrical_Engineers" title="Institution of Electrical Engineers">
       Institution of Electrical Engineers
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Electrical_Engineers" title="American Institute of Electrical Engineers">
       American Institute of Electrical Engineers
      </a>
      , and others, which began at the 1900
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_International_Electrical_Congress" title="Paris International Electrical Congress">
       Paris International Electrical Congress
      </a>
      , and continued with
      <a href="/wiki/Colonel_(United_Kingdom)" title="Colonel (United Kingdom)">
       Colonel
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/R._E._B._Crompton" title="R. E. B. Crompton">
       R. E. B. Crompton
      </a>
      playing a key role.
     </p>
     <p>
      The IEC was instrumental in developing and distributing standards for units of measurement, particularly
      <a href="/wiki/Gauss_(unit)" title="Gauss (unit)">
       Gauss
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hertz" title="Hertz">
       Hertz
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Weber_(unit)" title="Weber (unit)">
       Weber
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      It also first proposed a system of standards, the
      <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Giorgi" title="Giovanni Giorgi">
       Giorgi System
      </a>
      , which ultimately became the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SI" title="SI">
       SI
      </a>
      , or Systme International dunits (in English, the International System of Units).
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1938, it published a multilingual international vocabulary to unify terminology relating to electrical, electronic and related technologies. This effort continues, and the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Vocabulary" title="International Electrotechnical Vocabulary">
       International Electrotechnical Vocabulary
      </a>
      (the on-line version of which is known as the
      <i>
       Electropedia
      </i>
      ) remains an important work in the electrical and electronic industries.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/CISPR" title="CISPR">
       CISPR
      </a>
      (
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comit%C3%A9_International_Sp%C3%A9cial_des_Perturbations_Radio%C3%A9lectriques" title="Comit International Spcial des Perturbations Radiolectriques">
        Comit International Spcial des Perturbations Radiolectriques
       </a>
      </i>
      )  in English, the International Special Committee on Radio Interference  is one of the groups founded by the IEC.
     </p>
     <p>
      Currently, 82 countries are members while another 82 participate in the Affiliate Country Programme, which is not a form of membership but is designed to help industrializing countries get involved with the IEC. Originally located in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      , the commission moved to its current headquarters in
      <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">
       Geneva
      </a>
      in 1948. It has regional centres in Asia-Pacific (
      <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
       Singapore
      </a>
      ), Latin America (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(city)" title="So Paulo (city)">
       So Paulo
      </a>
      , Brazil) and North America (
      <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">
       Boston
      </a>
      , United States).
     </p>
     <p>
      Today, the IEC is the world's leading international organization in its field, and its standards are adopted as national standards by its members. The work is done by some 10,000 electrical and electronics experts from industry, government, academia, test labs and others with an interest in the subject.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="IEC_standards">
       IEC standards
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Electrotechnical_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: IEC standards">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_IEC_standards" title="List of IEC standards">
       List of IEC standards
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      IEC standards have numbers in the range 6000079999 and their titles take a form such as
      <i>
       IEC 60417: Graphical symbols for use on equipment
      </i>
      . The numbers of older IEC standards were converted in 1997 by adding 60000, for example IEC 27 became IEC 60027.
     </p>
     <p>
      The IEC cooperates closely with the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">
       International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">
       International Telecommunication Union (ITU)
      </a>
      . In addition, it works with several major standards development organizations, including the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IEEE" title="IEEE">
       IEEE
      </a>
      with which it signed a cooperation agreement in 2002, which was amended in 2008 to include joint development work.
     </p>
     <p>
      Standards developed jointly with ISO such as
      <i>
       ISO/IEC 26300, Open Document Format for Office Applications (OpenDocument) v1.0
      </i>
      carry the acronym of both organizations. The use of the ISO/IEC prefix covers publications from
      <a href="/wiki/ISO/IEC_JTC_1" title="ISO/IEC JTC 1">
       ISO/IEC Joint Technical Committee 1 - Information Technology
      </a>
      , as well as conformity assessment standards developed by ISO CASCO and IEC CAB (Conformity Assessment Board). Other standards developed in cooperation between IEC and ISO are assigned numbers in the 80000 series, such as IEC 82045-1.
     </p>
     <p>
      The 60000 series of standards are also found preceded by EN to indicate the IEC standards harmonized as European standards; for example IEC 60034 would be EN 60034.
     </p>
     <p>
      IEC standards are also being adopted as harmonized standards by other certifying bodies such as
      <a href="/wiki/BSI_Group" title="BSI Group">
       BSI
      </a>
      (United Kingdom),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Standards_Association" title="Canadian Standards Association">
       CSA
      </a>
      (Canada),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underwriters_Laboratories" title="Underwriters Laboratories">
       UL
      </a>
      &amp;
      <a href="/wiki/American_National_Standards_Institute" title="American National Standards Institute">
       ANSI
      </a>
      /
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/InterNational_Committee_for_Information_Technology_Standards" title="InterNational Committee for Information Technology Standards">
       INCITS
      </a>
      (United States),
      <a href="/wiki/South_African_Bureau_of_Standards" title="South African Bureau of Standards">
       SABS
      </a>
      (South Africa),
      <a href="/wiki/SAI_Global" title="SAI Global">
       SAI
      </a>
      (Australia),
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Standards_Press_for_China&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Standards Press for China (page does not exist)">
       SPC
      </a>
      /
      <a href="/wiki/Guobiao_standards" title="Guobiao standards">
       GB
      </a>
      (China) and
      <a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Institut_f%C3%BCr_Normung" title="Deutsches Institut fr Normung">
       DIN
      </a>
      (Germany). IEC standards harmonized by other certifying bodies generally have some noted differences from the original IEC standard.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">

         Full members
        </div>
        <div class="legend">
         <span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #00ff00; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">

     <p>
      The IEC is made up of members, called national committees, and each NC represents its nation's electrotechnical interests in the IEC. This includes manufacturers, providers, distributors and vendors, consumers and users, all levels of governmental agencies, professional societies and trade associations as well as standards developers from national standards bodies. National committees are constituted in different ways. Some NCs are public sector only, some are a combination of public and private sector, and some are private sector only. About 90% of those who prepare IEC standards work in industry.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Affiliates">
       Affiliates
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Electrotechnical_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Affiliates">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2001 and in response to calls from the WTO to open itself to more developing nations, the IEC launched the Affiliate Country Programme to encourage developing nations to become involved in the Commission's work or to use its International Standards. Countries signing a pledge to participate in the work and to encourage the use of IEC Standards in national standards and regulations are granted access to a limited number of technical committee documents for the purposes of commenting. In addition, they can select a limited number of IEC Standards for their national standards' library. Countries as of 2011 participating in the Affiliate Country Programme are:

       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
