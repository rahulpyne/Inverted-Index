     <p>
      The
      <b>
       London Array
      </b>
      is a 175 turbine 630MW
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" title="Round 2 wind farm">
       Round 2 offshore wind farm
      </a>
      located 20Â km off the
      <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">
       Kent
      </a>
      coast in the outer
      <a href="/wiki/Thames_Estuary" title="Thames Estuary">
       Thames Estuary
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      . It is the largest offshore wind farm in the world, and the largest wind farm in Europe by megawatt capacity.
     </p>
     <p>
      Construction of phase 1 of the wind farm began in March 2011 and was completed by mid 2013, being formally inaugurated by the
      <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">
       Prime Minister of the United Kingdom
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">
       David Cameron
      </a>
      on 4 July 2013.
     </p>
     <p>
      The second phase of the project was refused planning consent in 2014 due to concerns on the impact to sea birds.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The wind farm site is more than 20 kilometres (12Â mi) off the
      <a href="/wiki/North_Foreland" title="North Foreland">
       North Foreland
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">
       Kent
      </a>
      coast in the area between Long Sand and
      <a href="/wiki/Kentish_Knock_(England)" title="Kentish Knock (England)">
       Kentish Knock
      </a>
      , between
      <a href="/wiki/Margate" title="Margate">
       Margate
      </a>
      in Kent and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clacton" title="Clacton">
       Clacton
      </a>
      in Essex.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pim_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pim-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The site is mostly away from shipping lanes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The first phase consisted of 175
      <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">
       Siemens Wind Power
      </a>
      SWT-3.6 turbines and two offshore substations, giving a wind farm with a peak rated power of 630Â MW.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nseng_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nseng-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Each turbine and offshore substation is erected on a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monopile_foundation" title="Monopile foundation">
       monopile foundation
      </a>
      , and connected together by 210 kilometres (130Â mi) of 33Â kV array cables. The two offshore substations are connected to an onshore substation at Cleve Hill (near
      <a href="/wiki/Graveney" title="Graveney">
       Graveney
      </a>
      ) on the north Kent coast, by four 150Â kV subsea export cables, in total 220 kilometres (140Â mi).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nseng_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-nseng-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is named after London because the power goes to the London grid.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The smaller
      <a href="/wiki/Thanet_Wind_Farm" title="Thanet Wind Farm">
       Thanet Wind Farm
      </a>
      is to the south of it.
     </p>
     <p>
      The array is intended to reduce annual
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CO2" title="CO2">
       CO
       <sub>
        2
       </sub>
      </a>
      emissions by about 900,000 tons, equal to the emissions of 300,000 passenger cars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2001 environmental studies identified areas of the outer
      <a href="/wiki/Thames_Estuary" title="Thames Estuary">
       Thames Estuary
      </a>
      as potential sites for offshore wind farm;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-la6_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-la6-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the
      <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Trade_and_Industry_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department of Trade and Industry (United Kingdom)">
       Department of Trade and Industry
      </a>
      published the paper
      <i>
       Future Offshore -A Strategic Framework for the Offshore Wind Industry
      </i>
      , which identified the outer Thames Estuary as one of three potential areas for future wind farm development (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" title="Round 2 wind farm">
       Round 2 wind farms
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Crown_Estate" title="Crown Estate">
       Crown Estate
      </a>
      awarded a 50-year lease to
      <i>
       London Array Ltd.
      </i>
      (a consortium of E.ON UK Renewables, Shell WindEnergy, and CORE Limited.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [note 1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      ) in December 2003.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-la6_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-la6-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2005_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbc2005-12">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      A planning application was submitted in 2005,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2005_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbc2005-12">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      which was approved in December 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Planning permission for the onshore electricity substation was received in November 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onfarm1_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-onfarm1-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In May 2008, Shell announced that it was pulling out of the project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was announced in July 2008 that E.ON UK and DONG Energy would buy Shell's stake.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Subsequently on 16 October 2008, London Array announced the
      <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">
       Abu Dhabi
      </a>
      based Masdar would join E.ON as a joint venture party in the scheme. Under the agreement, Masdar purchased 40% of E.ON's half share of the scheme, giving Masdar a 20% stake in the project overall.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-masdar_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-masdar-18">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The resultant ownership was 50%
      <a href="/wiki/DONG_Energy" title="DONG Energy">
       DONG Energy
      </a>
      , 30%
      <a href="/wiki/E.ON_UK" title="E.ON UK">
       E.ON UK Renewables
      </a>
      and 20%
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Future_Energy_Company" title="Abu Dhabi Future Energy Company">
       Masdar
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ingdong_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ingdong-19">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In March 2009, the backers agreed on an initial investment of â¬2.2Â billion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Financing of phase 1 was achieved through the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">
       European Investment Bank
      </a>
      and the Danish Export Credit Fund with Â£250 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ingdong_19-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ingdong-19">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2013, in response to "Offshore Transmission Owner" regulations (see
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ofgem" title="Ofgem">
       Ofgem
      </a>
      ), the consortium divested the electrical transmission assets of the wind farm (valued at Â£459 million) to "Blue Transmission London Array Limited" â an entity incorporated by Barclays Infrastructure Funds Management Limited (
      <a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">
       Barclays
      </a>
      ) and Diamond UK Transmission Corporation (
      <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Corporation" title="Mitsubishi Corporation">
       Mitsubishi Corporation
      </a>
      subsidiary).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thames_Estuary_and_Wind_Farms_from_Space_NASA_with_annotations.jpg">

     <p>
      In January 2014 Dong sold half its stake to Quebec public pension plan manager
      <a href="/wiki/Caisse_de_d%C3%A9p%C3%B4t_et_placement_du_Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Caisse de dÃ©pÃ´t et placement du QuÃ©bec">
       Caisse de dÃ©pÃ´t et placement du QuÃ©bec
      </a>
      ("La Caisse").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      At the time of its construction it was the largest offshore wind farm in the world.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_commissioning">
       Construction and commissioning
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Construction and commissioning">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Offshore work began in March 2011;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offshore051112_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-offshore051112-24">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the first foundation was installed in March 2011
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Turbines were supplied by
      <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">
       Siemens Wind Power
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siemens_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-siemens-26">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their foundations were built by the joint-venture between Per Aarsleff and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bilfinger_Berger_Ingenieurbau_GmbH&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bilfinger Berger Ingenieurbau GmbH (page does not exist)">
       Bilfinger Berger Ingenieurbau GmbH
      </a>
      . The same company supplied and installed the monopiles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upstream141209_27-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-upstream141209-27">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Generators were installed by MPI and
      <a href="/wiki/A2SEA" title="A2SEA">
       A2SEA
      </a>
      by using an installation vessel
      <a href="/wiki/TIV_MPI_Adventure" title="TIV MPI Adventure">
       TIV
       <i>
        MPI Adventure
       </i>
      </a>
      and a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack-up" title="Jack-up">
       jack-up
      </a>
      barge
      <i>
       Sea Worker
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-array040210_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-array040210-28">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Two offshore substations were designed, fabricated and installed by Future Energy, a joint venture between Fabricom, Iemants and Geosea, while electrical systems and onshore substation work was undertaken by Siemens Transmission &amp; Distribution. The subsea export cable was supplied by
      <a href="/wiki/Nexans" title="Nexans">
       Nexans
      </a>
      and array cables by JDR Cable Systems. The array cables and the export cables were installed by VSMC.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upstream141209_27-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-upstream141209-27">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The wind farm started producing electricity at the end of October 2012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offshore051112_24-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-offshore051112-24">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      All 175 turbines of phase 1 were confirmed fully operational on 8 April 2013,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-renewableuk_29-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-renewableuk-29">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the wind farm was formally inaugurated by the Prime minister
      <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">
       David Cameron
      </a>
      on 4 July 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20130704gulfnews_30-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-20130704gulfnews-30">
        [29]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In December 2015 it produced 369 GWh; a monthly
      <a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">
       capacity factor
      </a>
      of 78.9%. It produced 2.5 TWh in 2015. During two days of January 2016, production varied from 3 MW to 619 MW.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        [30]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        [31]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_2">
       Phase 2
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Phase 2">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A second phase was planned which would have seen a further 166 turbines installed to increase the capacity to 1000MW.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc140219_33-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbc140219-33">
        [32]
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, the second phase was scaled back and finally cancelled in February 2014 after concerns were raised by the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_for_the_Protection_of_Birds" title="Royal Society for the Protection of Birds">
       Royal Society for the Protection of Birds
      </a>
      about its effect on a local population of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red-throated_diver" title="Red-throated diver">
       red-throated divers
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc140219_33-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-bbc140219-33">
        [32]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        [33]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
