     <p>
      <b>
       Carbon Recycling International Inc.
      </b>
      (CRI) is an Icelandic
      <a href="/wiki/Renewable_fuels" title="Renewable fuels">
       renewable
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">
       methanol
      </a>
      company. Its emissions-to-liquid process uses
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">
       carbon dioxide
      </a>
      and water and electricity to create
      <a href="/wiki/Methanol_fuel" title="Methanol fuel">
       methanol fuel
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      CRI, incorporated in 2006, was founded by Fridrik Jonsson, Art Shulenberger, Oddur Ingolfsson, and KC Tran.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Partners include
      <a href="/wiki/Ol%C3%ADs" title="Olís">
       Olís
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HS_Orka" title="HS Orka">
       HS Orka
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Grindav%C3%ADk" title="Grindavík">
       Grindavík municipality
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mannvit_Engineering" title="Mannvit Engineering">
       Mannvit Engineering
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Government_agencies_in_Iceland" title="Government agencies in Iceland">
       Innovation Center Iceland
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      CRI's first commercial scale plant, the George Olah Plant (named after
      <a href="/wiki/George_Andrew_Olah" title="George Andrew Olah">
       George Andrew Olah
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the Nobel Prize Laureate), was completed in 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Renewable_methanol">
       Renewable methanol
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Renewable methanol">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Renewable methanol is methanol derived from renewable energy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It can be blended into transportation fuel or processed as a chemical feedstock. RM is also a viable input for
      <a href="/wiki/Biodiesel" title="Biodiesel">
       biodiesel
      </a>
      production. Fuel blends range from RM3 (3% methanol and 97% other) to RM100 (100% methanol with trace additives). High percentage blends can be used with
      <a href="/wiki/Flexible-fuel_vehicle" title="Flexible-fuel vehicle">
       flexible-fuel vehicles
      </a>
      (FFVs), while low percentage blends can be used in existing cars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      CRI is interested in increasing the number of flex-fuel cars in Iceland and supplying RM85.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_concerns">
       Usage concerns
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Usage concerns">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Alcohols, including methanol and ethanol, can be
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corrosive" title="Corrosive">
       corrosive
      </a>
      to certain materials in some vehicles. Replacement parts may be necessary for higher blends, but most modern cars are capable of using low blends of methanol.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:117px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="1080" height="204" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg/115px-Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg/173px-Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg/230px-Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg 2x" width="115"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carbon_Recycling_International_GO_Plant.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        CRI's first CSP, the GO Plant
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Production of renewable methanol is similar to that of conventional methanol, except that carbon dioxide is substituted for
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">
       carbon monoxide
      </a>
      and hydrogen is produced by electrolysis of water.
     </p>
     <p>
      RM has no connection to agriculture, as water, electricity, and carbon dioxide are the primary inputs of its production process. Production of RM is less vulnerable to changes in feedstock prices.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plants">
       Plants
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Plants">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The George Olah Plant, or the GO Plant, has a capacity of 5 million liters per year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is located close to the
      <a href="/wiki/Blue_Lagoon_(geothermal_spa)" title="Blue Lagoon (geothermal spa)">
       Blue Lagoon
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Svartsengi_Power_Station" title="Svartsengi Power Station">
       Svartsengi power station
      </a>
      . The plant is expected to use around 10% of the carbon dioxide produced by Svartsengi power station.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legislation">
       Legislation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legislation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      's renewable energy
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewables_Directive" title="Renewables Directive">
       directives
      </a>
      consider RM to be a renewable fuel, making it a candidate for the EU's renewable fuel blending mandates.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">
       Impact
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Recycling_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Impact">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Carbon dioxide is a major component of
      <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">
       global warming
      </a>
      . By removing carbon dioxide from industrial emissions, CRI's process could help mitigate environmental impact. Renewable methanol burns cleanly as a fuel, but it has trace amounts of impurities.
     </p>
     <p>
      CRI's process can also be used to store energy in the form of methanol, especially in cases where the energy source is inconsistent. For example,
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">
       wind
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar power
      </a>
      are irregularly available.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Methanol is also a good energy carrier, being less volatile and easier to store than hydrogen.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Future_projects">
       Future projects
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        ]
       </span>

     <p>
      CRI plans to implement standardized CSPs (commercial scale plants), each with a capacity of 50 million liters per year.

       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]

      </sup>
      plant in
      <a href="/wiki/H%C3%BAsav%C3%ADk" title="Húsavík">
       Húsavík
      </a>
      , having signed a memorandum of understanding with power company
      <a href="/wiki/Landsvirkjun" title="Landsvirkjun">
       Landsvirkjun
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
