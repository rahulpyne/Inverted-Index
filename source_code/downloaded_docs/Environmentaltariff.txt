     <p>
      An
      <b>
       Environmental tariff
      </b>
      , also known as a
      <b>
       green tariff
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       eco-tariff
      </b>
      , is an import or export tax placed on products being imported from, or also being sent to countries with substandard environmental pollution controls. They can be used as controls on global pollution and can also be considered as corrective measures against "environmental races to the bottom" and "eco-dumping".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kraus_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Kraus-1">
        [1]

      There has been debate on the role that increased international trade has played in increasing pollution.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">
       environmental degradation
      </a>
      and a global
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tragedy_of_the_commons" title="Tragedy of the commons">
        tragedy of the commons
       </a>
      </i>
      are intimately linked to increases in international trade, others have argued that as citizens become more affluent they'll also advocate for cleaner environments. According to a
      <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">
       World Bank
      </a>
      paper:
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       "Since freer trade raises income, it directly contributes to increasing pollution levels via the scale effect. However, it thereby induces the composition (and) technique effects of increased income, both of which tend to reduce pollution levels".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fredricksson_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Fredricksson-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dean_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dean-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_tariff_implementation_proposal">
       Early tariff implementation proposal
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_tariff&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early tariff implementation proposal">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Although the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      has in the past been accused of dragging its feet on implementing tough new anti-pollution measures, it was the originator of a legislative proposal suggesting an environmental tariff be applied against exporting countries whose exports gained significant cost advantages due to less stringent environmental regulations. The proposed legislation was tabled as the
      <b>
       International Pollution Deterrence Act of 1991
      </b>
      and was introduced in its Senate in April of that year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WB_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-WB-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_International_Pollution_Control_Index">
       Proposed International Pollution Control Index
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_tariff&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Proposed International Pollution Control Index">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A notable feature of the proposed U.S.
      <i>
       International Pollution Deterrence Act
      </i>
      was the international pollution control index it cited within its Section 5, which read:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thomas_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Thomas-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       <b>
        INTERNATIONAL POLLUTION CONTROL INDEX
       </b>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Section 8002 of the Solid Waste Disposal Act (42 U.S.C. 6982) is amended by adding the following new subsections at the end thereof:
       <br/>
      </p>
      `(t) The Administrator shall prepare, within one hundred and twenty days of the enactment of this section and yearly thereafter, a pollution control index for each of the top fifty countries identified by the Office of Trade and Investment of the Department of Commerce based on the value of exports to the United States from that country's attainment of pollution control standards in the areas of air, water, hazardous waste and solid waste as compared to the United States. The purpose of this index is to measure the level of compliance within each country with standards comparable to or greater than those in the United States. The Administrator shall analyze, in particular, the level of technology employed and actual costs incurred for pollution control in the major export sectors of each country in formulating the index.
     </blockquote>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Agitation_against_use_of_environmental_tariffs">
       Agitation against use of environmental tariffs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_tariff&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Agitation against use of environmental tariffs">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Environment tariffs were not implemented in the past, in part, because they were not sanctioned by multilateral trade regimes such as the
      <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">
       World Trade Organization
      </a>
      and within the
      <a href="/wiki/General_Agreement_on_Tariffs_and_Trade" title="General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade">
       General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade
      </a>
      (GATT), a fact which generated considerable criticism and calls for reform.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kraus_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Kraus-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Additionally, many foreign factory owners in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newly_industrialized_countries" title="Newly industrialized countries">
       newly industrialized countries
      </a>
      and underdeveloped countries saw the attempts to impose pollution controls on them as suspicious...
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       "...seeing it as a threat to their growth and fearing that developed countries would attempt to export their preferences for pollution control or to place 'environmental' tariffs on imports from countries with lower standards."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leonard_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Leonard-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </dd>
     </dl>
