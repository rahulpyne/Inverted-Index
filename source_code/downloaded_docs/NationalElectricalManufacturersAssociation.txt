     <p>
      The
      <b>
       National Electrical Manufacturers Association
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       NEMA
      </b>
      )
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      is the largest trade association of electrical equipment manufacturers in the United States.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was founded in 1926 and maintains its headquarters in
      <a href="/wiki/Rosslyn,_Virginia" title="Rosslyn, Virginia">
       Rosslyn, Virginia
      </a>
      , just outside Washington, D.C.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its approximately 450
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      member companies
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      manufacture products used in the
      <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">
       generation
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Power_transmission" title="Power transmission">
       transmission
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_distribution" title="Power distribution">
       distribution
      </a>
      , control, and end use of electricity. These products are used in utility, industrial, commercial, institutional, and residential applications. The association’s
      <a href="/wiki/Medical_Imaging_and_Technology_Alliance" title="Medical Imaging and Technology Alliance">
       Medical Imaging &amp; Technology Alliance (MITA)
      </a>
      division represents manufacturers of cutting-edge medical diagnostic imaging equipment including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MRI" title="MRI">
       MRI
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X-ray_computed_tomography" title="X-ray computed tomography">
       CT
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">
       x-ray
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Ultrasound" title="Ultrasound">
       ultrasound
      </a>
      products.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      NEMA also has offices in Beijing and Mexico City.
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to
      <a href="/wiki/Lobbying" title="Lobbying">
       lobbying
      </a>
      activities, NEMA publishes more than 600 standards,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      application guides,
      <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">
       white papers
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and technical papers. Among its major standards are those for AC plugs and recepticles; the
      <a href="/wiki/NEMA_connector" title="NEMA connector">
       NEMA connectors
      </a>
      are universal in North America and also used by some other countries.
     </p>
