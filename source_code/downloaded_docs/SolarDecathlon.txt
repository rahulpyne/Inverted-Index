     <p>
      The U.S.
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">
       Department of Energy
      </a>
      <b>
       Solar Decathlon
      </b>
      is an international competition that challenges 20 collegiate teams to design, build, and operate the most attractive, effective, and energy-efficient
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar-powered
      </a>
      house. The winner of the competition is the team that best blends affordability, consumer appeal, and design excellence with optimal energy production and efficiency.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first Solar Decathlon was held in 2002. The competition has since occurred biennially in 2005, 2007, 2009, 2011 and 2013;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the next Solar Decathlon will take place in
      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
       California
      </a>
      in October 2015.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Open to the public and free of charge, the Solar Decathlon allows visitors to tour ultra-efficient houses, gather ideas to use in their own homes, and learn how energy-saving features can help reduce power bills.
     </p>
     <p>
      The competition is presented by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Energy" title="U.S. Department of Energy">
       U.S. Department of Energy
      </a>
      and organized by the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Renewable_Energy_Laboratory" title="National Renewable Energy Laboratory">
       National Renewable Energy Laboratory
      </a>
      (NREL). There is also a
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_Decathlon_Europe" title="Solar Decathlon Europe">
       Solar Decathlon Europe
      </a>
      , which was established under a 2007 agreement between the United States and
      <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
       Spain
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Solar Decathlon China was established with the signing of a memorandum of understanding between the U.S. Department of Energy, China’s National Energy Administration,
      <a href="/wiki/Peking_University" title="Peking University">
       Peking University
      </a>
      and Applied Materials on January 20, 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The first Solar Decathlon China was held in 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="792" data-file-width="612" height="285" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg/220px-Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg/330px-Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg/440px-Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Solar_Decathlon_2011_Map.svg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The Solar Decathlon 2011 was held in the
        <a href="/wiki/National_Mall" title="National Mall">
         National Mall
        </a>
        ’s
        <a href="/wiki/West_Potomac_Park" title="West Potomac Park">
         West Potomac Park
        </a>
        in Washington D.C.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The inaugural Solar Decathlon was open to the public between September 19 and October 6, 2002. Fourteen teams from across the United States, including
      <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">
       Puerto Rico
      </a>
      , presented their projects on the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Mall" title="National Mall">
       National Mall
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">
       Washington, D.C.
      </a>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Colorado" title="University of Colorado">
       University of Colorado
      </a>
      was awarded first place. At the second Solar Decathlon, likewise held on the National Mall on October 6–16, 2005, eighteen teams from the United States, Canada, and Spain participated; the University of Colorado successfully defended its championship.
     </p>
     <p>
      The third Solar Decathlon took place on the National Mall on October 12–20, 2007. Twenty teams from the United States, Canada, Spain, and Germany competed, and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darmstadt_University_of_Technology" title="Darmstadt University of Technology">
       Technische Universität Darmstadt
      </a>
      (Team Germany) was named the overall champion. The fourth Solar Decathlon was held on the National Mall on October 8–18, 2009, and included teams from the United States, Canada, Germany, and Spain; Team Germany was named the winner for a second time.
     </p>
     <p>
      The fifth Solar Decathlon took place between September 23 and October 2, 2011, with nineteen participating teams representing the United States, China, New Zealand, Belgium, and Canada. The event was held in Washington D.C.'s
      <a href="/wiki/West_Potomac_Park" title="West Potomac Park">
       West Potomac Park
      </a>
      , near the
      <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">
       Potomac River
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Tidal_Basin" title="Tidal Basin">
       Tidal Basin
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt_Memorial" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial">
       Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial
      </a>
      , along a road between the
      <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Memorial" title="Lincoln Memorial">
       Lincoln
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Memorial" title="Jefferson Memorial">
       Jefferson Memorials
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" title="University of Maryland">
       University of Maryland
      </a>
      was the overall competition winner.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The sixth Solar Decathlon took place on October 3–13, 2013, in
      <a href="/wiki/Orange_County_Great_Park" title="Orange County Great Park">
       Orange County Great Park
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Irvine,_California" title="Irvine, California">
       Irvine, California
      </a>
      – it was the first Solar Decathlon to take place outside Washington D.C., and was won by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vienna_University_of_Technology" title="Vienna University of Technology">
       Vienna University of Technology
      </a>
      (Team Austria).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The seventh Solar Decathlon will take place in Cali, Colombia, as the first version in Latin America &amp; Caribbean, with main focus on social housing, regional relevance and density.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">
       Awards
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2010, the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Building_Museum" title="National Building Museum">
       National Building Museum
      </a>
      awarded the Solar Decathlon an
      <a href="/wiki/Honor_Award" title="Honor Award">
       Honor Award
      </a>
      for its emphasis on "renewable energy, energy-efficient, and environmentally responsible systems" and its role in "educating a new generation of built-environment professionals".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_of_contests">
       Scope of contests
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scope of contests">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Solar Decathlon organizers selected the following ten contests for the 2011 competition. Each contest was worth a maximum of 100 points, for a possible total of 1,000 points. Teams earn points through task completion, performance monitoring, and jury evaluation.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_1:_Architecture">
       Contest 1: Architecture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Contest 1: Architecture">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Teams are required to design and build attractive, high-performance houses that integrate solar and energy-efficiency technology seamlessly into the design. A jury of professional architects evaluates team construction documents and the final constructed house. They evaluate three main factors: architectural elements, holistic design, and inspiration.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_2:_Market_Appeal">
       Contest 2: Market Appeal
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Contest 2: Market Appeal">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      For the Market Appeal Contest, teams build their houses for a target market of their choosing. Teams are then asked to demonstrate the potential of their houses to keep costs affordable within that market. A jury of professionals from the homebuilding industry evaluates how well suited each house is for everyday living; determines whether the construction documents would enable a contractor to construct the house as intended, and assesses whether the house offers potential homebuyers within the target market a good value.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_3:_Engineering">
       Contest 3: Engineering
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Contest 3: Engineering">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Solar Decathlon houses are marvels of modern engineering, and this contest “checks under the hood.” A jury of professional engineers evaluates each house for functionality, efficiency, innovation, and reliability.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_4:_Communications">
       Contest 4: Communications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Contest 4: Communications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Solar Decathlon challenges teams to communicate about the technical aspects of their houses, as well as their experiences, to a wide audience through Web sites and exhibits of their houses on the National Mall. The Communications Contest awards points to teams based on their success in delivering clear and consistent messages and images that represent the vision, process, and results of each team’s project. A jury of Web site development and public relations experts will evaluate the team Web sites, communications plans, and student-led house tours for effectiveness.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_5:_Affordability">
       Contest 5: Affordability
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Contest 5: Affordability">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      New for the U.S. Department of Energy Solar Decathlon 2011, the Affordability Contest encourages teams to design and build affordable houses that combine energy-efficient construction and appliances with renewable energy systems. A professional estimator will determine the construction cost of each house. Teams can earn the maximum possible 100 points for achieving a target construction cost of $250,000 or less. A sliding point scale will be applied to houses with estimated construction costs between $250,001 and $600,000. Houses with estimated costs that are more than $600,000 will receive zero points.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_6:_Comfort_Zone">
       Contest 6: Comfort Zone
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Contest 6: Comfort Zone">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Teams competing in the Solar Decathlon design their houses to maintain steady, uniform indoor environmental conditions. During the competition, full points are awarded for maintaining narrow temperature and relative humidity ranges inside the houses.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_7:_Hot_water">
       Contest 7: Hot water
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Contest 7: Hot water">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      This contest demonstrates that a solar-powered house can provide all of the energy necessary to heat water for domestic uses. Teams score points in this contest by successfully completing several daily hot water draws.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_8:_Appliances">
       Contest 8: Appliances
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Contest 8: Appliances">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Appliances Contest is designed to mimic the appliance use and amenity in the average U.S. home while using less energy. Points are earned for refrigerating and freezing food, washing and drying laundry, and running the dishwasher.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_9:_Home_Entertainment">
       Contest 9: Home Entertainment
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Contest 9: Home Entertainment">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Home Entertainment Contest is designed to demonstrate that houses powered solely by the sun can deliver more than just basic household functionality. They can also provide a comfortable setting with power for the electronics, appliances, and modern conveniences that we love. The Home Entertainment Contest gauges whether the house has what it takes to be a home. Can it accommodate the pleasures of living, such as sharing meals with friends and family, watching television, or surfing the Web?
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Contest_10:_Energy_Balance">
       Contest 10: Energy Balance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_Decathlon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Contest 10: Energy Balance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      This contest demonstrates that the sun can supply the energy necessary for all the daily energy demands of a small household. For the contest, each house is equipped with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Net_metering" title="Net metering">
        Net metering
       </a>
      </i>
      , a utility meter that measures the energy a house produces and consumes over the course of the competition. A team receives full points for producing at least as much energy as its house needs.
     </p>
