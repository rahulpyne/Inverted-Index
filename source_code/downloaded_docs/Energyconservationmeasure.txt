   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Energy conservation measure
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">
       energy conservation
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      An
      <b>
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">
        Energy conservation
       </a>
       measure
      </b>
      (ECM) is any type of project conducted, or technology implemented, to reduce the consumption of energy in a building. The types of projects implemented can be in a variety of forms but usually are designed to reduce utility costs: water, electricity and gas being the main three for industrial and commercial enterprises. The aim of an ECM should be to achieve a savings, reducing the amount of
      <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">
       energy
      </a>
      used by a particular process, technology or facility.
     </p>
     <p>
      Energy conservation measures are often combined into larger guaranteed
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_Savings_Performance_Contract" title="Energy Savings Performance Contract">
       Energy Savings Performance Contracts
      </a>
      to maximize energy savings while minimizing disruption to building occupants by coordinating renovations. Some ECMs cost less to implement yet return a higher energy savings. Traditionally, lighting projects were a good example of “low hanging fruit”
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      that could be used to drive implementation of more substantial upgrades to HVAC systems in large facilities. Smaller buildings might combine window replacement with modern insulation using advanced building foams to improve energy performance. Energy dashboard projects
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      are a new kind of ECM which relies on the behavioral change of building occupants to save energy. When implemented as part of a program, case studies (such as that for the DC Schools) report energy savings up 30%.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Under the right circumstances, open energy dashboards can even be implemented for free
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      to improve upon these savings even more.
     </p>
     <p>
      On a global basis
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">
       energy efficiency
      </a>
      works behind the scenes to improve our energy security, lower our energy bills and move us closer to reaching our climate goals. According to the IEA, some 40% of the global energy efficiency market is financed with debt and equity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_Performance_Investment" title="Energy Performance Investment">
       Energy Performance Investment
      </a>
      are one financing mechanism by which ECMs can be implemented now and paid for by the savings realized over the life of the project. While all 50 states, Puerto Rico and Washington, D.C., have statutes allowing companies to offer energy savings performance contracts, success varies because of variations in the approach, the state’s degree of involvement and other factors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Homes and businesses are implementing energy-efficiency measures that include low-energy lighting, insulation and even high tech energy dashboards to cut bills by avoiding waste and boosting productivity.
     </p>
     <p>
      Businesses implementing ECMs in their commercial buildings often employ
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_service_company" title="Energy service company">
       Energy Service Companies
      </a>
      (ESCOs) experienced in energy performance contracting . This industry has been around since the 1970s and is more prevalent than ever today. The US-based organization EVO (Efficiency Valuation Organization)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      has created a set of guidelines for ESCOs to adhere to in evaluating the savings achieved by ECMs. These guidelines are called the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IPMVP" title="IPMVP">
       International Performance Measurement and Verification Protocol
      </a>
      (IPMVP).
     </p>
     <p>
      Homeowners implementing ECMs in their residential buildings often start with an
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">
       energy audit
      </a>
      . This is a way homeowners look at what areas of their homes are using, and possibly losing energy. Residential energy auditors are accredited by the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Building_Performance_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Building Performance Institute (page does not exist)">
       Building Performance Institute
      </a>
      (BPI)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      or the Residential Energy Services Network (
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=RESNET&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="RESNET (page does not exist)">
       RESNET
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-energy.ca.gov_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-energy.ca.gov-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southface_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Southface-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Homeowners can hire a professional or do it themselves
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      or use a
      <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">
       smartphone
      </a>
      to help do an audit.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_Dashboards">
       Energy Dashboards
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation_measure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Energy Dashboards">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Energy Dashboards combine smart metering and Internet technologies to provide real-time data on energy use. Their success is based on the premise that real-time feedback drives behavior change and improves operational efficiency. Energy Dashboards are used to enable energy reduction competitions, showcase real-time building performance and
      <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">
       green building
      </a>
      features, and empower occupants to become active participants in energy management.
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">
       Sustainability
      </a>
      teams from major corporations, governments, universities and K–12 public schools use energy dashboards as an effective communication tool that creates transparency.
     </p>

      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/BACnet" title="BACnet">

       +
       <a href="/wiki/Drupal" title="Drupal">

      </li>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Lighting">
       Lighting
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation_measure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lighting">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      One of the simplest ways consumers save a copious amount of energy is switching
      <a href="/wiki/Incandescent_light_bulb" title="Incandescent light bulb">
       incandescent light bulb
      </a>
      to a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent" title="Compact fluorescent">
       compact fluorescent
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">
       CFL
      </a>
      is capable of providing just as much light as a 60W incandescent, while consuming just one fourth of the amount of energy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Water">
       Water
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation_measure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Water">

     <p>
      The average US homes wastes thousands of gallons of water a year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>

       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>

       1.6 GPM or less

        low flow showerheads

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Windows">
       Windows
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation_measure&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Windows">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">
       Windows
      </a>
      may be one of the biggest contributing factors to energy loss and uncomfortable spaces.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Individuals might find some ECMs related to windows more cost effective than others such as
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thermal_curtains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thermal curtains (page does not exist)">
       thermal curtains
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Window_insulation_film" title="Window insulation film">
       films
      </a>
      , or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smart_windows" title="Smart windows">
       Smart windows
      </a>
      .
     </p>
