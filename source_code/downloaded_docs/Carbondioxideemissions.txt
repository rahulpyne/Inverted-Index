     <p>
      This is a
      <b>
       list of sovereign states and territories by carbon dioxide emissions
      </b>
      due to certain forms of human activity, based on the EDGAR database created by
      <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
       European Commission
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Environmental_Assessment_Agency" title="Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency">
       Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency
      </a>
      released in 2014. The following table lists the 2014 annual CO
      <sub>
       2
      </sub>
      emissions estimates (in thousands of CO
      <sub>
       2
      </sub>
      tonnes) along with a list of emissions per capita (in tonnes of CO
      <sub>
       2
      </sub>
      per year) from same source. The data only considers carbon dioxide emissions from the burning of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" title="Fossil fuels">
       fossil fuels
      </a>
      and cement manufacture, but not emissions from
      <a href="/wiki/Land_use,_land-use_change_and_forestry" title="Land use, land-use change and forestry">
       land use, land-use change and forestry
      </a>
      . Emissions from international shipping or bunker fuels are also not included in national figures,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schrooten2_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Schrooten2-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      which can make a huge difference for small countries with important ports. The top 10 largest emitter countries account for 68.2% of the world total. Other powerful, more potent
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" title="Greenhouse gases">
       greenhouse gases
      </a>
      are not included in this data, including
      <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">
       methane
      </a>
      .
     </p>
