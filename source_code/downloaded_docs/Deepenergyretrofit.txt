     <p>
      A
      <b>
       deep energy retrofit
      </b>
      is a whole-building analysis and construction process that uses "integrative design" to achieve much larger energy savings than conventional
      <a href="/wiki/Green_retrofit" title="Green retrofit">
       energy retrofits
      </a>
      . Deep energy retrofits can be applied to both residential and non-residential (“commercial”) buildings. A deep energy retrofit typically results in savings of 30 percent or more, perhaps spread over several years, and may significantly improve the building value.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The term "deep energy retrofit" is often used interchangeably with "deep green retrofit" and "deep retrofit". A deep green retrofit may have less focus on energy efficiency and may emphasize obtaining certification from a green building rating system, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">
       LEED
      </a>
      . The definition of the term continues to be refined and debated.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Deep_energy_v._conventional_energy_retrofits">
       Deep energy v. conventional energy retrofits
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Deep energy v. conventional energy retrofits">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Conventional energy retrofits focus on isolated system upgrades (i.e. lighting and HVAC equipment). These retrofits are generally simple and fast, but they often miss opportunity for saving more energy cost-effectively.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Deep energy retrofits achieve much greater energy efficiency by taking a whole-building approach, addressing many systems at once. It is most economical and convenient to take this approach on buildings with overall poor efficiency performance, with multiple systems nearing the end of useful life, and perhaps other reasons.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_efficiency_measures">
       Energy efficiency measures
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Energy efficiency measures">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A deep energy retrofit combines energy efficiency measures such as energy efficient equipment, air sealing, moisture management, controlled ventilation, insulation, and solar control so that dramatic energy savings are achieved alongside optimal building performance.
     </p>
     <p>
      Durability, good interior air quality and energy efficiency are attained by sound building science practices. In a deep energy retrofit, filling a wall cavity with effective insulation also requires careful consideration of how that wall will dry if moisture does happen to get past its skin. Using very high R-value insulation systems on the exterior of the building enclosure is often one of the hallmarks of a deep energy retrofit. Where exactly the dewpoint will fall in (or out) of those thickened walls—and in what climate zone—becomes crucial. Careful detailing, flashing and air sealing of windows and other building penetrations is also key to a successful deep energy retrofit.
     </p>
     <p>
      Systems thinking is required for these kinds of retrofits, where highly efficient windows are "tuned" to their orientation, and mechanical systems and
      <a href="/wiki/Heat_recovery_ventilation" title="Heat recovery ventilation">
       heat recovery ventilation
      </a>
      units are sized and integrated with how the walls, roof and basement are being air sealed, moisture-managed and insulated.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Process">
       Process
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Process">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A Level III
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">
       energy audit
      </a>
      , as defined by
      <a href="/wiki/ASHRAE" title="ASHRAE">
       ASHRAE
      </a>
      , is required in order to complete a commercial building deep energy retrofit. Also known as an
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Investment_grade_audit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Investment grade audit (page does not exist)">
       investment grade audit
      </a>
      , this type of energy audit features analysis of the interactions between efficiency strategies and their life cycle cost.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Upon selection and implementation of measures, the energy savings are verified using the International Performance Measurement and Verification Protocol.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tools">
       Tools
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tools">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Deep energy retrofits make use of energy modeling tools that integrate with an organization's pro forma or other financial decision making mechanisms.
      <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">
       Smartphone
      </a>
      technologies have simplified the retrofit process as a number of audit and retrofit tools have appeared over the last 5 years to speed up retrofits and maximize efficiency in the field.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">
       Ratings
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ratings">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A building that has undergone a deep energy retrofit is well positioned for a green building rating such as
      <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">
       LEED
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">
       Benefits
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Benefits">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      There have been a number of studies done to determine and quantify the benefits afforded to owners, tenants, and various other stakeholders from the successful completion of deep energy retrofits.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Common owner related benefits include:
      </b>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       reduced costs through energy savings
      </li>
      <li>
       higher rent premiums
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       increased occupancy rates
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      <b>
       Common benefits to tenants include:
      </b>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       increased productivity
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       reduced employee sick days
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_case_studies">
       Notable case studies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Empire_State_Building">
       The Empire State Building
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Empire State Building">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">
       Empire State Building
      </a>
      is undergoing a deep energy retrofit process that is projected to be completed in 2013. Upon completion, the project team, consisting of representatives from
      <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Controls" title="Johnson Controls">
       Johnson Controls
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Institute" title="Rocky Mountain Institute">
       Rocky Mountain Institute
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinton_Climate_Initiative" title="Clinton Climate Initiative">
       Clinton Climate Initiative
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jones_Lang_LaSalle" title="Jones Lang LaSalle">
       Jones Lang LaSalle
      </a>
      , and NYSERDA will have achieved an annual energy use reduction of 38% and $4.4 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESB_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ESB-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A notable achievement of the project is that instead of replacing the chillers as originally planned, the design team were able to first reduce the building’s required cooling capacity by 1600 tons, allowing for a chiller retrofit instead of replacement which would have been $17.3 million more in capital costs.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Indianapolis_City-County_Building">
       The Indianapolis City-County Building
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_energy_retrofit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Indianapolis City-County Building">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The City-County Building recently underwent a deep energy retrofit process that is projected to be completed in September 2011. Upon completion, the project team, consisting of representatives from the Indianapolis Marion County Building Authority, Indianapolis Office of Sustainability, Rocky Mountain Institute, and Performance Services will have achieved an annual energy reduction of 46% and $750,000 annual energy savings.
     </p>
