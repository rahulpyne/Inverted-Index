     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">
       Hydrogen
      </a>
      technologies are technologies that relate to the production and use of hydrogen.
      <b>
       Hydrogen technologies
      </b>
      are applicable for many uses.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some hydrogen technologies are
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_neutral" title="Carbon neutral">
       carbon neutral
      </a>
      and could have a role in preventing
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      and a possible future
      <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_economy" title="Hydrogen economy">
       hydrogen economy
      </a>
      . Hydrogen is a widely used chemical used in various applications including
      <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">
       ammonia
      </a>
      production,
      <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">
       oil refining
      </a>
      and energy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Hydrogen is not a
      <a href="/wiki/Primary_energy" title="Primary energy">
       primary energy
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_development" title="Energy development">
       source
      </a>
      , because it is not naturally occurring as a
      <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">
       fuel
      </a>
      . It is, however widely regarded as an ideal
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_storage" title="Energy storage">
       energy storage
      </a>
      medium, due to the ease with which electric power can convert water into its hydrogen and oxygen components through
      <a href="/wiki/Electrolysis" title="Electrolysis">
       electrolysis
      </a>
      and can be converted back to electrical power using a fuel cell. There are a wide number of different types of fuel and electrolysis cells.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The potential environmental impact depends primarily on the methods used to generate the hydrogen fuel.
     </p>
