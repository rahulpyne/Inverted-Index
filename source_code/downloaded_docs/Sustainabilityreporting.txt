     <p>
      A sustainability report is an organizational report that gives information about economic, environmental, social and governance performance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Sustainability reporting is not just report generation from collected data; instead it is a method to internalize and improve an organizationâ€™s commitment to sustainable development in a way that can be demonstrated to both internal and external stakeholders.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_reporting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Corporate
      <b>
       sustainability reporting
      </b>
      has a long history going back to environmental reporting. The first environmental reports were published in the late 1980s by companies in the chemical industry which had serious image problems. The other group of early reporters was a group of committed small and medium-sized businesses with very advanced environmental management systems.
     </p>
     <p>
      Non-financial reporting, such as sustainability and
      <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">
       CSR
      </a>
      reporting, is a fairly recent trend which has expanded over the last twenty years. Many companies now produce an annual sustainability report and there are a wide array of ratings and standards around. There are a variety of reasons that companies choose to produce these reports, but at their core they are intended to be "vessels of transparency and accountability". Often they also intended to improve internal processes, engage stakeholders and persuade investors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Initiatives">
       Initiatives
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_reporting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Initiatives">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Organizations can improve their sustainability performance by measuring (
      <a href="/wiki/EthicalQuote" title="EthicalQuote">
       EthicalQuote (CEQ))
      </a>
      , monitoring and reporting on it, helping them have a positive impact on society, the economy, and a sustainable future. The key drivers for the quality of sustainability reports are the guidelines of the
      <a href="/wiki/Global_Reporting_Initiative" title="Global Reporting Initiative">
       Global Reporting Initiative
      </a>
      (GRI),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      (ACCA) award schemes or rankings. The
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.globalreporting.org/ReportingFramework/ReportingFrameworkDownloads/" rel="nofollow">
       GRI Sustainability Reporting Guidelines
      </a>
      enable all organizations worldwide to assess their sustainability performance and disclose the results in a similar way to financial reporting.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The largest database of corporate sustainability reports can be found on the website of the
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.unglobalcompact.org/participants/search" rel="nofollow">
       United Nations Global Compact
      </a>
      initiative.
     </p>
