   </h1>

    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">
       Sustainability
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      At the global scale and in the broadest sense
      <b>
       sustainability and environmental management
      </b>
      involves managing the
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">
       oceans
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">
       freshwater
      </a>
      systems,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terrestrial_ecoregion" title="Terrestrial ecoregion">
       land
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">
       atmosphere
      </a>
      , according to
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">
       sustainability
      </a>
      principles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">
       Land use
      </a>
      change is fundamental to the operations of the
      <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">
       biosphere
      </a>
      because alterations in the relative proportions of land dedicated to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urbanisation" title="Urbanisation">
       urbanisation
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">
       agriculture
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">
       forest
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">
       woodland
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">
       grassland
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">
       pasture
      </a>
      have a marked effect on the global water, carbon and nitrogen
      <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">
       biogeochemical cycles
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krebs_2001_pp._560.E2.80.93582_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Krebs_2001_pp._560.E2.80.93582-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Management of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">
       Earth's atmosphere
      </a>
      involves assessment of all aspects of the
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_cycle" title="Carbon cycle">
       carbon cycle
      </a>
      to identify opportunities to address human-induced
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      and this has become a major focus of scientific research because of the potential catastrophic effects on biodiversity and human communities.
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">
       Ocean
      </a>
      circulation patterns have a strong influence on
      <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">
       climate
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">
       weather
      </a>
      and, in turn, the food supply of both humans and other organisms.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Atmosphere">
       Atmosphere
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Atmosphere">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">
       Earth's atmosphere
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       Climate change
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In March 2009 at a meeting of the
      <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Climate_Council" title="Copenhagen Climate Council">
       Copenhagen Climate Council
      </a>
      2,500 climate experts from 80 countries issued a keynote statement that there is now "no excuse" for failing to act on global warming and that without strong carbon reduction targets "abrupt or irreversible" shifts in climate may occur that "will be very difficult for contemporary societies to cope with".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Management of the global atmosphere now involves assessment of all aspects of the
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_cycle" title="Carbon cycle">
       carbon cycle
      </a>
      to identify opportunities to address human-induced
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      and this has become a major focus of scientific research because of the potential catastrophic effects on biodiversity and human communities.
     </p>
     <p>
      Other human impacts on the atmosphere include the
      <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">
       air pollution
      </a>
      in cities, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pollutants" title="Pollutants">
       pollutants
      </a>
      including toxic chemicals like
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nitrogen_oxides" title="Nitrogen oxides">
       nitrogen oxides
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_oxide" title="Sulfur oxide">
       sulphur oxides
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" title="Volatile organic compounds">
       volatile organic compounds
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_particulate_matter" title="Atmospheric particulate matter">
       airborne particulate matter
      </a>
      that produce
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photochemical_smog" title="Photochemical smog">
       photochemical smog
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Acid_rain" title="Acid rain">
       acid rain
      </a>
      , and the
      <a href="/wiki/Chlorofluorocarbon" title="Chlorofluorocarbon">
       chlorofluorocarbons
      </a>
      that degrade the
      <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">
       ozone layer
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">
       Anthropogenic
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_particulate_matter" title="Atmospheric particulate matter">
       particulates
      </a>
      such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sulfate_aerosols" title="Sulfate aerosols">
       sulfate aerosols
      </a>
      in the atmosphere reduce the direct
      <a href="/wiki/Irradiance" title="Irradiance">
       irradiance
      </a>
      and reflectance (
      <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
       albedo
      </a>
      ) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
       Earth
      </a>
      's surface. Known as
      <a href="/wiki/Global_dimming" title="Global dimming">
       global dimming
      </a>
      the decrease is estimated to have been about 4% between 1960 and 1990 although the trend has subsequently reversed. Global dimming may have disturbed the global
      <a href="/wiki/Water_cycle" title="Water cycle">
       water cycle
      </a>
      by reducing evaporation and rainfall in some areas. It also creates a
      <a href="/wiki/Thermal_energy" title="Thermal energy">
       cooling
      </a>
      effect and this may have partially masked the effect of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" title="Greenhouse gases">
       greenhouse gases
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">
       global warming
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Oceans">
       Oceans
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Oceans">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Coastal_management" title="Coastal management">
       Coastal management
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Overfishing" title="Overfishing">
       Overfishing
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg">
        <img alt="Display of about ten well-known saltwater fish" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2384" data-file-width="2960" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg/220px-Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg/330px-Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg/440px-Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Salt_water_fish_with_Finnish_text.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A selection of the world's saltwater fish
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">
       Ocean
      </a>
      circulation patterns have a strong influence on
      <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">
       climate
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">
       weather
      </a>
      and, in turn, the food supply of both humans and other organisms. Scientists have warned of the possibility, under the influence of climate change, of a sudden alteration in circulation patterns of
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean_current" title="Ocean current">
       ocean currents
      </a>
      that could drastically alter the climate in some regions of the globe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Major human environmental impacts occur in the more habitable regions of the ocean fringes – the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estuaries" title="Estuaries">
       estuaries
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastline" title="Coastline">
       coastline
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bay" title="Bay">
       bays
      </a>
      . Ten per cent of the world's population – about 600 million people – live in low-lying areas vulnerable to sea level rise. Trends of concern that require management include:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Over-fishing" title="Over-fishing">
       over-fishing
      </a>
      (beyond sustainable levels);
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Coral_bleaching" title="Coral bleaching">
       coral bleaching
      </a>
      due to ocean warming and
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean_acidification" title="Ocean acidification">
       ocean acidification
      </a>
      due to increasing levels of dissolved carbon dioxide;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Current_sea_level_rise" title="Current sea level rise">
       sea level rise
      </a>
      due to climate change. Because of their vastness oceans also act as a convenient
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_pollution" title="Marine pollution">
       dumping ground
      </a>
      for human waste.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Remedial strategies include: more careful waste management, statutory control of overfishing by adoption of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_fishing" title="Sustainable fishing">
       sustainable fishing
      </a>
      practices and the use of environmentally sensitive and sustainable
      <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">
       aquaculture
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fish_farming" title="Fish farming">
       fish farming
      </a>
      , reduction of fossil fuel emissions and restoration of coastal and other marine habitat.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Freshwater">
       Freshwater
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Freshwater">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_crisis" title="Water crisis">
       Water crisis
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Water covers 71% of the Earth's surface. Of this, 97.5% is the salty water of the
      <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">
       oceans
      </a>
      and only 2.5% freshwater, most of which is locked up in the
      <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_ice_sheet" title="Antarctic ice sheet">
       Antarctic ice sheet
      </a>
      . The remaining freshwater is found in lakes, rivers, wetlands, the soil, aquifers and atmosphere. All life depends on the solar-powered global water cycle, the evaporation from oceans and land to form water vapour that later condenses from clouds as rain, which then becomes the renewable part of the freshwater supply.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atlas_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Atlas-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Awareness of the global importance of preserving
      <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">
       water
      </a>
      for
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">
       ecosystem services
      </a>
      has only recently emerged as, during the 20th century, more than half the world’s
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wetlands" title="Wetlands">
       wetlands
      </a>
      have been lost along with their valuable environmental services.
      <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">
       Biodiversity
      </a>
      -rich
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">
       freshwater
      </a>
      ecosystems are currently declining faster than
      <a href="/wiki/Marine_(ocean)" title="Marine (ocean)">
       marine
      </a>
      or land
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">
       ecosystems
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-water_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-water-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      making them the world's most vulnerable habitats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LPR_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LPR-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Increasing
      <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">
       urbanization
      </a>
      pollutes clean water supplies and much of the world still does not have access to clean, safe
      <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">
       water
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atlas_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Atlas-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In the industrial world
      <a href="/wiki/Demand_management" title="Demand management">
       demand management
      </a>
      has slowed absolute usage rates but increasingly water is being transported over vast distances from water-rich natural areas to population-dense urban areas and energy-hungry
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_desalination" title="Water desalination">
       desalination
      </a>
      is becoming more widely used. Greater emphasis is now being placed on the improved management of blue (harvestable) and green (soil water available for plant use) water, and this applies at all scales of water management.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-water_13-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-water-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Land">
       Land
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Land">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">
       Land use
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Loss of biodiversity stems largely from the habitat loss and fragmentation produced by the human appropriation of land for development, forestry and agriculture as
      <a href="/wiki/Natural_capital" title="Natural capital">
       natural capital
      </a>
      is progressively converted to man-made capital. Land use change is fundamental to the operations of the
      <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">
       biosphere
      </a>
      because alterations in the relative proportions of land dedicated to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urbanisation" title="Urbanisation">
       urbanisation
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">
       agriculture
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">
       forest
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">
       woodland
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">
       grassland
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pasture" title="Pasture">
       pasture
      </a>
      have a marked effect on the global water, carbon and nitrogen
      <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">
       biogeochemical cycles
      </a>
      and this can impact negatively on both natural and human systems.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krebs_2001_pp._560.E2.80.93582_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Krebs_2001_pp._560.E2.80.93582-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the local human scale major sustainability benefits accrue from the pursuit of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_cities" title="Green cities">
       green cities
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_gardening" title="Sustainable gardening">
       sustainable parks and gardens
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Forests">
       Forests
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Forests">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">
       Forestry
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ecoforestry" title="Ecoforestry">
       Ecoforestry
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grib_skov.jpg">
        <img alt="Beech forest – Grib Skov, Denmark" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1700" data-file-width="1810" height="207" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Grib_skov.jpg/220px-Grib_skov.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Grib_skov.jpg/330px-Grib_skov.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Grib_skov.jpg/440px-Grib_skov.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grib_skov.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Beech forest – Grib Skov, Denmark
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Since the Neolithic Revolution, human use has reduced the world’s forest cover by about 47%. Present-day forests occupy about a quarter of the world’s ice-free land with about half of these occurring in the tropics
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In temperate and boreal regions forest area is gradually increasing (with the exception of Siberia), but
      <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">
       deforestation
      </a>
      in the tropics is of major concern.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Forests moderate the local climate and the global water cycle through their light reflectance (
      <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
       albedo
      </a>
      ) and
      <a href="/wiki/Evapotranspiration" title="Evapotranspiration">
       evapotranspiration
      </a>
      . They also conserve
      <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">
       biodiversity
      </a>
      , protect water quality, preserve soil and soil quality, provide fuel and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceuticals" title="Pharmaceuticals">
       pharmaceuticals
      </a>
      , and purify the air. These free
      <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">
       ecosystem services
      </a>
      are not given a market value under most current economic systems, and so forest conservation has little appeal when compared with the economic benefits of logging and clearance which, through soil degradation and organic decomposition returns carbon dioxide to the atmosphere.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAO1_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FAO1-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The United Nations
      <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">
       Food and Agriculture Organization
      </a>
      (FAO) estimates that about 90% of the carbon stored in land vegetation is locked up in trees and that they sequester about 50% more carbon than is present in the atmosphere. Changes in land use currently contribute about 20% of total global carbon emissions (heavily logged Indonesia and Brazil are a major source of emissions).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAO1_19-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-FAO1-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       Climate change
      </a>
      can be mitigated by sequestering carbon in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reafforestation" title="Reafforestation">
       reafforestation
      </a>
      schemes, plantations and timber products. Also wood biomass can be utilized as a renewable carbon-neutral fuel. The FAO has suggested that, over the period 2005–2050, effective use of tree planting could absorb about 10–20% of man-made emissions – so monitoring the condition of the world's forests must be part of a global strategy to mitigate emissions and protect ecosystem services.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, climate change may pre-empt this FAO scenario as a study by the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Forest_Research_Organizations" title="International Union of Forest Research Organizations">
       International Union of Forest Research Organizations
      </a>
      in 2009 concluded that the stress of a 2.5C (4.5F) temperature rise above pre-industrial levels could result in the release of vast amounts of carbon
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      so the potential of forests to act as carbon "sinks" is "at risk of being lost entirely".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultivated_land">
       Cultivated land
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cultivated land">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_agriculture" title="Sustainable agriculture">
       Sustainable agriculture
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rice_Field.jpg">
        <img alt="A farmer working in a rice paddy" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="279" data-file-width="315" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Rice_Field.jpg/220px-Rice_Field.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bd/Rice_Field.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rice_Field.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        A rice paddy. Rice, wheat, corn and potatoes make up more than half the world's food supply
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Feeding more than six billion human bodies takes a heavy toll on the Earth’s resources. This begins with the appropriation of about 38% of the Earth’s land surface
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and about 20% of its net primary productivity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Added to this are the resource-hungry activities of industrial agribusiness – everything from the crop need for irrigation water, synthetic
      <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">
       fertilizers
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pesticide" title="Pesticide">
       pesticides
      </a>
      to the resource costs of food packaging,
      <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">
       transport
      </a>
      (now a major part of global trade) and retail.
      <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">
       Food
      </a>
      is essential to life. But the list of environmental costs of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_production" title="Food production">
       food production
      </a>
      is a long one: topsoil depletion,
      <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">
       erosion
      </a>
      and conversion to desert from constant tillage of annual crops; overgrazing;
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salinization" title="Salinization">
       salinization
      </a>
      ;
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sodification" title="Sodification">
       sodification
      </a>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Waterlogging_(agriculture)" title="Waterlogging (agriculture)">
       waterlogging
      </a>
      ; high levels of
      <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">
       fossil fuel
      </a>
      use; reliance on inorganic fertilisers and synthetic organic
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pesticides" title="Pesticides">
       pesticides
      </a>
      ; reductions in
      <a href="/wiki/Genetic_diversity" title="Genetic diversity">
       genetic diversity
      </a>
      by the mass use of
      <a href="/wiki/Monoculture" title="Monoculture">
       monocultures
      </a>
      ; water resource depletion;
      <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">
       pollution
      </a>
      of waterbodies by run-off and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Groundwater_contamination" title="Groundwater contamination">
       groundwater contamination
      </a>
      ; social problems including the decline of family farms and weakening of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rural_communities" title="Rural communities">
       rural communities
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      All of these environmental problems associated with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_agriculture" title="Industrial agriculture">
       industrial agriculture
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Agribusiness" title="Agribusiness">
       agribusiness
      </a>
      are now being addressed through such movements as
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_agriculture" title="Sustainable agriculture">
       sustainable agriculture
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Organic_farming" title="Organic farming">
       organic farming
      </a>
      and more sustainable business practices.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Extinctions">
       Extinctions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Extinctions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">
       Extinction
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ExtinctDodoBird.jpeg">
        <img alt="Picture of the now extinct bird the dodo" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="244" data-file-width="225" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/ExtinctDodoBird.jpeg/150px-ExtinctDodoBird.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/ExtinctDodoBird.jpeg 1.5x" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ExtinctDodoBird.jpeg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The extinct
        <a href="/wiki/Dodo" title="Dodo">
         dodo
        </a>
        (
        <i>
         Raphus cucullatus
        </i>
        )
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Although
      <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">
       biodiversity
      </a>
      loss can be monitored simply as loss of species, effective conservation demands the protection of species within their natural habitats and ecosystems. Following human migration and population growth, species
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extinctions" title="Extinctions">
       extinctions
      </a>
      have progressively increased to a rate unprecedented since the
      <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous%E2%80%93Paleogene_extinction_event" title="Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event">
       Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event
      </a>
      . Known as the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocene_extinction_event" title="Holocene extinction event">
       Holocene extinction event
      </a>
      this current human-induced extinction of species ranks as one of the worlds six mass
      <a href="/wiki/Extinction_event" title="Extinction event">
       extinction events
      </a>
      . Some scientific estimates indicate that up to half of presently existing species may become extinct by 2100.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Current extinction rates are 100 to 1000 times their prehuman levels with more than 10% birds and mammals threatened, about 8% of plants, 5% of fish and more than 20% of freshwater species.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        [29]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The 2008
      <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">
       IUCN Red List
      </a>
      warns that long-term droughts and extreme weather put additional stress on key habitats and, for example, lists 1,226 bird species as threatened with extinction, which is one-in-eight of all bird species.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        [30]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        [31]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/Red_List_Index" title="Red List Index">
       Red List Index
      </a>
      also identifies 44 tree species in Central Asia as under threat of extinction due to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Over-exploitation" title="Over-exploitation">
       over-exploitation
      </a>
      and human development and threatening the region's forests which are home to more than 300 wild ancestors of modern domesticated fruit and nut cultivars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        [32]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biological_invasions">
       Biological invasions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Biological invasions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">
       Introduced species
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta,_Georgia.jpg">
        <img alt="Trees infested with Kudzu (Pueraria lobata)" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1242" data-file-width="2016" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg/220px-Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg/330px-Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg/440px-Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta%2C_Georgia.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kudzu_on_trees_in_Atlanta,_Georgia.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Kudzu" title="Kudzu">
         Kudzu
        </a>
        (
        <i>
         Pueraria lobata
        </i>
        ) infesting trees in
        <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">
         Atlanta
        </a>
        , Georgia, USA
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In many parts of the industrial world land clearing for agriculture has diminished and here the greatest threat to biodiversity, after
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      , has become the destructive effect of
      <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">
       invasive species
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        [33]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Increasingly efficient global transport has facilitated the spread of
      <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">
       organisms
      </a>
      across the planet. The potential danger of this aspect of
      <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">
       globalization
      </a>
      is starkly illustrated through the spread of human diseases like
      <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">
       HIV
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">
       AIDS
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mad_cow_disease" title="Mad cow disease">
       mad cow disease
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Avian_influenza" title="Avian influenza">
       bird flu
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swine_flu" title="Swine flu">
       swine flu
      </a>
      , but invasive plants and animals are also having a devastating impact on native
      <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">
       biodiversity
      </a>
      . Non-indigenous organisms can quickly occupy disturbed land and natural areas where, in the absence of their natural
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Predator" title="Predator">
       predators
      </a>
      , they are able to thrive.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        [34]
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the global scale this issue is being addressed through the
      <a href="/wiki/Global_Invasive_Species_Information_Network" title="Global Invasive Species Information Network">
       Global Invasive Species Information Network
      </a>
      but there is improved international
      <a href="/wiki/Biosecurity" title="Biosecurity">
       biosecurity
      </a>
      legislation to minimise the transmission of pathogens and invasive organisms. Also, through
      <a href="/wiki/CITES" title="CITES">
       CITES
      </a>
      legislation there is control the trade in rare and threatened species. Increasingly at the local level public awareness programs are alerting communities, gardeners, the nursery industry, collectors, and the pet and aquarium industries, to the harmful effects of potentially invasive species.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Resistance_to_change">
       Resistance to change
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_and_environmental_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Resistance to change">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_and_systemic_change_resistance" title="Sustainability and systemic change resistance">
       Sustainability and systemic change resistance
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The environmental sustainability problem has proven difficult to solve. The modern
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">
       environmental movement
      </a>
      has attempted to solve the problem in a large variety of ways. But little progress has been made, as shown by severe
      <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">
       ecological footprint
      </a>
      overshoot and lack of sufficient progress on the
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      problem. Something within the human system in preventing change to a sustainable mode of behavior. That system trait is systemic change resistance. Change resistance is also known as organizational resistance, barriers to change, or policy resistance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        [36]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
