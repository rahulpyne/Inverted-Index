     <p>
      <b>
       Eco commerce
      </b>
      is a business, investment, and technology-development model that employs
      <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">
       market-based
      </a>
      solutions to balancing the world’s energy needs and environmental integrity. Through the use of
      <a href="/wiki/Green_trading" title="Green trading">
       green trading
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Green_finance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Green finance (page does not exist)">
       green finance
      </a>
      , eco-commerce promotes the further development of "
      <a href="/wiki/Clean_technology" title="Clean technology">
       clean technologies
      </a>
      " such as
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">
       wind power
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">
       solar power
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">
       biomass
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">
       hydropower
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      EcoCommerce is an integrated ecological-economical model that provides a means to account for and value land management activities that improves the condition of natural capital and values the output of ecoservices. EcoCommerce is more comprehensive than a compilation or organization of ecosystems service markets as it provides the framework to build an ecological intelligence system that allows the public arena of commerce to define sustainability.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
