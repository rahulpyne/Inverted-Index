     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Mercedes-Benz Bionic
      </b>
      was a
      <a href="/wiki/Concept_car" title="Concept car">
       concept car
      </a>
      created by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler_AG" title="DaimlerChrysler AG">
       DaimlerChrysler AG
      </a>
      under the
      <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">
       Mercedes
      </a>
      Group. It was first introduced in 2005 at the DaimlerChrysler Innovation Symposium in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_D._C." title="Washington, D. C.">
       Washington, D. C.
      </a>
      The Bionic was modelled after a type of
      <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">
       fish
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Yellow_boxfish" title="Yellow boxfish">
       yellow boxfish
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       Ostracion cubicus
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and also has 80% lower
      <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">
       nitrogen
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen_oxide" title="Nitrogen oxide">
       oxide
      </a>
      emissions with its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Selective_Catalytic_Reduction" title="Selective Catalytic Reduction">
       Selective Catalytic Reduction
      </a>
      technology.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Engine_and_Performance">
       Engine and Performance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mercedes-Benz_Bionic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Engine and Performance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Bionic is powered by a
      <i>
       103 kW direct-injection
       <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">
        diesel engine
       </a>
      </i>
      that gives out around 70
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miles_per_gallon" title="Miles per gallon">
       MPG (US)
      </a>
      (~3.36 L/100 km). This engine also gives out around a total of 140 horsepower (100 kW) and a little over 221 ft·lbf (300 N·m) of
      <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">
       torque
      </a>
      at around 1600
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rpm" title="Rpm">
       rpm
      </a>
      . The Bionic can go from 0-60 in about 8 seconds and has a top speed of a little over 190 km/h (118 mph) .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">
       Design
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mercedes-Benz_Bionic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The exterior design was modelled after the yellow boxfish (
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ostracion_cubicus" title="Ostracion cubicus">
        Ostracion cubicus
       </a>
      </i>
      ), a marine fish that lives in coral reefs. Mercedes-Benz decided to model the Bionic after this fish due to the supposed low
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coefficient_of_drag" title="Coefficient of drag">
       coefficient of drag
      </a>
      of its body shape and the rigidity of its
      <a href="/wiki/Exoskeleton" title="Exoskeleton">
       exoskeleton
      </a>
      ; this influenced the car's unusual looks. Other parts of the design include the fact that the rear wheels are partially fitted with plastic and that it's considered as a lightweight vehicle. Mercedes-Benz reported a
      <a href="/wiki/Drag_coefficient" title="Drag coefficient">
       drag coefficient
      </a>
      of 0.19;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      for comparison, the production vehicle with the lowest ever C
      <sub>
       d
      </sub>
      value was the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GM_EV1" title="GM EV1">
       GM EV1
      </a>
      , at 0.195. While the Bionic had a much larger internal volume than the EV1, the Bionic's larger frontal area made the EV1 more aerodynamic overall, as drag is a product of the area and the drag coefficient.
     </p>
