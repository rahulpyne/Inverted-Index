     <p>
      <b>
       Silesian University of Technology
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">
       Polish
      </a>
      name:
      <b>
       Politechnika Śląska
      </b>
      IPA: [pɔliˈtɛxɲika ˈɕlɔ̃ska]) is a
      <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">
       university
      </a>
      located in
      <a href="/wiki/Gliwice" title="Gliwice">
       Gliwice
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Silesia" title="Silesia">
       Silesia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      . It was founded in 1945 by Polish professors of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lviv_University_of_Technology" title="Lviv University of Technology">
       Lwow Polytechnic
      </a>
      , who were forced to leave their native city and move to the
      <a href="/wiki/Recovered_Territories" title="Recovered Territories">
       Recovered Territories
      </a>
      (see also
      <a href="/wiki/Kresy" title="Kresy">
       Kresy
      </a>
      ). The University consistently ranks among the top most prestigious technical universities in Poland.
     </p>
     <p>
      Silesian University of Technology is divided into 13 faculties, 2 colleges, 1 Institute and 2 Science and Education Centres:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">
        Architecture
       </a>
       ,
      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a href="/wiki/Automatic_control" title="Automatic control">
        Automatic control
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">
        Electronics
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_Science" title="Computer Science">
        Computer Science
       </a>
       ,
      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering">

      </li>
      <li>

       ,
      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_Engineering" title="Electrical Engineering">
        Electrical Engineering
       </a>
       ,
      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">

       ,
      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biomedical_Engineering" title="Biomedical Engineering">

      </li>
      <li>

       <a href="/wiki/Materials_science" title="Materials science">

       <a href="/wiki/Metallurgy" title="Metallurgy">

      </li>
      <li>
       Faculty of
       <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">
        Energy

       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Engineering" title="Environmental Engineering">
        Environmental Engineering

      </li>
      <li>

       <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">

     <p>
      Twelve units (faculties, colleges and institute) are situated in
      <a href="/wiki/Gliwice" title="Gliwice">
       Gliwice
      </a>
      , two in
      <a href="/wiki/Katowice" title="Katowice">
       Katowice
      </a>
      and two in
      <a href="/wiki/Zabrze" title="Zabrze">
       Zabrze
      </a>
      . Centres of science and education are located in
      <a href="/wiki/Rybnik" title="Rybnik">
       Rybnik
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sosnowiec" title="Sosnowiec">
       Sosnowiec
      </a>
      .
     </p>
