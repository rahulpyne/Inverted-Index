     <p>
      <b>
       Piracicaba
      </b>
      (
      <small>
       Portuguese pronunciation:
      </small>
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">
        [piɾɐsiˈkabɐ]
       </a>
      </span>
      or
      <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
       <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">
        [piˌɾasiˈkabɐ]
       </a>
      </span>
      ) is a city located in the
      <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
       Brazilian
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_state" title="Brazilian state">
       state
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">
       São Paulo
      </a>
      . The population is 391,449 (2015 est.) in an area of 1378.07 km².
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibge_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ibge-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is at an elevation of 547 m above sea level.
     </p>

     <p>
      The place name comes from a word in the
      <a href="/wiki/Tupi_language" title="Tupi language">
       Tupi language
      </a>
      that means "place where the fish stops".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-:0-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The name arises from the fact that the waterfalls of the
      <a href="/wiki/Piracicaba_River" title="Piracicaba River">
       Piracicaba River
      </a>
      , which bisects the city, is a point at which "piracema"—fish swimming upstream to reproduce.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Larger fish species such as the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zungaro_zungaro" title="Zungaro zungaro">
       dourados
      </a>
      can still be observed.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [

     <p>
      In 1766, Antonio Correa Barbosa, charged with the task of establishing a settlement on the estuary of Piracicaba river, opted for a location about 90 km from it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The settlement was officially founded on August 1, 1767, as a
      <i>
       povoação
      </i>
      subordinated to the
      <i>
       vila of
      </i>
      <a href="/wiki/Itu,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Itu, São Paulo">
       Itu
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-:0-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1784, Piracicaba gets emancipated from Itu, becoming a
      <i>
       freguesia.
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-:0-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1821, the freguesia is promoted to
      <i>
       vila
      </i>
      , known as Vila Nova da Constituição. In 1856, Vila Nova da Constituição is promoted to city status and in 1877 it is officially named "Piracicaba", following a law by the then councilman and future Brazilian president
      <a href="/wiki/Prudente_de_Morais" title="Prudente de Morais">
       Prudente de Morais
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1944, the city was made the seat of the
      <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Piracicaba" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Piracicaba">
       Roman Catholic Diocese of Piracicaba
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      Piracicaba is home to the oldest agricultural university in Brazil, the
      <a href="/wiki/Escola_Superior_de_Agricultura_Luiz_de_Queiroz" title="Escola Superior de Agricultura Luiz de Queiroz">
       Luiz de Queiroz School of Agriculture
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="University of São Paulo">
       University of São Paulo
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Founded in 1901, the school is located on a farm with a large collection of trees and plants.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      It is also recognized as a leader in the field of
      <a href="/wiki/Precision_agriculture" title="Precision agriculture">
       precision agriculture
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Other universities based in Piracicaba are the
      <a href="/wiki/Methodist_University_of_Piracicaba" title="Methodist University of Piracicaba">
       Methodist University of Piracicaba
      </a>
      (UNIMEP)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Campinas" title="University of Campinas">
       University of Campinas
      </a>
      ' School of Dentistry.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Piracicaba is known by the nickname "Noiva da Colina" (bride of the hills),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and is the annual host to the "Festa das Nações" (Nations' Party, where people may find foods from all over the world),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and to the "Salão Internacional do Humor" (International Humor Exhibition), where cartoonists from around the world display comic drawings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
