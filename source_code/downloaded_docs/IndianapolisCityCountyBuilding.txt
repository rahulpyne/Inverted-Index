     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Indianapolis City-County Building
      </b>
      is a 28-story building at 200 E.
      <a href="/wiki/Washington_Street_(Indianapolis)" title="Washington Street (Indianapolis)">
       Washington Street
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Indianapolis" title="Downtown Indianapolis">
       downtown
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">
       Indianapolis, Indiana
      </a>
      that houses the offices of the
      <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_city-county" title="Consolidated city-county">
       consolidated city-county
      </a>
      government of Indianapolis and
      <a href="/wiki/Marion_County,_Indiana" title="Marion County, Indiana">
       Marion County, Indiana
      </a>
      , known as
      <a href="/wiki/Unigov" title="Unigov">
       Unigov
      </a>
      , in its 665,534 square feet
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibj_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ibj-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      of office space. It also houses the Marion County Courts and the headquarters of the
      <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Metropolitan_Police_Department" title="Indianapolis Metropolitan Police Department">
       Indianapolis Metropolitan Police Department
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_City-County_Council" title="Indianapolis City-County Council">
       Indianapolis City-County Council
      </a>
      . The office of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Indianapolis" title="Mayor of Indianapolis">
       Mayor of Indianapolis
      </a>
      is on the top floor of the Indianapolis City-County Building.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The building opened in 1962 after two years of construction,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emporis_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-emporis-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      at a cost of $22 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indy_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Indy-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The City-County Building was the first building in the city to be taller than the
      <a href="/wiki/Soldiers%27_and_Sailors%27_Monument_(Indianapolis)" title="Soldiers' and Sailors' Monument (Indianapolis)">
       Soldiers' and Sailors' Monument
      </a>
      , and was the tallest building in the city until 1970.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emporis_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-emporis-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
