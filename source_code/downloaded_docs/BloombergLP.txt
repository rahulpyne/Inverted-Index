     <p>
      <b>
       Bloomberg L.P.
      </b>
      is a privately held financial software, data, and media company headquartered in
      <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">
       Midtown Manhattan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      . Bloomberg L.P. was founded by
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bloomberg" title="Michael Bloomberg">
       Michael Bloomberg
      </a>
      in 1981 with the help of
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Secunda" title="Thomas Secunda">
       Thomas Secunda
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duncan_MacMillan_(Bloomberg)" title="Duncan MacMillan (Bloomberg)">
       Duncan MacMillan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Charles_Zegar" title="Charles Zegar">
       Charles Zegar
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and a 30% ownership investment by
      <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" title="Merrill Lynch">
       Merrill Lynch
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bloomberg L.P. provides financial software tools such as an analytics and equity trading platform, data services, and news to financial companies and organizations through the
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Terminal" title="Bloomberg Terminal">
       Bloomberg Terminal
      </a>
      (via its Bloomberg Professional Service), its core
      <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">
       revenue
      </a>
      -generating product.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bloomberg L.P. also includes a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wire_service" title="Wire service">
       wire service
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">
       Bloomberg News
      </a>
      ), a global television network (
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Television" title="Bloomberg Television">
       Bloomberg Television
      </a>
      ),
      <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">
       digital websites
      </a>
      , a radio station (
      <a href="/wiki/WBBR" title="WBBR">
       WBBR
      </a>
      ), subscription-only newsletters, and three magazines:
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">
        Bloomberg Businessweek
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Markets" title="Bloomberg Markets">
        Bloomberg Markets
       </a>
      </i>
      , and
      <i>
       Bloomberg Pursuits
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2014, Bloomberg L.P. launched Bloomberg Politics, a multiplatform media property that merged the company's political news teams, and has recruited two veteran political journalists,
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_Halperin" title="Mark Halperin">
       Mark Halperin
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/John_Heilemann" title="John Heilemann">
       John Heilemann
      </a>
      , to run it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1981,
      <a href="/wiki/Salomon_Brothers" title="Salomon Brothers">
       Salomon Brothers
      </a>
      was acquired, and
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bloomberg" title="Michael Bloomberg">
       Michael Bloomberg
      </a>
      , a general partner, was given a $10 million partnership settlement.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-auto-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bloomberg, having designed in-house computerized financial systems for Salomon, used his $10 million severance check to start Innovative Market Systems (IMS).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Make_It_New_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Make_It_New-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bloomberg developed and built his own computerized system to provide real-time market data, financial calculations and other financial analytics to
      <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street" title="Wall Street">
       Wall Street
      </a>
      firms. In 1983, Merrill Lynch invested $30 million in IMS to help finance the development of "the Bloomberg" terminal computer system and by 1984, IMS was selling machines to all of Merrill Lynch's clients.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Make_It_New_13-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Make_It_New-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1986, the company was renamed Bloomberg L.P., and 5,000 terminals had been installed in subscribers' offices.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_L.P._.E2.80.93_Swot_Analysis_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_L.P._.E2.80.93_Swot_Analysis-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Within a few years, ancillary products including
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Tradebook" title="Bloomberg Tradebook">
       Bloomberg Tradebook
      </a>
      (a trading platform), the Bloomberg Messaging Service, and the Bloomberg newswire were launched. Bloomberg launched its news services division in 1990. Bloomberg.com was first established on September 29, 1993 as a financial portal with information on markets, currency conversion, news and events, and Bloomberg Terminal subscriptions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-National_Arbitration_Forum_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-National_Arbitration_Forum-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In late 1996, Bloomberg bought back one-third of Merrill Lynch's 30 percent stake in the company for $200 million, valuing the company at $2 billion. In 2008, facing losses during the financial crisis, Merrill Lynch agreed to sell its remaining 20 percent stake in the company back to Bloomberg Inc.,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      majority-owned by Michael Bloomberg,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      for a reported $4.43 billion, valuing Bloomberg L.P. at approximately $22.5 billion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_New_York_Times_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_New_York_Times-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters.com_19-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-reuters.com-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bloomberg L.P. has remained a private company since its founding; the majority of which is owned by Michael Bloomberg.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_New_York_Times_18-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_New_York_Times-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
      To run for the position of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_New_York" title="Mayor of New York">
       Mayor of New York
      </a>
      against
      <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">
       Democrat
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Mark_J._Green" title="Mark J. Green">
       Mark Green
      </a>
      in 2001, Bloomberg gave up his position of CEO and appointed Lex Fenwick as CEO in his stead.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leadership_.E2.80.93_Lex_Fenwick_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Leadership_.E2.80.93_Lex_Fenwick-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Grauer" title="Peter Grauer">
       Peter Grauer
      </a>
      is the chairman.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_21-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2008, Fenwick became the CEO of Bloomberg Ventures, a new venture capital division.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Doctoroff" title="Daniel Doctoroff">
       Daniel Doctoroff
      </a>
      , former deputy mayor in the Bloomberg administration, now serves as president and CEO.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mediadecoder.blogs.nytimes.com_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mediadecoder.blogs.nytimes.com-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In September 2014 it was announced that Michael Bloomberg would be taking the reins of his eponymous market data company from Doctoroff, who was chief executive of Bloomberg for the past six years after his term as deputy mayor.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Financial_Times_23-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Financial_Times-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2014, Bloomberg sold its Bloomberg Sports analysis division to the data analysis firm
      <a href="/wiki/STATS_LLC" title="STATS LLC">
       STATS LLC
      </a>
      for a fee rumored to be between $15 million and $20 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">
       Acquisitions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Since its founding, Bloomberg L.P. has made several acquisitions including the radio station
      <a href="/wiki/WBBR" title="WBBR">
       WNEW
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" title="BusinessWeek">
        BusinessWeek
       </a>

      magazine, research company New Energy Finance, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bureau_of_National_Affairs" title="Bureau of National Affairs">
       Bureau of National Affairs
      </a>
      and the financial software company Bloomberg PolarLake. On July 9, 2014, Bloomberg L.P. acquired RTS Realtime Systems, a global provider of low-latency connectivity and trading support services.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="WNEW">
       WNEW
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: WNEW">
        edit

      <a href="/wiki/WNEW-FM" title="WNEW-FM">
       WNEW
      </a>
      for $13.5 million. The station was converted into an all-news format, known as
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Radio" title="Bloomberg Radio">
       Bloomberg Radio
      </a>
      , and the call letters were changed to
      <a href="/wiki/WBBR" title="WBBR">
       WBBR
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="BusinessWeek">
       BusinessWeek
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: BusinessWeek">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Bloomberg L.P. bought weekly business magazine,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">
        BusinessWeek
       </a>
      </i>
      , from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McGraw-Hill" title="McGraw-Hill">
       McGraw-Hill
      </a>
      in 2009. The company acquired the magazine - which was suffering from declining advertising revenue and limited circulation numbers - to attract general business to its media audience composed primarily of terminal subscribers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Following the acquisition,
      <i>
       BusinessWeek
      </i>
      was renamed
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">
        Bloomberg Businessweek
       </a>
      </i>
      . Ellen Pollok edits the magazine.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Eagle_Eye_Publishing">
       Eagle Eye Publishing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        edit
       </a>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2010, Bloomberg L.P acquired Eagle Eye Publishing, a Fairfax, VA based company that publishes data about procurement by the Federal Government. This acquisition became part of
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Government" title="Bloomberg Government">
       Bloomberg Government
      </a>
      which was launched in early 2011.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="New_Energy_Finance">
       New Energy Finance
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: New Energy Finance">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2009, Bloomberg L.P. purchased New Energy Finance, a data company focused on energy investment and carbon markets research based in the United Kingdom.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
      New Energy Finance was created by
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Liebreich" title="Michael Liebreich">
       Michael Liebreich
      </a>
      in 2004 to provide news, data and analysis on carbon and clean energy markets. Bloomberg L.P. acquired the company to become an industry resource for information to support low-carbon energy solutions. Liebreich continued to lead the company, serving as the chief executive officer
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        [29]
       </a>
      </sup>
      until 2014, when he stepped down as CEO but remained involved as Chairman of the Advisory Board.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        [30]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bureau_of_National_Affairs_.28BNA.29">

      <span class="mw-editsection">

      </a>
      in August 2011 for $990 million to bolster its existing
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Government" title="Bloomberg Government">
       Bloomberg Government
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Law" title="Bloomberg Law">
       Bloomberg Law
      </a>
      services.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        [31]
       </a>
      </sup>
      BNA publishes specialized online and print news and information for professionals in business and government. The company produces more than 350 news publications in topic areas that include corporate law and business, employee benefits, employment and labor law, environment, health and safety, health care, human resources, intellectual property, litigation, and tax and accounting.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsweek_32-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Newsweek-32">
        [32]
       </a>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="EEOC_v._Bloomberg_L.P.">
       EEOC v. Bloomberg L.P.
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: EEOC v. Bloomberg L.P.">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In September 2007, the
      <a href="/wiki/Equal_Employment_Opportunity_Commission" title="Equal Employment Opportunity Commission">
       Equal Employment Opportunity Commission
      </a>
      filed a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class-action_lawsuit" title="Class-action lawsuit">
       class-action lawsuit
      </a>
      against Bloomberg L.P. on behalf of more than 80 female employees who argued that Bloomberg L.P. engaged in discrimination against women who took
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maternity_leave" title="Maternity leave">
       maternity leave
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">
       <a href="#cite_note-64">
        [64]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In August 2011, Judge
      <a href="/wiki/Loretta_A._Preska" title="Loretta A. Preska">
       Loretta A. Preska
      </a>
      of the Federal
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court" title="United States District Court">
       District Court
      </a>
      in Manhattan dismissed the charges, writing that the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission did not present sufficient evidence to support their claim.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65">
       <a href="#cite_note-65">
        [65]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2013, Preska dismissed an EEOC lawsuit on behalf of 29 pregnant employees of Bloomberg L.P.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66">
       <a href="#cite_note-66">
        [66]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition, she dismissed pregnancy bias claims from five individual plaintiffs, and allowed part of the case from a sixth plaintiff to proceed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67">
       <a href="#cite_note-67">
        [67]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bloomberg_L.P._v._Board_of_Governors_of_the_Federal_Reserve">
       Bloomberg L.P. v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Bloomberg L.P. v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Bloomberg L.P. brought a
      <a href="/wiki/Lawsuit" title="Lawsuit">
       lawsuit
      </a>
      against the Board of Directors of the
      <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_System" title="Federal Reserve System">
       Federal Reserve System
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P._v._Board_of_Governors_of_the_Federal_Reserve_System" title="Bloomberg L.P. v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System">
       Bloomberg L.P. v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System
      </a>
      ) to force the Fed to share details about its lending programs during the
      <a href="/wiki/Emergency_Economic_Stabilization_Act_of_2008" title="Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008">
       U.S. Government bailout
      </a>
      in 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68">
       <a href="#cite_note-68">
        [68]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The records documented Federal Reserve loans issued to financial firms and revealed the identities of the firms, the amounts borrowed and the collateral posted in return.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69">
       <a href="#cite_note-69">
        [69]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bloomberg L.P. won at the trial court level.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70">
       <a href="#cite_note-70">
        [70]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Second Circuit Court ruled in favor of Bloomberg L.P. in March 2010, but the case was appealed to the
      <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">
       Supreme Court
      </a>
      by a group of large U.S. commercial banks in October. In March 2011, the Supreme Court let stand the Second Circuit Court ruling mandating the release of Fed bailout details.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71">
       <a href="#cite_note-71">
        [71]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bloomberg_L.P._v._Bloomberg_Ltd">
       Bloomberg L.P. v. Bloomberg Ltd
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bloomberg_L.P.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Bloomberg L.P. v. Bloomberg Ltd">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Companies_Act_2006" title="Companies Act 2006">
       Companies Act 2006
      </a>
      . Bloomberg L.P. then amended its name to Bloomberg Finance Three L.P. Bloomberg Ltd was ordered at the
      <a href="/wiki/Company_Names_Tribunal" title="Company Names Tribunal">
       Company Names Tribunal
      </a>
      on May 11, 2009 to change its name so as to not have a name that would likely interfere, by similarity, with the goodwill of Bloomberg Finance Three L.P. as well as to pay costs.
