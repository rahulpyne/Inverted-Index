     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Joint BioEnergy Institute
      </b>
      (JBEI) is a research institute funded by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">
       United States Department of Energy
      </a>
      . It is led by the
      <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Berkeley_National_Laboratory" title="Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory">
       Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory
      </a>
      , and includes participation from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandia_National_Laboratory" title="Sandia National Laboratory">
       Sandia National Laboratory
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Livermore_National_Laboratory" title="Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory">
       Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory
      </a>
      , as well as
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">
       UC Berkeley
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Davis" title="University of California, Davis">
       UC Davis
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Institution_for_Science" title="Carnegie Institution for Science">
       Carnegie Institute
      </a>
      . It is located in
      <a href="/wiki/Emeryville,_California" title="Emeryville, California">
       Emeryville, California
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The goal of the Institute is to develop
      <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">
       biofuels
      </a>
      , bio-synthesized from cellulosic materials (see
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_generation_biofuels" title="Second generation biofuels">
       Second generation biofuels
      </a>
      ) as an alternative to fossil fuels.
     </p>
