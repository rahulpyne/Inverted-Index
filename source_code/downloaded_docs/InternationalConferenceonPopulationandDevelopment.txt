   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    International Conference on Population and Development
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>
     ,
     <a href="#p-search">
      search
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       United Nations
      </a>
      coordinated an
      <b>
       International Conference on Population and Development
      </b>
      (ICPD) in
      <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">
       Cairo
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      from 5–13 September 1994. Its resulting Program of Action is the steering document for the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">
       United Nations Population Fund
      </a>
      (UNFPA).
     </p>
     <p>
      Some 20,000 delegates from various governments, UN agencies,
      <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">
       NGOs
      </a>
      , and the media gathered for a discussion of a variety of
      <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">
       population
      </a>
      issues, including
      <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">
       immigration
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">
       infant mortality
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Birth_control" title="Birth control">
       birth control
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Family_planning" title="Family planning">
       family planning
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Female_education" title="Female education">
       education of women
      </a>
      , and protection for women from unsafe
      <a href="/wiki/Abortion" title="Abortion">
       abortion
      </a>
      services.
     </p>
     <p>
      The conference received considerable media attention due to disputes regarding the assertion of
      <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_rights" title="Reproductive rights">
       reproductive rights
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">
       Holy See
      </a>
      and several predominantly
      <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">
       Islamic
      </a>
      nations were staunch critics and
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       U.S. President
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">
       Bill Clinton
      </a>
      received considerable criticism from
      <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">
       conservatives
      </a>
      for his participation. The official spokesman for the Holy See was archbishop
      <a href="/wiki/Renato_Martino" title="Renato Martino">
       Renato Martino
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      According to the official ICPD release, the conference delegates achieved consensus on the following four qualitative and quantitative goals:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       <b>
        Universal education
       </b>
       : Universal primary education in all countries by 2015. Urge countries to provide wider access to women for secondary and higher level education as well as vocational and technical training.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Reduction of infant and child mortality
       </b>
       : Countries should strive to reduce infant and under-5 child mortality rates by one-third or to 50-70 deaths per 1000 by the year 2000. By 2015 all countries should aim to achieve a rate below 35 per 1,000 live births and under-five mortality rate below 45 per 1,000.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Reduction of maternal mortality
       </b>
       : A reduction by ½ the 1990 levels by 2000 and ½ of that by 2015. Disparities in maternal mortality within countries and between geographical regions, socio-economic and ethnic groups should be narrowed.
      </li>
      <li>
       <b>
        Access to reproductive and sexual health services including family planning
       </b>
       : Family-planning counseling, pre-natal care, safe delivery and post-natal care, prevention and appropriate treatment of infertility, prevention of abortion and the management of the consequences of abortion, treatment of reproductive tract infections, sexually transmitted diseases and other reproductive health conditions; and education, counseling, as appropriate, on human sexuality, reproductive health and responsible parenthood. Services regarding HIV/AIDS, breast cancer, infertility, and delivery should be made available. Active discouragement of
       <a href="/wiki/Female_genital_mutilation" title="Female genital mutilation">
        female genital mutilation
       </a>
       (FGM).
      </li>
     </ol>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="ICPD_and_abortion">
       ICPD and abortion
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Conference_on_Population_and_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: ICPD and abortion">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      During and after the ICPD, some interested parties attempted to interpret the term ‘reproductive health’ in the sense that it implies abortion as a means of family planning or, indeed, a right to abortion. These interpretations, however, do not reflect the consensus reached at the Conference. For the European Union, where legislation on abortion is certainly less restrictive than elsewhere, the Council Presidency has clearly stated that the Council’s commitment to promote ‘reproductive health’ did not include the promotion of abortion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Likewise, the European Commission, in response to a question from a Member of the European Parliament, clarified:
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       “The term ‘reproductive health’ was defined by the United Nations (UN) in 1994 at the Cairo International Conference on Population and Development. All Member States of the Union endorsed the Programme of Action adopted at Cairo. The Union has never adopted an alternative definition of ‘reproductive health’ to that given in the Programme of Action,
       <b>
        which makes no reference to abortion
       </b>
       .”
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      With regard to the US, only a few days prior to the Cairo Conference, the head of the US delegation, Vice President
      <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">
       Al Gore
      </a>
      , had stated for the record:
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       “Let us get a false issue off the table: the US does not seek to establish a new international right to abortion, and we do not believe that abortion should be encouraged as a method of family planning.”
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Some years later, the position of the US Administration in this debate was reconfirmed by US Ambassador to the UN,
      <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Sauerbrey" title="Ellen Sauerbrey">
       Ellen Sauerbrey
      </a>
      , when she stated at a meeting of the UN Commission on the Status of Women that:
      <i>
       “nongovernmental organizations are attempting to assert that Beijing in some way creates or contributes to the creation of an internationally recognized fundamental right to abortion”
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      She added:
      <i>
       “There is no fundamental right to abortion. And yet it keeps coming up largely driven by NGOs trying to hijack the term and trying to make it into a definition”
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
