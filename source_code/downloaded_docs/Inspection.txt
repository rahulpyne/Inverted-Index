     <p>
      An
      <b>
       inspection
      </b>
      is, most generally, an organized
      <a href="/wiki/Observational_study" title="Observational study">
       examination
      </a>
      or formal evaluation exercise. In engineering activities inspection involves the
      <a href="/wiki/Measurement" title="Measurement">
       measurements
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Test_(assessment)" title="Test (assessment)">
       tests
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Gauge_(instrument)" title="Gauge (instrument)">
       gauges
      </a>
      applied to certain characteristics in regard to an object or activity. The results are usually compared to specified
      <a href="/wiki/Requirement" title="Requirement">
       requirements
      </a>
      and standards for determining whether the item or activity is in line with these targets, often with a
      <a href="/wiki/Standard_Inspection_Procedure" title="Standard Inspection Procedure">
       Standard Inspection Procedure
      </a>
      in place to ensure consistent checking. Inspections are usually non-destructive.
     </p>
     <p>
      Inspections may be a
      <a href="/wiki/Visual_inspection" title="Visual inspection">
       visual inspection
      </a>
      or involve sensing technologies such as
      <a href="/wiki/Ultrasonic_testing" title="Ultrasonic testing">
       ultrasonic testing
      </a>
      , accomplished with a direct physical presence or remotely such as a
      <a href="/wiki/Remote_visual_inspection" title="Remote visual inspection">
       remote visual inspection
      </a>
      , and manually or automatically such as an
      <a href="/wiki/Automated_optical_inspection" title="Automated optical inspection">
       automated optical inspection
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/3D_scanner" title="3D scanner">
       Non-contact optical measurement
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Photogrammetry" title="Photogrammetry">
       Photogrammetry
      </a>
      have become common
      <a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">
       NDT
      </a>
      methods for inspection of manufactured components and design optimisation.
     </p>
     <p>
      A 2007 Scottish Government review of scrutiny of public services (the Crear Review, 2007
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crear_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Crear-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.scotland.gov.uk/Resource/Doc/82980/0053065.pdf" rel="nofollow">
       [1]
      </a>
      ) defined inspection of public services as "... periodic, targeted scrutiny of specific services, to check whether they are meeting national and local performance standards, legislative and professional requirements, and the needs of service users."
     </p>
     <p>
      A
      <i>
       surprise inspection
      </i>
      tends to have different results than an announced inspection. Leaders wanting to know how others in their organization perform can drop in without warning, to see directly what happens. If an inspection is made known in advance, it can give people a chance to cover up or to fix mistakes. This could lead to distorted and inaccurate findings. A surprise inspection, therefore, gives inspectors a better picture of the typical state of the inspected object or process than an announced inspection. It also enhances external confidence in the inspection process. See section 4.12 of the Crear report.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crear_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Crear-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Specific_inspection">
       Specific inspection
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing">
       Manufacturing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Manufacturing">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thickness_Measurement.png">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="506" data-file-width="1232" height="90" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Thickness_Measurement.png/220px-Thickness_Measurement.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Thickness_Measurement.png/330px-Thickness_Measurement.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Thickness_Measurement.png/440px-Thickness_Measurement.png 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thickness_Measurement.png" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Inspection and measurement of the thickness of the different layers of an electronic chip using THz and X-ray radiation. THz has the privilege of being non-ionizing (non-destructive) but the resolution of X-ray is higher.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Quality related in-process inspection/verification is an essential part of quality control in manufacturing.
     </p>
     <p>
      Inspection in manufacturing includes measuring, examining, testing, or gauging one or more characteristics of a product or process and comparing the results with specified requirements to determine whether is the requirements are met for each characteristic.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Common examples of inspection by measurement or
      <a href="/wiki/Gauge_(instrument)" title="Gauge (instrument)">
       gauging
      </a>
      include using a
      <a href="/wiki/Calipers" title="Calipers">
       caliper
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Micrometer" title="Micrometer">
       micrometer
      </a>
      to determine if a machined dimension of a manufactured part is within the
      <a href="/wiki/Geometric_dimensioning_and_tolerancing" title="Geometric dimensioning and tolerancing">
       dimensional tolerance
      </a>
      specified in a drawing for that part, and is thus acceptable for use.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Design_for_Inspection" title="Design for Inspection">
       Design for Inspection
      </a>
      (DFI) is a concept that should complement and work in collaboration with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_for_Manufacturability" title="Design for Manufacturability">
       Design for Manufacturability
      </a>
      (DFM) and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_for_Assembly" title="Design for Assembly">
       Design for Assembly
      </a>
      (DMA) to reduce product manufacturing cost and increase manufacturing practicality.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Photogrammetry" title="Photogrammetry">
       Photogrammetry
      </a>
      is a modern way of visual inspection, delivering high accuracy and traceability for various industries. The portable 3D system is a versatile optical coordinate measuring machine (CMM) with a wide range of capabilities. Highly accurate point measurements can be taken with inspection carried out directly to CAD models, geometry or drawings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      (DFI)
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_Equipment">
       Fire Equipment
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fire Equipment">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Most fire equipment needs to be inspected to make sure in the event of a fire, every effort has been taken to make sure it doesn't get out of control. Extinguishers are to be inspected every month by law and inspected by a servicing company at least once a year.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Business">
       Business
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Business">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/International_trade" title="International trade">
       international trade
      </a>
      several destination countries require
      <a href="/wiki/Pre-shipment_inspection" title="Pre-shipment inspection">
       pre-shipment inspection
      </a>
      . The importer instructs the shipper which inspection company should be used. The inspector makes pictures and a report to certify that the goods that are being shipped and produced are in accordance with the accompanying documents.
     </p>
     <p>
      Commodity inspection is other term that is used between buyers and sellers. The scope of work for commodity inspection depends to the buyers. Some buyers hire the inspection agencies only for pre-shipment inspections i.e. visual quality, quantity, packing, marking and loading inspections and some others request for higher level inspections and ask inspection agencies to attend in the vendor shops and inspect commodities during manufacturing processes. Normally inspection is done based on an agreed inspection and test plan (ITP).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Government">
       Government
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Government">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In government and politics, an
      <i>
       inspection
      </i>
      is the act of a monitoring authority administering an official review of various criteria (such as
      <a href="/wiki/Document" title="Document">
       documents
      </a>
      , facilities, records, and any other assets) that are deemed by the authority to be related to the inspection. Inspections are used for the purpose of determining if a body is complying with regulations. The inspector examines the criteria and talks with involved individuals. A report and evaluation follows such visits.
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_Safety_Inspection_Service" title="Food Safety Inspection Service">
       Food Safety Inspection Service
      </a>
      is charged with ensuring that all meat and egg products in the United States are safe to consume and accurately labeled. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meat_Inspection_Act" title="Meat Inspection Act">
       Meat Inspection Act
      </a>
      of 1906 authorized the Secretary of Agriculture to order meat inspections and condemn any found unfit for human consumption. The
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Monitoring,_Verification_and_Inspection_Commission" title="United Nations Monitoring, Verification and Inspection Commission">
       United Nations Monitoring, Verification and Inspection Commission
      </a>
      is a regulatory body that inspects for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">
       weapons of mass destruction
      </a>
      . The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Care_Commission" title="The Care Commission">
       Scottish Commission for the Regulation of Care
      </a>
      regulates and inspects care services in Scotland.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Road_vehicles">
       Road vehicles
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Road vehicles">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_inspection" title="Vehicle inspection">
       vehicle inspection
      </a>
      , e.g., an annual inspection, is a necessary inspection required on vehicles to conform with laws regarding safety, emissions, or both. It consists of an examination of a vehicle's components, usually done by a certified mechanic. Vehicles pass a pre-warranty inspection, if, and only if, a mechanic provide evidence for the proper working condition of the vehicle systems specified in the type of inspection.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Engineering.2C_mechanics">
       Engineering, mechanics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Engineering, mechanics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A mechanical inspection is usually undertaken to ensure the safety or reliability of structures or machinery.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In Europe bodies involved in engineering inspection may be assessed by accreditation bodies according to ISO 17020 "General criteria for the operation of various types of bodies performing inspection". This standard defines inspection as "examination of a product, process, service, or installation or their design and determination of its conformity with specific requirements or, on the basis of professional judgment, with general requirements".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">
       nondestructive testing
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Non-destructive examination (NDE) or
      <a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">
       nondestructive testing
      </a>
      (NDT) is a family of technologies used during inspection to analyze materials, components and products for either inherent defects (such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fractures" title="Fractures">
       fractures
      </a>
      or cracks), or service induced defects (damage from use). Some common methods are visual,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_computed_tomography_scanning" title="Industrial computed tomography scanning">
       industrial computed tomography scanning
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Microscopy" title="Microscopy">
       microscopy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Dye_penetrant_inspection" title="Dye penetrant inspection">
       dye penetrant inspection
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic-particle_inspection" title="Magnetic-particle inspection">
       magnetic-particle inspection
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">
       X-ray
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radiographic_testing" title="Radiographic testing">
       radiographic testing
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ultrasonic_testing" title="Ultrasonic testing">
       ultrasonic testing
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Eddy-current_testing" title="Eddy-current testing">
       eddy-current testing
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acoustic_emission_testing" title="Acoustic emission testing">
       acoustic emission testing
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Thermographic_inspection" title="Thermographic inspection">
       thermographic inspection
      </a>
      . In addition, many non-destructive inspections can be performed by a precision scale, or when in motion, a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Checkweigher" title="Checkweigher">
       checkweigher
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Stereo_microscope" title="Stereo microscope">
       Stereo microscopes
      </a>
      are often used for examining small products like
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circuit_boards" title="Circuit boards">
       circuit boards
      </a>
      for product defects.
     </p>
     <p>
      Inspection and technical assistance during turnarounds helps to decrease costly downtime as well as ensures restart of operations quickly and safely.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.eng.tau.ac.il/~bengal/SCI_paper.pdf" rel="nofollow">
       [2]
      </a>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Medical">
       Medical
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Medical">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Inspection_(medicine)" title="Inspection (medicine)">
        medical inspection
       </a>
      </i>
      is the thorough and unhurried visualization of a patient, this requires the use of the naked eye.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Military">
       Military
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Military">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      An
      <a href="/wiki/Examination_vessel" title="Examination vessel">
       examination vessel
      </a>
      is a craft used to inspect ships entering or leaving a port during wartime.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Railroad">
       Railroad
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Railroad">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The railroad's
      <a href="/wiki/Inspection_locomotive" title="Inspection locomotive">
       inspection locomotive
      </a>
      were special types of
      <a href="/wiki/Steam_locomotive" title="Steam locomotive">
       steam locomotives
      </a>
      designed to carry railroad officials on inspection tours of the railroad property.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Real_estate">
       Real estate
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Inspection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Real estate">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Property_condition_assessment" title="Property condition assessment">
       property condition assessment
      </a>
      is the examination for purposes of evaluating a commercial or business property's condition often as a part of a
      <a href="/wiki/Due_diligence" title="Due diligence">
       due diligence
      </a>
      investigation for a company to know what it is buying. Building code officials do a
      <a href="/wiki/Building_inspection" title="Building inspection">
       building inspection
      </a>
      to determine code compliance in new or altered buildings before issuing a certificate of occupancy. Residential inspections not for code compliance are called a
      <a href="/wiki/Home_inspection" title="Home inspection">
       home inspection
      </a>
      . There are numerous types of more specific real estate and infrastructure inspections such as
      <a href="/wiki/Windstorm_inspection" title="Windstorm inspection">
       windstorm inspection
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">
       energy audit
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Pipeline_video_inspection" title="Pipeline video inspection">
       pipeline video inspection
      </a>
      .
     </p>
