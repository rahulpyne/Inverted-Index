     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Consortium for Energy Efficiency
      </b>
      (
      <b>
       CEE
      </b>
      ) is a nonprofit
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501(c)(3)" title="501(c)(3)">
       501(c)(3)
      </a>
      organization that promotes the adoption of energy efficient products and services. CEE specifications are referenced by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">
       United States Department of Energy
      </a>
      and by the
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_efficiency_implementation" title="Energy efficiency implementation">
       efficiency programs
      </a>
      of many
      <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">
       natural gas
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Electric_utility" title="Electric utility">
       electric utilities
      </a>
      in the United States and Canada.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The organization’s Annual Industry Report documents the efficiency industry’s US$8 billion in annual expenditures.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Activities_and_funding">
       Activities and funding
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consortium_for_Energy_Efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Activities and funding">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      CEE influences the market for efficient products and services through 17 initiatives covering the residential, commercial and industrial sectors; Product examples include space heating, refrigeration lighting, and industrial water treatment.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      These initiatives are voluntarily adopted by CEE members—such as utility efficiency programs—to establish common levels for high efficiency equipment.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Member organizations implement initiatives through rebates, technical assistance, or other efforts in their service territories, states, or provinces. CEE also publishes Qualified Product Lists of equipment meeting high levels of efficiency performance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some equipment criteria are specifically cited by US law
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      for energy-related federal tax credits.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since 2000, CEE has conducted the Household
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ENERGY_STAR" title="ENERGY STAR">
       ENERGY STAR
      </a>
      Survey, identifying the ENERGY STAR program as one of the most recognized brands among US consumers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      According to the organization's 2013 IRS Form 990, membership dues represented US$2,533,118 (64%) of annual revenue.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      CEE received significant additional funding through its partnerships with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="US Environmental Protection Agency">
       US Environmental Protection Agency
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Energy" title="US Department of Energy">
       US Department of Energy
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Natural_Resources_Canada" title="Natural Resources Canada">
       Natural Resources Canada
      </a>
      . Founded in 1991,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      CEE members consist of more than 100 natural gas and electric utilities, 10 efficiency organizations and state agencies, and 4
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy_national_laboratories" title="United States Department of Energy national laboratories">
       DOE national laboratories
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_Industry_Report">
       Annual Industry Report
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consortium_for_Energy_Efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Annual Industry Report">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The CEE Annual Industry Report provides funding information and program activities for natural gas and electric demand side management. The 2014 study identified US$7.6 billion in investment in the US and Canada, and detailed expenditures by customer class and state.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The report indicated an increase in program budgets from US$4.4 billion in 2008 to US$7.3 billion in 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
