     <p>
      <b>
       Shinjuku
      </b>
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        新宿区
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        Shinjuku-ku
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       , "New Lodge")
      </span>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">
       special ward
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">
       Tokyo
      </a>
      , Japan. It is a major commercial and administrative centre, housing the busiest railway station in the world (
      <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">
       Shinjuku Station
      </a>
      ) and the
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Government_Building" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building">
       Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building
      </a>
      , the administration centre for the government of Tokyo. As of 2015, the ward has an estimated
      <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">
       population
      </a>
      of 337,556 and a
      <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">
       population density
      </a>
      of 18,517 people per km². The total area is 18.23 km².
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_neighborhoods">
       Geography and neighborhoods
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shinjuku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and neighborhoods">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_busker.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="5184" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Shinjuku_busker.jpg/220px-Shinjuku_busker.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Shinjuku_busker.jpg/330px-Shinjuku_busker.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Shinjuku_busker.jpg/440px-Shinjuku_busker.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_busker.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Man with guitar immediately south of the Shinjuku JR Station, a popular busking location
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Shinjuku is surrounded by
      <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">
       Chiyoda
      </a>
      to the east;
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bunkyo,_Tokyo" title="Bunkyo, Tokyo">
       Bunkyo
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toshima,_Tokyo_(ward)" title="Toshima, Tokyo (ward)">
       Toshima
      </a>
      to the north;
      <a href="/wiki/Nakano,_Tokyo" title="Nakano, Tokyo">
       Nakano
      </a>
      to the west, and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shibuya,_Tokyo" title="Shibuya, Tokyo">
       Shibuya
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">
       Minato
      </a>
      to the south.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The current city of Shinjuku grew out of several separate towns and villages, which have retained some distinctions despite growing together as part of the Tokyo metropolis.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       East Shinjuku: The area east of
       <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">
        Shinjuku Station
       </a>
       and surrounding
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinjuku-sanchome_Station" title="Shinjuku-sanchome Station">
        Shinjuku-sanchome Station
       </a>
       , historically known as Naito-Shinjuku, houses the city hall and the flagship
       <a href="/wiki/Isetan" title="Isetan">
        Isetan
       </a>
       department store, as well as several smaller areas of interest:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Kabukich%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Kabukichō, Tokyo">
          Kabukichō
         </a>
         : Tokyo's best-known red-light district, renowned for its variety of bars, restaurants, and
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sex_trade" title="Sex trade">
          sex-related establishments
         </a>
         .
        </li>
        <li>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Gai" title="Golden Gai">
          Golden Gai
         </a>
         : An area of tiny shanty-style bars and clubs. Musicians, artists, journalists, actors and directors gather here, and the ramshackle walls of the bars are literally plastered with film posters.
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Gyoen" title="Shinjuku Gyoen">
          Shinjuku Gyoen
         </a>
         : A large park, 58.3 hectares, 3.5 km in circumference, blending
         <a href="/wiki/Japanese_garden" title="Japanese garden">
          Japanese traditional
         </a>
         ,
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landscape_garden" title="Landscape garden">
          English Landscape
         </a>
         and
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_garden" title="French garden">
          French Formal
         </a>
         style gardens.
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Ni-ch%C5%8Dme" title="Shinjuku Ni-chōme">
          Shinjuku Ni-chōme
         </a>
         : Tokyo's best-known
         <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">
          gay
         </a>
         district.
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Shinjuku" title="Nishi-Shinjuku">
        Nishi-Shinjuku
       </a>
       : The area west of Shinjuku Station, historically known as Yodobashi, is home to Tokyo's largest concentration of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skyscrapers" title="Skyscrapers">
        skyscrapers
       </a>
       . Several of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tallest_buildings_in_Tokyo" title="Tallest buildings in Tokyo">
        tallest buildings in Tokyo
       </a>
       are located in this area, including the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building, KDDI Building and
       <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Park_Tower" title="Shinjuku Park Tower">
        Park Tower
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Ochiai: The northwestern corner of Shinjuku, extending to the area around
       <a href="/wiki/Ochiai-minami-nagasaki_Station" title="Ochiai-minami-nagasaki Station">
        Ochiai-minami-nagasaki Station
       </a>
       and the south side of
       <a href="/wiki/Mejiro_Station" title="Mejiro Station">
        Mejiro Station
       </a>
       , is largely residential with a small business district around
       <a href="/wiki/Nakai_Station" title="Nakai Station">
        Nakai Station
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ckubo,_Tokyo" title="Ōkubo, Tokyo">
        Ōkubo
       </a>
       : The area surrounding
       <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ckubo_Station_(Tokyo)" title="Ōkubo Station (Tokyo)">
        Okubo Station
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shin-Okubo_Station" title="Shin-Okubo Station">
        Shin-Okubo Station
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Higashi-Shinjuku_Station" title="Higashi-Shinjuku Station">
        Higashi-Shinjuku Station
       </a>
       is best known as Tokyo's historic ethnic Korean neighborhood.
      </li>
      <li>
       Totsuka: The northern portion of Shinjuku surrounding
       <a href="/wiki/Takadanobaba_Station" title="Takadanobaba Station">
        Takadanobaba Station
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Waseda_University" title="Waseda University">
        Waseda University
       </a>
       . The
       <a href="/wiki/Takadanobaba" title="Takadanobaba">
        Takadanobaba
       </a>
       area is a major residential and nightlife area for students, as well as a commuter hub.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ushigome,_Tokyo" title="Ushigome, Tokyo">
        Ushigome
       </a>
       : A largely residential area in the eastern portion of the city.
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Ichigaya" title="Ichigaya">
          Ichigaya
         </a>
         : A commercial area in eastern Shinjuku, site of the
         <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defense_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Defense (Japan)">
          Ministry of Defense
         </a>
         .
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Kagurazaka" title="Kagurazaka">
          Kagurazaka
         </a>
         : A hill descending to the
         <a href="/wiki/Iidabashi_Station" title="Iidabashi Station">
          Iidabashi Station
         </a>
         area, once one of Tokyo's last remaining
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Hanamachi" title="Hanamachi">
           hanamachi
          </a>
         </i>
         or
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Geisha" title="Geisha">
           geisha
          </a>
         </i>
         districts, and currently known for hosting a sizable French community.
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
          <a href="#cite_note-4">
           [4]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Yotsuya" title="Yotsuya">
        Yotsuya
       </a>
       : An upscale residential and commercial district in the southeast corner of Shinjuku. The Arakichō area is well known for its many small restaurants, bars, and
       <i>
        izakaya
       </i>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      "Shinjuku" is often popularly understood to mean the entire area surrounding
      <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">
       Shinjuku Station
      </a>
      , but the Shinjuku Southern Terrace complex and the areas to the west of the station and south of
      <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Kaid%C5%8D" title="Kōshū Kaidō">
       Kōshū Kaidō
      </a>
      are part of the
      <a href="/wiki/Yoyogi" title="Yoyogi">
       Yoyogi
      </a>
      district of the special ward of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shibuya,_Tokyo" title="Shibuya, Tokyo">
       Shibuya
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Naturally, most of Shinjuku is occupied by the Yodobashi Plateau, the most elevated portion of which extends through most of the Shinjuku Station area. The Kanda River runs through the Ochiai and Totsuka areas near sea level, but the Toshima Plateau also builds elevation in the northern extremities of Totsuka and Ochiai. The highest point in Shinjuku is Hakone-san in Toyama Park, 44.6 m above sea level.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shinjuku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_night_view.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="683" data-file-width="1024" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Shinjuku_night_view.jpg/220px-Shinjuku_night_view.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Shinjuku_night_view.jpg/330px-Shinjuku_night_view.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Shinjuku_night_view.jpg/440px-Shinjuku_night_view.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_night_view.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Shinjuku at night
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="689" data-file-width="1024" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg/220px-Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg/330px-Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg/440px-Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sunset_over_Shinjuku.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Street level in Shinjuku
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 1634, during the
      <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">
       Edo period
      </a>
      , as the outer moat of the
      <a href="/wiki/Edo_Castle" title="Edo Castle">
       Edo Castle
      </a>
      was built, a number of temples and shrines moved to the Yotsuya area on the western edge of Shinjuku. In 1698, Naitō-Shinjuku had developed as a new (shin) station (
      <a href="/wiki/Shukuba" title="Shukuba">
       shuku or juku
      </a>
      ) on the
      <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Kaid%C5%8D" title="Kōshū Kaidō">
       Kōshū Kaidō
      </a>
      , one of the major
      <a href="/wiki/Kaid%C5%8D" title="Kaidō">
       highways
      </a>
      of that era. Naitō was the family name of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">
       daimyo
      </a>
      whose mansion stood in the area; his land is now a public park, the Shinjuku Gyoen.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1920, the town of Naitō-Shinjuku, which comprised large parts of present-day Shinjuku (the neighbourhood, not the municipality), parts of
      <a href="/wiki/Nishi-Shinjuku" title="Nishi-Shinjuku">
       Nishi-Shinjuku
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Kabukich%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Kabukichō, Tokyo">
       Kabukichō
      </a>
      was integrated into
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_City" title="Tokyo City">
       Tokyo City
      </a>
      . Shinjuku began to develop into its current form after the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1923_Great_Kanto_earthquake" title="1923 Great Kanto earthquake">
       Great Kantō earthquake
      </a>
      in 1923, since the seismically stable area largely escaped the devastation. Consequently, West Shinjuku is one of the few areas in Tokyo with many
      <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">
       skyscrapers
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Tokyo_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Tokyo in World War II">
       Tokyo air raids
      </a>
      from May to August 1945 destroyed almost 90% of the buildings in the area in and around Shinjuku Station.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The pre-war form of Shinjuku, and the rest of Tokyo, for that matter, was retained after the war because the roads and rails, damaged as they were, remained, and these formed the heart of the Shinjuku in the post-war construction. Only in Kabuki-cho was a grand reconstruction plan put into action.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The present ward was established on March 15, 1947 with the merger of the former wards of Yotsuya, Ushigome, and Yodobashi. It served as part of the
      <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1964_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1964 Summer Olympics">
       athletics
      </a>
      50 km walk and marathon course during the
      <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">
       1964 Summer Olympics
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1991, the Tokyo Metropolitan Government moved from the
      <a href="/wiki/Marunouchi" title="Marunouchi">
       Marunouchi
      </a>
      district of
      <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">
       Chiyoda
      </a>
      to the current building in Shinjuku. (The
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Forum" title="Tokyo International Forum">
       Tokyo International Forum
      </a>
      stands on the former site vacated by the government.)
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">
       Economy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shinjuku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The area surrounding Shinjuku Station is a major economic hub of Tokyo. Many companies have their headquarters or Tokyo offices in this area, including regional telephone operator
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NTT" title="NTT">
       NTT East
      </a>
      , global camera and medical device manufacturer
      <a href="/wiki/Olympus_Corporation" title="Olympus Corporation">
       Olympus Corporation
      </a>
      , electronics giant
      <a href="/wiki/Seiko_Epson" title="Seiko Epson">
       Seiko Epson
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      fast food chains
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McDonald%27s_Japan" title="McDonald's Japan">
       McDonald's Japan
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Yoshinoya" title="Yoshinoya">
       Yoshinoya
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      travel agency
      <a href="/wiki/H.I.S._(travel_agency)" title="H.I.S. (travel agency)">
       H.I.S.
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Heavy_Industries" title="Fuji Heavy Industries">
       Fuji Heavy Industries

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">
       Government and politics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shinjuku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government and politics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1067" data-file-width="800" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg/220px-Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg/330px-Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg/440px-Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shinjuku_City_Office_2007-01.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Shinjuku City Office
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Like the
      <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">
       other wards of Tokyo
      </a>
      , Shinjuku has a status equivalent to that of a
      <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Japan" title="Municipalities of Japan">
       city
      </a>
      . The current mayor is Kenichi Yoshizumi. The ward council
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        区議会
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        kugikai
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      consists of 38 elected members; the
      <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)">
       Liberal Democratic Party
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Komeit%C5%8D_Party" title="New Komeitō Party">
       New Komeitō Party
      </a>
      together currently hold a majority. The
      <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Japan" title="Democratic Party of Japan">
       Democratic Party of Japan
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Communist_Party" title="Japanese Communist Party">
       Japanese Communist Party
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Social Democratic Party (Japan)">
       Social Democratic Party
      </a>
      are also represented together with four
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independents_(politician)" title="Independents (politician)">
       independents
      </a>
      . Shinjuku's city office
      <span style="font-weight: normal">
       (
       <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">
        区役所
       </span>
       <span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">
        ,
       </span>
       <i>
        kuyakusho
       </i>
       <sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets">
         <span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">
          ?
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       )
      </span>
      is located on the southeastern edge of Kabukichō.
     </p>
     <p>
      Shinjuku is also the location of the metropolitan government of Tokyo. The governor's office, the metropolitan assembly chamber, and all administrative head offices are located in the
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolitan_Government_Building" title="Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building">
       Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building
      </a>
      . Technically, Shinjuku is therefore the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefectural_capital" title="Prefectural capital">
       prefectural capital
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">
       Tokyo
      </a>
      ; but according to a statement by the governor's office, Tokyo (the – as administrative unit: former –
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_City" title="Tokyo City">
       Tokyo City
      </a>
      , the area of today's 23
      <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">
       special wards
      </a>
      collectively) can usually be considered the capital of Tokyo (prefecture/"Metropolis") for geographical purposes. The Geographical Survey Institute (Kokudo Chiriin) names Tōkyō (the city) as capital of Tōkyō-to (the prefecture/"Metropolis").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">
       Transportation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </span>

     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Further information:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation_in_Greater_Tokyo" title="Transportation in Greater Tokyo">

      </a>

     <p>
      Shinjuku is a major urban transit hub.
      <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Station" title="Shinjuku Station">
       Shinjuku Station
      </a>
      sees an estimated 3.64 million passengers pass through each day, making it the busiest station in the world. It houses interchanges to three subway lines and three privately owned commuter lines, as well as several JR lines.
     </p>
