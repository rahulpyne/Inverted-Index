     <p>
      An
      <b>
       intermediary
      </b>
      (or
      <b>
       go-between
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Third-party_beneficiary" title="Third-party beneficiary">
       third party
      </a>
      that offers
      <a href="/wiki/Intermediation" title="Intermediation">
       intermediation
      </a>
      services between two parties.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomacy">
       Diplomacy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Diplomacy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Diplomacy" title="Diplomacy">
       diplomacy
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">
       international relations
      </a>
      , an intermediary may convey messages between principals in a dispute, allowing the avoidance of direct principal-to-principal contact.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Where the two parties are geographically distant, the process may be termed
      <a href="/wiki/Shuttle_diplomacy" title="Shuttle diplomacy">
       shuttle diplomacy
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Law">
       Law
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Law">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">
       law
      </a>
      , intermediaries can facilitate communication between a vulnerable
      <a href="/wiki/Witness" title="Witness">
       witness
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Defendant" title="Defendant">
       defendant
      </a>
      and court personnel to acquire valuable evidence and to ensure all parties have a fair trial.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Trading">
       Trading
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Trading">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The intermediary acts as a conduit for goods or services offered by a supplier to a consumer. Typically the intermediary offers some added value to the transaction that may not be possible by direct trading.
     </p>
     <p>
      Common usage includes the
      <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">
       insurance
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">
       financial services
      </a>
      industry where e.g.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortgage_brokers" title="Mortgage brokers">
       mortgage brokers
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Insurance_broker" title="Insurance broker">
       insurance broker
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Financial_adviser" title="Financial adviser">
       financial advisers
      </a>
      offer intermediation services in the supply of financial products such as
      <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_loan" title="Mortgage loan">
       mortgage loans
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">
       insurance
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">
       investment
      </a>
      products.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Barter">
       Barter
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barter">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barter_(economics)" title="Barter (economics)">
       barter
      </a>
      , the intermediary is a person or group who stores valuables in trade until they are needed, parties to the barter or others have space available to take delivery of them and store them, or until other conditions are met. In a larger sense, an intermediary can be a person or organization who or which facilitates a contract between two other parties.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Internet is creating a transparent awareness on the threats and opportunities available to automate intermediaries in many industries.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Types">
       Types
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Types">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Intermediaries can be classified as merchant intermediaries or as accountant intermediaries. Bailey and Bakos (1997) analyzed a number of case studies and identified four roles of electronic intermediaries including information aggregating, providing trust, facilitating and matching.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
