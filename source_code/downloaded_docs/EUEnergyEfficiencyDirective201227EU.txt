     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Energy Efficiency Directive 2012/27/EU
      </b>
      (abbreviated
      <b>
       EED
      </b>
      ) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Union_directive" title="European Union directive">
       European Union directive
      </a>
      which mandates
      <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">
       energy efficiency
      </a>
      improvements within the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directive-2012-27-ec_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-directive-2012-27-ec-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was approved on 25
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      October 2012 and entered into force on 4
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      December 2012.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eceee-2013_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-eceee-2013-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :2
      </sup>
      The directive introduces legally binding measures to encourage efforts to use energy more efficiently in all stages and sectors of the supply chain. It establishes a common framework for the promotion of energy efficiency within the EU in order to meet its energy efficiency headline target of 20% by 2020. It also paves the way for further improvements thereafter.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-build-up-2012_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-build-up-2012-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The directive provides for the establishment of indicative national energy efficiency targets for 2020.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_European_Union" title="Member states of the European Union">
       Member states
      </a>
      were to have submitted their National Energy Efficiency Action Plans (NEEAP) by 30
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      April 2014, outlining the measures they have implemented to improve energy efficiency and their expected and/or achieved energy savings. In addition, member states are required to report annually on progress toward their national targets. The policy requirements in the directive are minimum obligations and member states may introduce more stringent measures.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-build-up-2012_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-build-up-2012-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Energy Efficiency Directive 2012/27/EU was preceded by the Energy Services Directive 2006/32/EC.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-national-energy-efficiency-action-plans_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-national-energy-efficiency-action-plans-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directive-2006-32-ec_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-directive-2006-32-ec-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This earlier directive contained a target of a 9% reduction in energy usage within 9
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      years of the directive coming into force. The earlier directive also required EU members to submit National Energy Efficiency Action Plans, with the first plan to be lodged by 30
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      June 2007.
     </p>
     <p>
      On 23
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      July 2014 the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
       European Commission
      </a>
      announced a new target of a 30% improvement in energy efficiency by 2030.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harvey-2013_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-harvey-2013-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
       Development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=EU_Energy_Efficiency_Directive_2012/27/EU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Documents leaked in
      <span class="nowrap">
       mid-2012
      </span>
      show that the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
       United Kingdom
      </a>
      repeatedly fought to water down key measures during the development of the directive and forced some measures to become voluntary rather than mandatory.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harvey-2012-a_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-harvey-2012-a-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      As a result, a new version of the directive allows member states to set their own energy efficiency targets, instead of the original requirement of a mandatory
      <span class="nowrap">
       EU-wide
      </span>
      target of 20% improvement.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harvey-2012-b_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-harvey-2012-b-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Measures">
       Measures
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=EU_Energy_Efficiency_Directive_2012/27/EU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Measures">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The directive promotes rules to remove
      <a href="/wiki/Barriers_to_entry" title="Barriers to entry">
       barriers
      </a>
      in energy markets and to overcome
      <a href="/wiki/Market_failure" title="Market failure">
       market failures
      </a>
      that may impede the uptake of energy efficiency. Under the directive, the public sector is to play an exemplary role and consumers will have a right to know how much energy they consume.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-build-up-2012_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-build-up-2012-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The following categories are covered by the directive:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-build-up-2012_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-build-up-2012-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       energy efficiency targets
      </li>
      <li>
       building renovation
      </li>
      <li>
       an exemplary role for public buildings
      </li>
      <li>
       energy efficiency obligation schemes
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">
        energy audits
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_management" title="Energy management">
        energy management
       </a>
       systems
      </li>
      <li>
       metering and billing information systems and the right to access this data
      </li>
      <li>
       consumer information and empowerment
      </li>
      <li>
       promotion of efficiency in heating and cooling
      </li>
      <li>
       energy transformation, transmission, and distribution
      </li>
      <li>
       availability of qualification, accreditation, and certification schemes
      </li>
      <li>
       information and training
      </li>
      <li>
       energy services
      </li>
      <li>
       an energy efficiency national fund, financing, and technical support
      </li>
      <li>
       other measures to promote energy efficiency
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="National_Energy_Efficiency_Action_Plans_and_Annual_Reports">
       National Energy Efficiency Action Plans and Annual Reports
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=EU_Energy_Efficiency_Directive_2012/27/EU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National Energy Efficiency Action Plans and Annual Reports">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Individual National Energy Efficiency Action Plans (NEEAP) for 2014 and Annual Reports for 2016 are available for download.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-national-energy-efficiency-action-plans_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-national-energy-efficiency-action-plans-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some national action plans have Wikipedia articles as well:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/German_National_Action_Plan_on_Energy_Efficiency" title="German National Action Plan on Energy Efficiency">
        German National Action Plan on Energy Efficiency
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_effectiveness">
       Reception and effectiveness
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=EU_Energy_Efficiency_Directive_2012/27/EU&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception and effectiveness">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A 2014 study finds that, despite the directive being technically complex and lacking binding targets, it is an improvement over earlier European Union policy on energy efficiency. Notwithstanding, the document is weakened by the number of exemptions and the number of passages it contains requiring interpretation. The process of implementation is also subject to problems.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zgajewski-2014_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-zgajewski-2014-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :3â€“4
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A 2016 study examines the treatment of article
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      7 of the directive
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directive-2012-27-ec_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-directive-2012-27-ec-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :15
      </sup>
      by each of the 28 member states. Titled
      <i>
       Energy efficiency obligation schemes
      </i>
      , this key article requires that countries "implement energy efficiency obligations and/or alternative policy instruments in order to reach a reduction in final energy use of 1.5% per year".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosenow-etal-2016_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rosenow-etal-2016-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       :1
      </sup>
      To fulfill this requirement, the member states have proposed very different policy measures and adopted very different calculation methods and monitoring and verification schemes. The study analyses each national action plan and estimates whether the projected savings are likely to materialise and whether these will be sufficient to meet the article
      <span class="nowrap">
      </span>
      7 target.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosenow-etal-2016_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-rosenow-etal-2016-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
