   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Renewable energy in Greece
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>
     ,
     <a href="#p-search">
      search
     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <b>
       Renewable energy in Greece
      </b>
      accounted for 8% of the country's total energy consumption in 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eurostat_renewable_energy_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Eurostat_renewable_energy-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      12% of Greece's electricity comes from Hydroelectric power plants
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Invest_in_Greece_energy_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Invest_in_Greece_energy-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_power">
       Wind power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Wind power">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Greece" title="Wind power in Greece">
       Wind power in Greece
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Wind power was due to expand
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2015)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      by 352% by 2010 to meet the European target of 20% coverage of energy needs from renewable sources. Previously,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2015)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      there were 1,028 wind turbines installed throughout Greece and the number was set to reach 2,587 wind turbines before the end of 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      According to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_for_the_Environment,_Physical_Planning_and_Public_Works_(Greece)" title="Minister for the Environment, Physical Planning and Public Works (Greece)">
       Ministry of Environment and Public Works
      </a>
      , the system would have a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intermittent_power_source#Terminology" title="Intermittent power source">
       nameplate capacity
      </a>
      of 3,372
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">
       MW
      </a>
      of power from wind alone
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2015)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      compared to 746 MW at the end of 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Greece chose to invest primarily to wind power by 77%, while the rest of renewable sources altogether comprise the remaining 23% of production with hydroelectric power being second with 11%.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_power">
       Solar power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </span>

     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_in_Greece" title="Solar power in Greece">

     <p>
      The solar energy development in Greece started in 2006 and increased substantially from 2009 because of the high feed-in tariffs introduced and the corresponding regulations for domestic applications of PVs on rooftops.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      However this mechanism overheated the market creating a big deficit of more than 500 million euros in the Greek "Operator of Electricity Market".
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      From August 2012 new regulations have been introduced including a temporary tax imposed to all operating PV plants (excluding residential applications), licensing of new PV projects have been put on halt and the feed-in tariffs were drastically reduced.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As of December 2013, the total installed photovoltaic capacity in Greece reached 2,419.2 MWp
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      of which 987.2 MWp were installed in the period between January–September 2013 despite the financial crisis.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Greece ranks 5th worldwide with regard to per capita installed PV capacity. It is expected that PV produced energy will cover up to 7% of the country's electricity demand in 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
