     <p>
      <b>
       Motors Liquidation Company
      </b>
      formerly
      <b>
       General Motors Corporation
      </b>
      , was the company left to settle past liability claims from
      <a href="/wiki/General_Motors_Chapter_11_reorganization" title="General Motors Chapter 11 reorganization">
       Chapter 11 reorganization
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">
       General Motors
      </a>
      . It exited bankruptcy on March 31, 2011 only to be carved into four trusts; the first to settle the claims of
      <a href="/wiki/Unsecured_creditor" title="Unsecured creditor">
       unsecured creditors
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/OTC_Markets_Group" title="OTC Markets Group">
       OTC Pink
      </a>
      :
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.otcmarkets.com/stock/MTLQU/quote" rel="nofollow">
       MTLQU
      </a>
      ), the second to handle environmental response for MLC's remaining assets, a third to handle present and future
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asbestos_litigation" title="Asbestos litigation">
       asbestos-related claims
      </a>
      , and a fourth for litigation claims.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtr_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-rtr-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Motors Liquidation Company's stock symbol was changed from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Motors_Corporation" title="General Motors Corporation">
       GMGMQ
      </a>
      to MTLQQ, effective July 15, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      MTLQQ stock was cancelled. Its unsecured creditors were issued stock for the Motors Liquidation Company General Unsecured Creditors Trust under the symbol MTLQU.
     </p>

     <p>
      On the morning of 1 June 2009,
      <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet-Saturn_of_Harlem" title="Chevrolet-Saturn of Harlem">
       Chevrolet-Saturn of Harlem
      </a>
      , a dealership in
      <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">
       Manhattan
      </a>
      that was owned by GM itself, filed for bankruptcy protection there, followed in the same court by General Motors Corporation (the main GM in Detroit), GM's subsidiary
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturn_LLC" title="Saturn LLC">
       Saturn LLC
      </a>
      , and Saturn LLC's subsidiary Saturn Distribution Corporation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hughes_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hughes-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      All cases were assigned to Judge
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gerber" title="Robert Gerber">
       Robert Gerber
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The filing by the dealership declared General Motors to be a
      <a href="/wiki/Debtor_in_possession" title="Debtor in possession">
       debtor in possession
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hughes_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-hughes-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Manhattan dealership's filing allowed General Motors to file its own bankruptcy petition in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Bankruptcy_Court_for_the_Southern_District_of_New_York" title="United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of New York">
       United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of New York
      </a>
      , its preferred court. Normally for such cases, the company would have filed in the courts located in the state(s) where the company is incorporated, or where it conducts operations, which for
      <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">
       Detroit
      </a>
      -headquartered General Motors would have been the courts in
      <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">
       Michigan
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">
       Delaware
      </a>
      , where it is incorporated. General Motors' attorneys, however, preferred to file in the federal courts in New York, because those courts have a reputation for expertise in bankruptcy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters1_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters1-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a press conference that began four hours and eighteen minutes after the filing, the GM Chief Executive Officer,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fritz_Henderson" title="Fritz Henderson">
       Fritz Henderson
      </a>
      , stressed that he intended for the bankruptcy process to move quickly.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition to Henderson's press conference,
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">
       President of the United States
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">
       Barack Obama
      </a>
      made a speech from the
      <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">
       White House
      </a>
      four hours three minutes after the court filing. This did not include GMCL as this would help the Canadian economy like the pipe line was stated
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <p>
      Prior to General Motors
      <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy" title="Bankruptcy">
       bankruptcy
      </a>
      filing, U.S. state law governed the minimum required distance between same-franchise dealers; known as the eight-mile rule. The eight-mile rule prohibits same-franchise dealers from locating within eight miles of each other. In 2001 General Motors formally declared Project 2000 would be implemented as part of a brand-channeling strategy designed to consolidate its network of privately owned individual
      <a href="/wiki/Franchising" title="Franchising">
       franchise
      </a>
      dealerships and reduce the number of locations from 7,300 to 3,800. Project 2000 served as the primary mechanism for combining single-point Buick-Pontiac-GMC dealer locations together forming a single corporate brand division (BPG). Project 2000 also targeted single-point Cadillac-Hummer-Saab dealer locations and allowed single-point Chevrolet and Saturn dealer locations to stand on their own. Project 2000 calculated the
      <a href="/wiki/Fair_market_value" title="Fair market value">
       fair market value
      </a>
      for each individually owned dealer franchise to be $3,000 per vehicle based on total volume from any one of the previous three years. Upon agreement of a fair market value, dealers with interest in selling their franchise rights were free to negotiate with each other, and ultimately execute a buy-sell agreement which contained mutually agreed upon terms and conditions required to legally sell and transfer franchise
      <a href="/wiki/Property" title="Property">
       property
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ownership" title="Ownership">
       ownership
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Rights" title="Rights">
       rights
      </a>
      . Section 363 of the Federal Bankruptcy Code expedited General Motors Project 2000 efforts to reduce its dealer network and circumvent state and federal
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criminal" title="Criminal">
       criminal
      </a>
      laws prohibiting the illegal sale of property, or transfer of ownership by an unauthorized party. Thousands of family-owned, [profitable], well-capitalized dealerships were forced to forfeit their franchise rights to a neighboring dealer-competitor selected by General Motors. Compensation has not yet
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2011)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      been awarded in cases where individual dealer franchise property and ownership rights were transferred for the purpose of granting such rights to a nearby dealer selected by General Motors. In addition to reports of
      <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">
       fraud
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Theft" title="Theft">
       theft
      </a>
      filed with the police, GM dealers joined to form the
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.hometownautodealers.org" rel="nofollow">
       Committee to Restore Dealer Rights
      </a>
      . The committee to restore dealer rights is responsible for helping draft HR 2743, which asserts the action taken by General Motors to consolidate its dealer network by transferring individual dealer franchise ownership and property without compensation was illegal. As of 22 February 2010
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      , HR 2743 remains in committee.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Court_schedule_and_motions">
       Court schedule and motions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Court schedule and motions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The General Motors bankruptcy case was formally entitled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/In_re" title="In re">
        In re
       </a>
       General Motors Corp.
      </i>
      , case number 09-50026 in the Southern District, Manhattan, New York.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pettersso_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pettersso-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      General Motors was represented by the New York specialist law firm Weil, Gotshal &amp; Manges. The United States Treasury and an ad hoc group of the bondholders of General Motors Corporation were also represented in court.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters2_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      One of the first motions filed in court was to void the leases on the seven corporate jets, and corporate aircraft hangar at Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport, which the company said were no longer valuable to the company's business. A GM spokesman said that the company had found itself unable to escape the lease in 2008 when it had tried to.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pettersso_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pettersso-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On June 1, 2009, the court gave interim approval to GM's request to borrow $15 billion as
      <a href="/wiki/Debtor-in-possession_financing" title="Debtor-in-possession financing">
       debtor-in-possession financing
      </a>
      , the company only having $2 billion cash in hand. The United States Treasury had argued in court that it was the only source of such debtor in possession funding, and that without the money from the loan General Motors would have no option but
      <a href="/wiki/Liquidation" title="Liquidation">
       liquidation
      </a>
      . Other motions in the first-day hearing included motions to approve payments to key suppliers and to employees and distributors who are in possession of goods manufactured for General Motors. All motions passed in court without substantial objection.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters2_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scinta_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Scinta-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The case schedule laid out by the court was as follows:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scinta_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Scinta-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       June 19, 2009: Deadline for filing all objections to the sale of General Motors.
      </li>
      <li>
       June 22, 2009: Deadline for making competing bids in the auction of General Motors' assets.
      </li>
      <li>
       June 25, 2009: Final hearing on the bankruptcy loan.
      </li>
      <li>
       July 10, 2009: Deadline for completion of the sale, requested by the U.S. Treasury and General Motors.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters2_9-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scinta_10-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Scinta-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_of_good_assets_to_New_GM">
       Sale of good assets to New GM
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sale of good assets to New GM">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      GM's assets were sold in a
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/html/uscode11/usc_sec_11_00000363----000-.html" rel="nofollow">
       section 363 sale
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters2_9-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GM_Bankruptcy_Press_Release-2009-06-01_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-GM_Bankruptcy_Press_Release-2009-06-01-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pleading-Sale_to_VAH_LLC-2009-06-01_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pleading-Sale_to_VAH_LLC-2009-06-01-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Business_Week-AP-Beck-2009-06-02_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Business_Week-AP-Beck-2009-06-02-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Because the price that these assets were expected to sell for was very high, there was expected to be only one bidder in the auction, a new company NGMCO Inc.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-publish.media.gm.com_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-publish.media.gm.com-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This company had been formed by the United States government with a 60% stake, the federal government of
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Canada" title="Government of Canada">
       Canada
      </a>
      and provincial government of
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ontario" title="Government of Ontario">
       Ontario
      </a>
      with a 12% stake, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Auto_Workers" title="United Auto Workers">
       United Auto Workers
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Auto_Workers" title="Canadian Auto Workers">
       Canadian Auto Workers
      </a>
      unions
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      with a 17.5% stake, and the unsecured bondholders of General Motors with a 10% stake.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters2_9-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-Reuters2-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      "Old GM" was renamed Motors Liquidation Company.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-Maynard-2009-07-10_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NYT-Maynard-2009-07-10-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A creditor meeting, at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Hilton" title="New York Hilton">
       New York Hilton
      </a>
      , held by the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Trustee_Program" title="United States Trustee Program">
       United States Trustee Program
      </a>
      , was scheduled for June 3, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scinta_10-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Scinta-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Hummer_collapse">
       Hummer collapse
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hummer collapse">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On June 1, 2009, GM announced that the
      <a href="/wiki/Hummer" title="Hummer">
       Hummer
      </a>
      brand would be discontinued. The following day GM announced that it had reached a deal to sell the brand to an undisclosed buyer.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Later, on June 2, 2009, the buyer was disclosed to be China-based
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sichuan_Tengzhong_Heavy_Industrial_Machinery_Company" title="Sichuan Tengzhong Heavy Industrial Machinery Company">
       Sichuan Tengzhong Heavy Industrial Machinery Company
      </a>
      Ltd.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Sichuan Tengzhong itself confirmed the deal on their website the same day.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sichuan_Tengzhong-Hummer_Deal-2009-06-02_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sichuan_Tengzhong-Hummer_Deal-2009-06-02-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The proposed transaction was scheduled to close in the third quarter of 2009, subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals; financial terms of the agreement were not disclosed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sichuan_Tengzhong-Hummer_Deal-2009-06-02_22-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Sichuan_Tengzhong-Hummer_Deal-2009-06-02-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Chinese regulators refused to allow for the purchase of the brand and GM decided on February 24, 2010, to retire the brand. Despite the failed sale, GM discussed entertaining interest in part of the Hummer brand, subsequently made no effort in that direction, leaving Hummer to close.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Failed_sale_of_Saturn">
       Failed sale of Saturn
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Failed sale of Saturn">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On June 5, 2009, GM announced that the
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Corporation" title="Saturn Corporation">
       Saturn
      </a>
      brand would be sold to the
      <a href="/wiki/Penske_Automotive_Group" title="Penske Automotive Group">
       Penske Automotive Group
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      GM would continue building the
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Aura" title="Saturn Aura">
       Aura
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Outlook" title="Saturn Outlook">
       Outlook
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Vue" title="Saturn Vue">
       Vue
      </a>
      for Penske for two years. However, the Penske deal failed and now the Saturn division is defunct.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_of_Saab_to_Spyker_Cars">
       Sale of Saab to Spyker Cars
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Motors_Liquidation_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sale of Saab to Spyker Cars">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On June 16, 2009, it was announced that
      <a href="/wiki/Koenigsegg" title="Koenigsegg">
       Koenigsegg
      </a>
      and a group of Norwegian investors plan to acquire the
      <a href="/wiki/Saab_Automobile" title="Saab Automobile">
       Saab
      </a>
      brand from
      <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">
       General Motors
      </a>
      . GM will continue to supply architecture and powertrain technology for an unspecified amount of time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It also becomes the last brand/subsidiary from GM to be sold (
      <a href="/wiki/Hummer" title="Hummer">
       Hummer
      </a>
      was first, followed by
      <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Corporation" title="Saturn Corporation">
       Saturn
      </a>
      ). The deal failed on November 24, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
      GM, however, requested
      <a href="/wiki/Spyker_Cars" title="Spyker Cars">
       Spyker Cars
      </a>
      to acquire Saab from MLC a few weeks later. But however, MLC announced it would close Saab on December 19, 2009, although this plan was later reversed. Motors Liquidation Company had until January 7, 2010, for the deadline of the revised bid. The sale of Saab to Spyker was approved on January 26, 2010,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and completed on February 23, 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
