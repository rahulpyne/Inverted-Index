     <p>
      The
      <b>
       National Energy Foundation
      </b>
      (NEF) is an independent British charity, established to encourage the more sustainable use and generation of energy.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">
       Aims
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Energy_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aims">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The charity aims to
      <i>
       improve the use of energy in buildings
      </i>
      . This statement simplified the earlier stated mission to mobilise individuals, businesses and communities to make their contribution to reducing
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">
       carbon dioxide
      </a>
      emissions through
      <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">
       energy efficiency
      </a>
      and use of sustainable energy sources (notably
      <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">
       renewable energy
      </a>
      ) to maintain affordable energy services and combat
      <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">
       global climate change
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">
       Activities
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Energy_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      NEF was founded in 1988 by
      <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">
       Milton Keynes
      </a>
      Development Corporation to preserve for the future benefit of the UK public some of the energy initiatives that had been undertaken within the new city. Its initial projects included extending a technical home energy label (known as the Milton Keynes Energy Cost Index – MKECI) into a more generally applicable home energy rating called the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Home_Energy_Rating" title="National Home Energy Rating">
       National Home Energy Rating
      </a>
      (NHER), and technical energy monitoring work on low energy homes in the Milton Keynes Energy Park. In 1994, the NHER Scheme was injected into a subsidiary company (sold in 2011) to run the business on behalf of the charity. In recent years, many of its projects have more of an applied research element, including collaborative projects with UK universities.
     </p>
     <p>
      From 1993–2001 NEF worked closely with the
      <a href="/wiki/Energy_Saving_Trust" title="Energy Saving Trust">
       Energy Saving Trust
      </a>
      , setting up the UK's network of
      <b>
       Energy Efficiency Advice Centres
      </b>
      (EEACs) on their behalf, and managing the network for the first five years. But as domestic energy efficiency moved more into the mainstream, NEF expanded its interests into Renewable Energy (from 1995) and non-domestic energy efficiency, through its management of the
      <b>
       Energy Efficiency Accreditation Scheme
      </b>
      from 1998 onwards. In 2005 this scheme was transferred to the
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_Trust" title="Carbon Trust">
       Carbon Trust
      </a>
      but still managed by NEF; in 2008 it was relaunched as the Carbon Trust Standard with some NEF administrative involvement until 2013. NEF's current focus includes post-occupancy
      <a href="/wiki/Building_performance" title="Building performance">
       building performance evaluation
      </a>
      , as well as identifying strategies to minimise the performance gap between the design and in-use energy consumption of buildings. It also works with housing stock owners (such as UK local authorities and
      <a href="/wiki/Housing_association" title="Housing association">
       housing associations
      </a>
      ) to minimise tenants' housing use, and operates a number of regional programmes to combat
      <a href="/wiki/Fuel_poverty_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Fuel poverty in the United Kingdom">
       fuel poverty
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      After around 2002, most local and regional domestic energy efficiency activities were handed over to the United Sustainable Energy Agency (formerly Milton Keynes Energy Agency – MKEA), but NEF retained involvement in projects such as Milton Keynes' Low Carbon Living (formerly ThimkAgain!) campaign, and in 2013 agreed to merge with USEA. The Foundation undertakes work with local communities across the UK in partnership with the Sustainable Energy Academy, most notably through the
      <b>
       Superhomes
      </b>
      project (originally known as Old Home Superhome), and through its involvement with the Local United and Communities &amp; Climate Action Alliance projects.
     </p>

      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">

     <p>
      Most of the charity's work is within the UK, although it participates in a number of EU projects, has undertaken some work in SE Asia and actively supports international standardisation work through
      <a href="/wiki/European_Committee_for_Standardization" title="European Committee for Standardization">
       CEN
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">
       ISO
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Renewable_Energy">
       Renewable Energy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Energy_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renewable Energy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      NEF has also been active in the area of
      <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">
       renewable energy
      </a>
      and, in particular, with integrating it into buildings and assisting local authorities and other bodies to create the right environment for a wider uptake of
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">
       sustainable energy
      </a>
      . Since 2015, it has operated the formerly independent
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.yougen.co.uk/" rel="nofollow">
       Yougen
      </a>
      website, targeted mainly at UK households wishing to install small-scale renewable energy measures. Historically, it also provided secretariat functions to trade associations such as the Solar Trade Association and the Ground Source Heat Pump Association, as well as running from 2010-15 the Green Energy Supply Certification Scheme that implemented Ofgem's guidelines for marketing "green" electricity.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Work_with_young_people">
       Work with young people
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Energy_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Work with young people">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Although working mainly with organisations and the general public, NEF has from time to time run projects aimed at educating young people. This includes the
      <b>
       Energy Envoys
      </b>
      project, aimed at encouraging students undertaking the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Edinburgh%27s_Award" title="Duke of Edinburgh's Award">
       Duke of Edinburgh's Award
      </a>
      to become involved in saving energy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2007-8 it launched a
      <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_game" title="Global warming game">
       global warming game
      </a>
      called
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logicity" title="Logicity">
       Logicity
      </a>
      , funded by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defra" title="Defra">
       Defra
      </a>
      Climate Challenge Programme, in an attempt to raise interest in the subject among young adults. For several years it ran a mobile education unit called the
      <b>
       Green Energy Machine
      </b>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">
       Buildings
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Energy_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Buildings">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      NEF occupies a purpose built low energy building designed to demonstrate some of the features that it espouses. Built in 2002-3, its office is heated by a
      <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_heat_pump" title="Geothermal heat pump">
       Ground Source Heat Pump
      </a>
      , with hot water being partly provided by an evacuated tube
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_hot_water" title="Solar hot water">
       solar water heating
      </a>
      system. There is a winter back-up heating system in the form of a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wood_pellets" title="Wood pellets">
       wood pellet
      </a>
      stove. The building is naturally ventilated, with
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Brise_soleil" title="Brise soleil">
        brise-soleils
       </a>
      </i>
      to prevent overheating in summer, and use of daylight is encouraged through
      <a href="/wiki/Light_tube" title="Light tube">
       Light tubes
      </a>
      ('SunPipes'). Around a fifth of the office's energy is supplied by a 6.47kWp
      <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">
       photovoltaic
      </a>
      array. As well as energy features, its environmental credentials include the use of rainwater for toilet flushing and a low thermal mass sustainably sourced timber frame structure.
     </p>
     <p>
      The adjacent low energy building was constructed in 1999 to show how super-insulation can enable a 1,000m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      building to be heated from a gas condensing boiler little larger than would be used in a typical UK home. This served as NEF's headquarters until 2004. A 39kWp photovoltaic array was added to this building in 2012, taking advantage of special fixings that had been designed into the original building.
     </p>
