     <p>
      <b>
       Renewable energy in Pakistan
      </b>
      is a relatively underdeveloped sector; however, in recent years, there has been some interest by environmentalist groups and from the authorities to explore renewable energy resources for energy production, in light of the energy crises and power shortages affecting the country. Most of the renewable energy in Pakistan comes from
      <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">
       hydroelectricity
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_power">
       Solar power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Solar power">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_in_Pakistan" title="Solar power in Pakistan">
       Solar power in Pakistan
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      There have been some efforts to install and expand the use of solar energy in Pakistan. The average amount of daily sunlight in Pakistan is nine and a half hours
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      ; there are a few cloudy days even in the wettest regions. Eight power generation plants have been installed and eleven are in various stages of completion.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (April 2014)">
          when?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Further feasibility studies are undergoing. In December 1981, the first solar
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic" title="Photovoltaic">
       photovoltaic
      </a>
      system was commissioned, located in
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mumniala&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mumniala (page does not exist)">
       Mumniala
      </a>
      (a village 60 km from
      <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">
       Islamabad
      </a>
      ). Four solar systems has been commissioned in Khukhera (Lasbela district), Ghakar (Attock district), Malmari (Thatta district) (now that system is unserviceable) and Dittal Khan Leghari, Digri (Mirpurkhas district).The Punjab government announced the establishment of Quaid-e-Azam Solar Park over an area of 5,000 acres in the Cholistan Development Authority in Bahawalpur.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A practical example of the use of solar energy can be seen in some rural villages of Pakistan where houses have been provided with solar panels that run electric fans and energy-saving bulbs. One notable and successfully implemented case was the village of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Narian_Khorian&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Narian Khorian (page does not exist)">
       Narian Khorian
      </a>
      (about 50 kilometers from Islamabad), which employs the use of 100 solar panels installed by a local firm, free of cost; these panels have provide energy through lights and fan facilities to some 100 households.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The alternative energy development board installed 200 solar home systems at District Khuzdar Balochistan in a remote off-grid village Karak. Government of Balochistan launched a programme to electrify off-grid villages of various districts through solar technology.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_power">
       Wind power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wind power">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Pakistan" title="Wind power in Pakistan">
       Wind power in Pakistan
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Pakistan is developing wind power plants in
      <a href="/wiki/Jhimpir" title="Jhimpir">
       Jhimpir
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Gharo" title="Gharo">
       Gharo
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Keti_Bandar" title="Keti Bandar">
       Keti Bandar
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Bin_Qasim_Town" title="Bin Qasim Town">
       Bin Qasim
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">
       Sindh
      </a>
      . The government of Pakistan decided to develop wind power energy sources due to problems supplying energy to the southern coastal regions of
      <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">
       Sindh
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Balochistan" title="Balochistan">
       Balochistan
      </a>
      , the project was undertaken with assistance from the government of China. Another area with potential is Swat which shows good wind conditions in windpower investment. The
      <a href="/wiki/Chagai_District" title="Chagai District">
       Chagai District
      </a>
      in Balochistan has good potential for wind power in the Nukundi area near the Afghan/Iran Border, with wind speeds often 12.5% higher than average required for energy generation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Some examples are:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Jhimpir_Wind_Power_Plant" title="Jhimpir Wind Power Plant">
        Jhimpir Wind Power Plant
       </a>
       (operational)
      </li>
      <li>
       Gharo Wind Power Plant (operational)
      </li>
      <li>
       Bin Qasim Wind Power Project (under construction)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Tidal_power">
       Tidal power
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tidal power">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Tidal_power" title="Tidal power">
       Tidal power
      </a>
      has not yet been operational in Pakistan compared to other renewable energy technologies, but in near future it may play a key role. Some think that tidal power plants in coastal creeks of Pakistan can serve the energy crisis conundrum up to some level.
     </p>
     <p>
      The coastline of Pakistan, which is about 1,045 km-long with dominant features, is the best resource for harnessing tidal energy. In
      <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">
       Sindh
      </a>
      , two sites, creek system of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_delta" title="Indus delta">
       Indus delta
      </a>
      of 170 km and two to five metres tidal heights at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korangi_Creek" title="Korangi Creek">
       Korangi Creek
      </a>
      , are available to exploit the tidal energy.
      <a href="/wiki/Sonmiani_Beach" title="Sonmiani Beach">
       Sonmiani Beach
      </a>
      and Kalamat are also good prospects of tidal energy in
      <a href="/wiki/Balochistan" title="Balochistan">
       Balochistan
      </a>
      . Government has issued licence to private companies to take measures to build tidal power stations in February 2013. Since then, the engineering work is under process, and initially a 10 MW plant is proposed at Sonmiani Bay. Construction was expected to start by the end of 2013.
     </p>
