     <p>
      <b>
       Renewable energy in Chile
      </b>
      is a fast growing sector that in 2014 provided 9% of the
      <a href="/wiki/Electricity_sector_in_Chile" title="Electricity sector in Chile">
       country's electricity
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
       Chile
      </a>
      has vast
      <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_energy" title="Geothermal energy">
       geothermal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_energy" title="Wind energy">
       wind energy
      </a>
      resources while
      <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">
       fossil fuel
      </a>
      resources are limited.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_2014_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_2014-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It has a legislated
      <a href="/wiki/Mandatory_renewable_energy_target" title="Mandatory renewable energy target">
       renewable energy target
      </a>
      of 20% by 2025.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Geothermal">
       Geothermal
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geothermal">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_power_in_Chile" title="Geothermal power in Chile">
       Geothermal power in Chile
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      With 15% of the world's volcanoes, Chile has vast geothermal potential, estimated at 16,000 MW. This includes the
      <a href="/wiki/El_Tatio" title="El Tatio">
       El Tatio
      </a>
      geyser field, with 8% of the world's geysers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_energy">
       Solar energy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Solar energy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_power_in_Chile" title="Solar power in Chile">
       Solar power in Chile
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In 2014, a 100 MW photovoltaic plant located near
      <a href="/wiki/Copiap%C3%B3" title="Copiapó">
       Copiapó
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Atacama_Desert" title="Atacama Desert">
       Atacama Desert
      </a>
      was inaugurated. It was the largest in Latin America at the time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2016, SolarPack won an electricity auction (held without disclosing bidders' power source) by bidding $29.1/MWh;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      a record low price.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ct2016_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ct2016-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_energy">
       Wind energy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wind energy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The 115 MW
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Array%C3%A1n_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Arrayán Wind Farm (page does not exist)">
       El Arrayán Wind Farm
      </a>
      is the largest in Chile,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-REF_2014_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-REF_2014-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and at the time of its inauguration in 2014 it was the largest in Latin America.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_2014_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_2014-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The 2016 electricity auction for a combined 12.34 TWh was won partly by wind power.
      <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Renewable_Power" title="Mainstream Renewable Power">
       Mainstream Renewable Power
      </a>
      won 7 projects with 985 MW supplying 3.7 TWh/year at between $38.8 and $47.2 per MWh.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ct2016_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-ct2016-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
