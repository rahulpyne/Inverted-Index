     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Department of Industry and Science
      </b>
      was a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_department" title="Government department">
       department
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Australia" title="Government of Australia">
       Australian Government
      </a>
      responsible for consolidating the Government’s efforts to drive economic growth, productivity and competitiveness by bringing together industry, energy, resources and science.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Industry_and_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The department was established on 23 December 2014, taking on most of the functions of the previous
      <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Industry_(Australia)" title="Department of Industry (Australia)">
       Department of Industry
      </a>
      ; but transferring skills responsibilities to the
      <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Education_and_Training_(Australia)" title="Department of Education and Training (Australia)">
       Department of Education and Training
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Department's creation, and the associated swearing in of Industry Minister
      <a href="/wiki/Ian_Macfarlane_(politician)" title="Ian Macfarlane (politician)">
       Ian Macfarlane
      </a>
      when he added 'Science' to his official title, was seen by several media outlets as an admission from the
      <a href="/wiki/Abbott_Government" title="Abbott Government">
       Abbott Government
      </a>
      that it had been a mistake to not have a dedicated Science minister in 2014.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Department was abolished and replaced with the
      <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Industry,_Innovation_and_Science" title="Department of Industry, Innovation and Science">
       Department of Industry, Innovation and Science
      </a>
      in September 2015, to create a new federal government focus on innovation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">
       Scope
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Department_of_Industry_and_Science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scope">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As outlined in the December 2014 Administrative Arrangements Orders,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the department was responsible for a wide range of functions including:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Manufacturing and commerce including industry and market development
      </li>
      <li>
       Industry innovation policy and technology diffusion
      </li>
      <li>
       Construction industry, excluding workplace relations
      </li>
      <li>
       Facilitation of the development of service industries generally
      </li>
      <li>
       Trade marks, plant breeders’ rights and patents of inventions and designs
      </li>
      <li>
       Anti-dumping
      </li>
      <li>
       Civil space issues
      </li>
      <li>
       Science policy
      </li>
      <li>
       Energy policy
      </li>
     </ul>
