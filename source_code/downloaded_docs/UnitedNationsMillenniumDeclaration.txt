     <p>
      On 8 September 2000, following a three-day
      <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Summit" title="Millennium Summit">
       Millennium Summit
      </a>
      of world leaders at the headquarters of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
       United Nations
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">
       General Assembly
      </a>
      adopted the
      <b>
       Millennium Declaration
      </b>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUN2000_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUN2000-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      A follow-up outcome of the resolution was passed by the General Assembly on 14 December 2000 to guide its implementation. Progress on implementation of the Declaration was reviewed at the
      <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">
       2005 World Summit
      </a>
      of leaders.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Chapters">
       Chapters
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Millennium Declaration has eight chapters and key objectives, adopted by 189 world leaders during the summit: The Declaration, after the
      <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Declaration_and_Programme_of_Action" title="Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action">
       Vienna Declaration and Programme of Action
      </a>
      , stresses the observance of
      <a href="/wiki/International_human_rights_law" title="International human rights law">
       international human rights law
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/International_humanitarian_law" title="International humanitarian law">
       international humanitarian law
      </a>
      under the Principles of
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">
       United Nations Charter
      </a>
      as well as the treaties on
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">
       sustainable development
      </a>
      . The Declaration also urges observance of the
      <a href="/wiki/Olympic_truce" title="Olympic truce">
       Olympic truce
      </a>
      individually and collectively.CITEREFUN2000
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       Values and Principles
      </li>
     </ol>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Freedom
        </li>
        <li>
         Equality
        </li>
        <li>
         Solidarity
        </li>
        <li>
         Tolerance
        </li>
        <li>
         Respect for nature - "Shown in the management of all living species and natural resources, in accordance with the precepts of sustainable development."
        </li>
        <li>
         Shared responsibility
        </li>
       </ul>
      </dd>
     </dl>
