     <p>
      <b>
       Nicola Armaroli
      </b>
      (born 2 September 1966 in
      <a href="/wiki/Bentivoglio,_Emilia-Romagna" title="Bentivoglio, Emilia-Romagna">
       Bentivoglio
      </a>
      ) is an
      <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">
       Italian
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Chemist" title="Chemist">
       chemist
      </a>
      . His main research fields are
      <a href="/wiki/Photochemistry" title="Photochemistry">
       photochemistry
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Photophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Photophysics (page does not exist)">
       photophysics
      </a>
      of materials and molecules for
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar energy
      </a>
      conversion and advanced
      <a href="/wiki/Lighting" title="Lighting">
       lighting
      </a>
      devices.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_research_activity">
       Life and research activity
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Armaroli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and research activity">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Armaroli studied
      <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">
       chemistry
      </a>
      at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bologna" title="University of Bologna">
       University of Bologna
      </a>
      and got his
      <a href="/wiki/Laurea" title="Laurea">
       laurea
      </a>
      in 1990. In 1994 he got his
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">
       PhD
      </a>
      under supervision of
      <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Balzani" title="Vincenzo Balzani">
       Vincenzo Balzani
      </a>
      . After that he was a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-doc" title="Post-doc">
       post-doc
      </a>
      at
      <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green_State_University" title="Bowling Green State University">
       Bowling Green State University
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Research_Council_(Italy)" title="National Research Council (Italy)">
       Italian National Research Counsil
      </a>
      . There he started his own research project in 1997 and consequently became a Senior Research Scientist in 2002. Since 2007 he is Research Director.
     </p>
     <p>
      As of October 2014 he had published 168 papers in refereed
      <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">
       scientific journals
      </a>
      . Furthermore he is the
      <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">
       author
      </a>
      of five books and several book chapters. His
      <a href="/wiki/H-index" title="H-index">
       H-index
      </a>
      is 54, making him one of the leading Italian scientists.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Besides his research activity he is member of the Editorial Boards of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemistry_-_A_European_Journal" title="Chemistry - A European Journal">
       Chemistry - A European Journal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Photochemical_and_Photobiological_Sciences" title="Photochemical and Photobiological Sciences">
       Photochemical and Photobiological Sciences
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Polyhedron" title="Polyhedron">
       Polyhedron
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frontiers_in_Inorganic_Chemistry&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frontiers in Inorganic Chemistry (page does not exist)">
       Frontiers in Inorganic Chemistry
      </a>
      and director of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SAPERE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SAPERE (page does not exist)">
       SAPERE
      </a>
      , the oldest Italian scientific journal, which was founded in 1935.
     </p>
