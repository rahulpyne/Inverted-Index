     <p>
      <b>
       Water conservation
      </b>
      includes all the policies, strategies and activities made to manage
      <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">
       fresh water
      </a>
      as a sustainable resource, to protect the water environment, and to meet current and future human demand. Population, household size, and growth and affluence all affect how much water is used. Factors such as
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
       climate change
      </a>
      have increased pressures on natural water resources especially in
      <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">
       manufacturing
      </a>
      and agricultural
      <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">
       irrigation
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The goals of water conservation efforts include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       Ensuring availability of water for future generations where the withdrawal of fresh water from an ecosystem does not exceed its natural replacement rate.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">
        Energy conservation
       </a>
       as water pumping, delivery and waste water treatment facilities consume a significant amount of energy. In some regions of the world over 15% of total electricity consumption is devoted to water management.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Habitat_conservation" title="Habitat conservation">
        Habitat conservation
       </a>
       where minimizing human water use helps to preserve freshwater habitats for local wildlife and migrating waterfowl, but also
       <a href="/wiki/Water_quality" title="Water quality">
        water quality
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ul>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Strategies">
       Strategies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Strategies">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The key activities that benefit water conservation are as follows :
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       Any beneficial deduction in water loss, use and waste of resources.
      </li>
      <li>
       Avoiding any damage to
       <a href="/wiki/Water_quality" title="Water quality">
        water quality
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       Improving
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_management" title="Water management">
        water management
       </a>
       practices that reduce the use or enhance the beneficial use of water.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vickers.2C_Amy_2002_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vickers.2C_Amy_2002-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
     </ol>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Social_solutions">
       Social solutions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Social solutions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:VineyardDrip.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2100" data-file-width="1500" height="308" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/VineyardDrip.JPG/220px-VineyardDrip.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/VineyardDrip.JPG/330px-VineyardDrip.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/VineyardDrip.JPG/440px-VineyardDrip.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:VineyardDrip.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Drip_irrigation" title="Drip irrigation">
         Drip irrigation
        </a>
        system in
        <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">
         New Mexico
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Water conservation programs involved in social solutions are typically initiated at the local level, by either municipal
      <a href="/wiki/Water_industry" title="Water industry">
       water utilities
      </a>
      or regional governments. Common strategies include public
      <a href="/wiki/Outreach" title="Outreach">
       outreach
      </a>
      campaigns,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      tiered water rates (charging progressively higher prices as water use increases), or restrictions on outdoor water use such as lawn watering and car washing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Cities in dry climates often require or encourage the installation of
      <a href="/wiki/Xeriscaping" title="Xeriscaping">
       xeriscaping
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Natural_landscaping" title="Natural landscaping">
       natural landscaping
      </a>
      in new homes to reduce outdoor water usage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      One fundamental conservation goal is universal metering. The prevalence of residential
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_meter" title="Water meter">
       water metering
      </a>
      varies significantly worldwide. Recent studies have estimated that water supplies are metered in less than 30% of UK households,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and about 61% of urban Canadian homes (as of 2001).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Although individual water meters have often been considered impractical in homes with private wells or in multifamily buildings, the U.S.
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">
       Environmental Protection Agency
      </a>
      estimates that metering alone can reduce consumption by 20 to 40 percent.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPA-Conserve_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-EPA-Conserve-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In addition to raising consumer awareness of their water use, metering is also an important way to identify and localize
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_leakage" title="Water leakage">
       water leakage
      </a>
      . Water metering would benefit society in the long run it is proven that water metering increases the efficiency of the entire water system, as well as help unnecessary expenses for individuals for years to come. One would be unable to waste water unless they are willing to pay the extra charges, this way the water department would be able to monitor water usage by public, domestic and manufacturing services.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some researchers have suggested that water conservation efforts should be primarily directed at farmers, in light of the fact that crop irrigation accounts for 70% of the world's fresh water use.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The agricultural sector of most countries is important both economically and politically, and water subsidies are common. Conservation advocates have urged removal of all subsidies to force
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farmers" title="Farmers">
       farmers
      </a>
      to grow more water-efficient crops and adopt less wasteful irrigation techniques.
     </p>
     <p>
      New technology poses a few new options for consumers, features such and full flush and half flush when using a toilet are trying to make a difference in water consumption and waste. Also available in our modern world is shower heads that help reduce wasting water, old shower heads are said to use 5-10 gallons per minute. All new fixtures available are said to use 2.5 gallons per minute and offer equal water coverage.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Household_applications">
       Household applications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Household applications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Home Water Works website contains useful information on household water conservation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Contrary to popular view, experts suggest the most efficient way is replacing toilets and retrofitting washers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Water-saving technology for the home includes:
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       Low-flow shower heads sometimes called energy-efficient shower heads as they also use less energy
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_flush_toilet" title="Low flush toilet">
        Low-flush toilets
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Composting_toilet" title="Composting toilet">
        composting toilets
       </a>
       . These have a dramatic impact in the developed world, as conventional Western
       <a href="/wiki/Toilet" title="Toilet">
        toilets
       </a>
       use large volumes of water
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Dual_flush_toilet" title="Dual flush toilet">
        Dual flush toilets
       </a>
       created by
       <a href="/wiki/Caroma" title="Caroma">
        Caroma
       </a>
       includes two buttons or handles to flush different levels of water. Dual flush toilets use up to 67% less water than conventional toilets
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Faucet_aerator" title="Faucet aerator">
        Faucet aerators
       </a>
       , which break water flow into fine droplets to maintain "wetting effectiveness" while using less water. An additional benefit is that they reduce splashing while washing hands and dishes
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raw_water_flushing" title="Raw water flushing">
        Raw water flushing
       </a>
       where toilets use sea water or non-purified water
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Reclaimed_water" title="Reclaimed water">
        Waste water reuse
       </a>
       or recycling systems, allowing:
       <ul>
        <li>
         Reuse of
         <a href="/wiki/Greywater" title="Greywater">
          graywater
         </a>
         for flushing toilets or watering gardens
        </li>
        <li>
         Recycling of wastewater through
         <a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">
          purification
         </a>
         at a water treatment plant.
         <i>
          See also
          <a href="/wiki/Wastewater#Reuse" title="Wastewater">
           Wastewater - Reuse
          </a>
         </i>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Rainwater_harvesting" title="Rainwater harvesting">
        Rainwater harvesting
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       High-efficiency
       <a href="/wiki/Washing_machine" title="Washing machine">
        clothes washers
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       Weather-based irrigation controllers
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Garden_hose" title="Garden hose">
        Garden hose
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Nozzle" title="Nozzle">
        nozzles
       </a>
       that shut off water when it is not being used, instead of letting a hose run.
      </li>
      <li>
       Low flow taps in wash basins
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Swimming_pool" title="Swimming pool">
        Swimming pool
       </a>
       covers that reduce evaporation and can warm pool water to reduce water, energy and chemical costs.
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Automatic_faucet" title="Automatic faucet">
        Automatic faucet
       </a>
       is a water conservation faucet that eliminates water waste at the faucet. It automates the use of faucets without the use of hands.
      </li>
     </ol>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_applications">
       Commercial applications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial applications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Many water-saving devices (such as low-flush toilets) that are useful in homes can also be useful for business water saving. Other water-saving technology for businesses includes:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterless_urinal" title="Waterless urinal">
        Waterless urinals
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterless_car_wash" title="Waterless car wash">
        Waterless car washes
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">
        Infrared
       </a>
       or foot-operated taps, which can save water by using short bursts of water for rinsing in a kitchen or bathroom
      </li>
      <li>
       Pressurized waterbrooms, which can be used instead of a hose to clean sidewalks
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Radiography" title="Radiography">
        X-ray
       </a>
       film processor re-circulation systems
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Cooling_tower" title="Cooling tower">
        Cooling tower
       </a>
       conductivity controllers
      </li>
      <li>
       Water-saving
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steam_sterilizer" title="Steam sterilizer">
        steam sterilizers
       </a>
       , for use in hospitals and health care facilities
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain_water_harvesting" title="Rain water harvesting">
        Rain water harvesting
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       Water to Water
       <a href="/wiki/Heat_exchanger" title="Heat exchanger">
        heat exchangers
       </a>
       .
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Agricultural_applications">
       Agricultural applications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Agricultural applications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:218px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="440" data-file-width="640" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg/216px-PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg/324px-PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg/432px-PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg 2x" width="216"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PivotIrrigationOnCotton.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Overhead irrigation,
        <a href="/wiki/Center_pivot_irrigation" title="Center pivot irrigation">
         center pivot
        </a>
        design
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      For crop irrigation, optimal water efficiency means minimizing losses due to
      <a href="/wiki/Evaporation" title="Evaporation">
       evaporation
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">
       runoff
      </a>
      or subsurface drainage while maximizing production. An
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evaporation_pan" title="Evaporation pan">
       evaporation pan
      </a>
      in combination with specific crop correction factors can be used to determine how much water is needed to satisfy plant requirements.
      <a href="/wiki/Surface_irrigation" title="Surface irrigation">
       Flood irrigation
      </a>
      , the oldest and most common type, is often very uneven in distribution, as parts of a field may receive excess water in order to deliver sufficient quantities to other parts.
      <a href="/wiki/Irrigation#Overhead_.28sprinkler.29_irrigation" title="Irrigation">
       Overhead irrigation
      </a>
      , using center-pivot or lateral-moving sprinklers, has the potential for a much more equal and controlled distribution pattern.
      <a href="/wiki/Drip_irrigation" title="Drip irrigation">
       Drip irrigation
      </a>
      is the most expensive and least-used type, but offers the ability to deliver water to plant roots with minimal losses. However, drip irrigation is increasingly affordable, especially for the home gardener and in light of rising water rates. There are also cheap effective methods similar to drip irrigation such as the use of soaking hoses that can even be submerged in the growing medium to eliminate evaporation.
     </p>
     <p>
      As changing irrigation systems can be a costly undertaking, conservation efforts often concentrate on maximizing the efficiency of the existing system. This may include chiseling compacted soils, creating furrow dikes to prevent runoff, and using soil moisture and rainfall sensors to optimize irrigation schedules.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPA-Conserve_12-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-EPA-Conserve-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Usually large gains in efficiency are possible through measurement and more effective management of the existing irrigation system. The 2011 UNEP Green Economy Report notes that "[i]mproved soil organic matter from the use of green manures, mulching, and recycling of crop residues and animal manure increases the water holding capacity of soils and their ability to absorb water during torrential rains",
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
      which is a way to optimize the use of rainfall and irrigation during dry periods in the season.
     </p>
