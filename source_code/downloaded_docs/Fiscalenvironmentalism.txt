     <p>
      <b>
       Fiscal Environmentalism
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hybrid" title="wiktionary:hybrid">
       hybrid term
      </a>
      of two traditional and often conflicting
      <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">
       philosophies
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">
       environmentalism
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_conservatism" title="Fiscal conservatism">
       fiscal conservatism
      </a>
      , created to emphasize the growing understanding of the middle ground between the two, where the goals of each are simultaneously fulfilled. The result is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_practice" title="Business practice">
       business practice
      </a>
      based upon principles of intelligent
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_design" title="Environmental design">
       environmental design
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Financial" title="Financial">
       financial
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Discipline" title="Discipline">
       discipline
      </a>
      , associated with each.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">
       Concept
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fiscal_environmentalism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Traditional practices of
      <a href="/wiki/Stewardship" title="Stewardship">
       environmental stewardship
      </a>
      (including
      <a href="/wiki/Conservation_(ethic)" title="Conservation (ethic)">
       preservation
      </a>
      , elimination of
      <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">
       waste
      </a>
      , and increased
      <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">
       energy efficiency
      </a>
      ), complement bottom-line-driven economic policy (including increased profitability, reduced waste, and organizational efficiency). As future economic growth will demand both a higher degree of environmental awareness and a stronger utilization of available investment monies, the hallmarks of an efficiently organized and profitable company, organization, government, or household stand to benefit from environmentalism’s
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holistic" title="Holistic">
       holistic
      </a>
      approach.
     </p>
     <p>
      Fiscal environmentalism is a useful term for individuals who are familiar with either of the philosophies, and is related to very general concepts such as "
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_business" title="Sustainable business">
       sustainable business
      </a>
      practices" and "socially responsible business practices", and other concepts more specific to traditional fields, such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological_Economics" title="Ecological Economics">
       Ecological Economics
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Management" title="Environmental Management">
       Environmental Management
      </a>
      Systems. Compared to these other terms, fiscal environmentalism emphasizes fiscal discipline. It is used in discussions with business leaders who are looking to answer public demand for increased environmental awareness while still focusing on bottom-line success.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_examples">
       Corporate examples
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fiscal_environmentalism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate examples">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Recent examples of fiscal environmentalism include the rise of
      <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">
       green
      </a>
      building practices among government and traditional businesses as an attempt to save costs in energy consumption and improve indoor air quality. Even retailer
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" title="Wal-Mart">
       Wal-Mart
      </a>
      , which has often been strongly criticized by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_groups" title="Environmental groups">
       environmental groups
      </a>
      for its environmental practices, has begun to embrace a fiscal environmentalist approach, if only to save money. It recently hired a new senior level Environmental Affairs officer. Wal-Mart has also recently made a monumental commitment to promoting energy-saving
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent" title="Compact fluorescent">
       compact fluorescent
      </a>
      light bulbs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      According to a global
      <a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">
       Newsweek
      </a>
      ranking, which measures how effectively the top 100 companies manage environmental risks and opportunities relative to their industry peers,
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Canada" title="Royal Bank of Canada">
       Royal Bank of Canada
      </a>
      is the most environmentally friendly company in the world.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsweek100_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Newsweek100-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Other companies in the top 5 include
      <a href="/wiki/Lafarge_(company)" title="Lafarge (company)">
       Lafarge Construction
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grupo_Ferrovial" title="Grupo Ferrovial">
       Grupo Ferrovial SA
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Westpac" title="Westpac">
       Westpac Banking Corp
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yell_Group" title="Yell Group">
       Yell Group PLC Advertising
      </a>
      .
