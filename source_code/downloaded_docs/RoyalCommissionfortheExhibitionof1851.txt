     <p>
      <b>
       Royal Commission for the Exhibition of 1851
      </b>
      is an institution founded in 1850 to administer the international exhibition of 1851, officially called the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Exhibition" title="Great Exhibition">
       Great Exhibition of the Works of Industry of all Nations
      </a>
      . The Great Exhibition was held in
      <a href="/wiki/The_Crystal_Palace" title="The Crystal Palace">
       The Crystal Palace
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       England
      </a>
      , the enormous building was designed by
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Paxton" title="Joseph Paxton">
       Joseph Paxton
      </a>
      for the Exhibition and construction was supervised by
      <a href="/wiki/William_Cubitt" title="William Cubitt">
       William Cubitt
      </a>
      using a
      <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">
       cast iron
      </a>
      space frame for the glass panes, with wooden beams for flooring.
     </p>
     <p>
      The founding President of the Commission was
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Albert_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">
       Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha
      </a>
      and its chief administrator was
      <a href="/wiki/Henry_Cole" title="Henry Cole">
       Henry Cole
      </a>
      . The current president is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HRH_The_Princess_Royal" title="HRH The Princess Royal">
       HRH The Princess Royal
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The exhibition was a great popular and financial success, and made a huge surplus of £186,000 (approximately £22m in today's money
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      ). An unusual decision was made to maintain the Royal Commission as a permanent administrative body to use the profits for charitable purposes. Its revised Charter charged the Commission with "increasing the means of industrial education and extending the influence of science and art upon productive industry".
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="South_Kensington">
       South Kensington
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Commission_for_the_Exhibition_of_1851&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: South Kensington">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The profit from the 1851 Exhibition was invested by The Commissioners who bought 86 acres (350,000 m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) of land in
      <a href="/wiki/South_Kensington" title="South Kensington">
       South Kensington
      </a>
      that was developed as a centre of educational and cultural institutions, often known as "
      <a href="/wiki/Albertopolis" title="Albertopolis">
       Albertopolis
      </a>
      ". These include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_College,_London" title="Imperial College, London">
        Imperial College
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_History_Museum,_London" title="Natural History Museum, London">
        Natural History Museum
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">
        Royal Albert Hall
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Art" title="Royal College of Art">
        Royal College of Art
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music" title="Royal College of Music">
        Royal College of Music
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Science_Museum_(London)" title="Science Museum (London)">
        Science Museum
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">
        Victoria and Albert Museum
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The Commission's headquarters are in Imperial College and since 1891 the role of the Commission has been to provide postgraduate scholarships for students to study in Britain and abroad, and former scholars include 13
      <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">
       Nobel Prize
      </a>
      laureates.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Commission currently has capital assets of over £76 million, with an annual charitable disbursement of over £2 million.
     </p>
