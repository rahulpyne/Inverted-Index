     <p>
      <b>
       Babatunde Raji Fashola
      </b>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Senior_Advocate_of_Nigeria" title="Senior Advocate of Nigeria">
       SAN
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Yoruba_language" title="Yoruba language">
       Yoruba
      </a>
      :
      <span lang="yo" xml:lang="yo">
       <i>
        Babátúndé Rájí Fáshọlá
       </i>
      </span>
      ; born 28 June 1963) is Nigeria's Federal Minister of Power, Works and Housing and was
      <a href="/wiki/Lagos_State_Governor" title="Lagos State Governor">
       Lagos State Governor
      </a>
      from May 29, 2007 to May 29, 2015.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baba_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Baba-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      As a candidate of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_Congress" title="Action Congress">
       Action Congress
      </a>
      party, now known as the
      <a href="/wiki/All_Progressives_Congress" title="All Progressives Congress">
       All Progressives Congress
      </a>
      , Fashola succeeded
      <a href="/wiki/Bola_Tinubu" title="Bola Tinubu">
       Bola Ahmed Tinubu
      </a>
      , on 14 April 2007,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baba_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Baba-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was sworn in on 29 May 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baba_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-Baba-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was re-elected on 26 April 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tribune20110428_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tribune20110428-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      On November 11th 2015, he was appointed by President Muhammadu Buhari to be the Minister of Power, Works and Housing.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">
       Personal life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Babatunde Raji Fashola of
      <a href="/wiki/Yoruba_people" title="Yoruba people">
       Yoruba
      </a>
      origin,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Lagos" title="Lagos">
       Lagos
      </a>
      on 28 June 1963. He attended Birch Freeman High school Lagos and
      <a href="/wiki/Igbobi_College" title="Igbobi College">
       Igbobi College
      </a>
      Lagos. He studied Law at the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Benin_(Nigeria)" title="University of Benin (Nigeria)">
       University of Benin
      </a>
      from where he graduated with a Bachelor of Laws, LL.B.(Hon), degree in 1987.
     </p>
     <p>
      He is married to Mrs. Abimbola Emmanuela Fashola and they have children.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_career">
       Legal career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legal career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      He was called to the Nigerian Bar as a solicitor and advocate of the
      <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Nigeria" title="Supreme Court of Nigeria">
       Supreme Court of Nigeria
      </a>
      in November 1988 after completing the professional training programme at the
      <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Law_School" title="Nigerian Law School">
       Nigerian Law School
      </a>
      , Lagos which he undertook between 1987 and 1988. His legal career of over one and a half decades, commenced in the law Firm of Sofunde, Osakwe, Ogundipe and Belgore, where he cut his legal teeth as a litigator over such wide-ranging areas of specialisation as, intellectual property (registration of trade marks), commercial law, covering general contracts, company activities, mergers, acquisitions, right issues, ownership of shares and equity of corporations, as well as land disputes, criminal law and chieftaincy matters, in all of which he has come to acquire appreciable expertise and vast experience.
     </p>
     <p>
      Fashola, a Notary Public of the Supreme Court of Nigeria, has been variously honoured with awards and certificates of merit including the Distinguished Alumnus Award conferred on him by the University of Benin Alumni Association in recognition of contributions to the Alumnus association and humanity. He is also a recipient of Lagos State public service club Platinum Award for outstanding contribution towards development. As well as Alliance for Democracy " Igbogbo Bayeku Local Government Award" in recognition of activities towards the success of the party.
     </p>
     <p>
      Babatunde Fashola is also a Patron of the Law Students Association of the University of Benin and he is the second law graduate from the University of Benin and the first member of the Nigerian Law School graduating class of 1988 to be conferred with the professional rank of
      <a href="/wiki/Senior_Advocate_of_Nigeria" title="Senior Advocate of Nigeria">
       Senior Advocate of Nigeria
      </a>
      . He is also the First ever Chief of Staff to be so honoured. He is a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Bar_Association" title="Nigerian Bar Association">
       Nigerian Bar Association
      </a>
      , the International Bar Association and an Associate of the
      <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Institute_of_Taxation_of_Nigeria" title="Chartered Institute of Taxation of Nigeria">
       Chartered Institute of Taxation of Nigeria
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Governor_of_Lagos_state">
       Governor of Lagos state
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governor of Lagos state">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Babatunde Raji Fashola commenced a four-year tenure as the Executive Governor of
      <a href="/wiki/Lagos_State" title="Lagos State">
       Lagos State
      </a>
      in Nigeria on 29 May 2007. In June 2007, Babatunde Fashola appointed former Inspector General of Police
      <a href="/wiki/Musiliu_Smith" title="Musiliu Smith">
       Musiliu Smith
      </a>
      head of the Lagos State Security council, a body charged with taking a holistic look at the anatomy of crime in the state.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Babatunde Fashola Good Governance Group (G3) movement is a good example of Fashola's commitment to reaching out to the people using various media channels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxation">
       Taxation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Taxation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      One of the main source of Lagos State government' funds are received as tax payers' money. Recently, a controversial proposal to charge
      <a href="/wiki/Consumption_tax" title="Consumption tax">
       consumption tax
      </a>
      on eateries had been enforced.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The matter was taken to court, but the ruling was announced in the government's favour.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Education_reforms">
       Education reforms
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education reforms">

     </h3>

     <p>
      Babatunde Fashola vowed to rehabilitate the state's public schools, long being neglected with time. Among the schools being habilitated included the governor's alma mater,
      <b>
       Birch Freeman High School,
      </b>
      which is located at a busy metropolis of
      <a href="/wiki/Surulere" title="Surulere">
       Surulere
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Eko_Mega_City_Project">
       The Eko Mega City Project
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Eko Mega City Project">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      See also:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eko_Atlantic_City" title="Eko Atlantic City">
       Eko Atlantic City
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The biggest task Babatunde Raji Fashola faced in office as Governor of Lagos State is his visionary project to transform Lagos into a mega city,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the first of its kind in Lagosian history. Although initialised under the former governor, Bola Ahmed Tinubu, in June 2002,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alausa_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-alausa-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the mega project task accelerated under Fashola's governorship. Both private and public sectors are involved in fulfilling the project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alausa_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-alausa-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg/220px-New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg/330px-New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg/440px-New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:New_toll_gate_and_roads_in_Lagos.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Newly added toll gates and roads at the Lekki-Ẹpẹ Expressway.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The crucial part of the mega city project is the rehabilitation of Lagos' infrastructures and districts. Formerly taken care of by the federal government, they were immediately neglected, as
      <a href="/wiki/Abuja" title="Abuja">
       Abuja
      </a>
      was declared Nigeria's new capital since 1991.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alausa_10-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-alausa-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      According to
      <a href="/wiki/Lagos_Rail_Mass_Transit" title="Lagos Rail Mass Transit">
       Lagos Rail Mass Transit
      </a>
      , proposed light railways will be consisted of the outskirt blue-line and inner red-line destinations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      As part of the project, the Badagry Expressway, which links Lagos directly to the south-eastern border of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benin_Republic" title="Benin Republic">
       Benin Republic
      </a>
      , is currently being rehabilitated by various contractors,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
      with
      <a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">
       BRT
      </a>
      and railway networks being established.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LagosRail_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LagosRail-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Another ambition is to recover lands at Victoria Island, being lost to erosion for decades.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This artificial terrain is the location of the proposed
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eko_Atlantic_City" title="Eko Atlantic City">
       Eko Atlantic City
      </a>
      . This is also to support housing project that would accommodate up to 25 million Lagosians by 2015, a population growth from 15 million that could turn Lagos into the world's third largest city.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG/220px-Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG/330px-Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG/440px-Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ayodele_Awojobi_Memorial_Park.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Ayodele_Awojobi" title="Ayodele Awojobi">
         Ayodele Awojobi
        </a>
        Memorial Park, Onike Round-about, Yaba, Lagos.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In October 2009, Babatunde Fashola posthumously dedicated a garden personally, in honour of the memory of late professor
      <a href="/wiki/Ayodele_Awojobi" title="Ayodele Awojobi">
       Ayodele Awojobi
      </a>
      at Onike Roundabout, Yaba, Lagos, with a statue of the famous academic erected at the centre.Artists of repute such as Oladejo Victor Akinlonu has commended the Governor on a job well done on the various value enhancement and beautification of Lagos state been a pioneer in the beautification of the state since the 80's Oladejo enjoined artists to work with the Governor.Oladejo's works such as the eyo masquerade and the Bust of
      <a href="/wiki/Molade_Okoya-Thomas" title="Molade Okoya-Thomas">
       Sir Alexander Molade Okoya Thomas
      </a>
      where commissioned by the Governor.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-compassded_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-compassded-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Haiti_earthquake_fund-raising">
       Haiti earthquake fund-raising

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Haiti earthquake fund-raising">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]

      <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">

As this was in place, the Speaker of the Lagos State House of Assembly, Mr. Adeyemi Ikuforiji, said Nigerians should be prepared in events of natural disasters.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Water_safety">
       Water safety
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Water safety">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In May 2014, Fashola announced the compulsory use of
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_flotation_device" title="Personal flotation device">
       life jackets
      </a>
      on Lagos waterways.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a 2012 accident, ten children from Ijora Awori, Lagos State, drowned while they were being ferried to school, because they were not wearing life jackets.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Political_limbo">
       Political limbo
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Babatunde_Fashola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Political limbo">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      By December 2009, there had been rumours and speculation by news media that Babatunde Fashola was subjected to a possible impeachment from within, primarily because of disagreements with the former governor. Ironically, a similar situation played itself out between Tinubu and
      <a href="/wiki/Femi_Pedro" title="Femi Pedro">
       Femi Pedro
      </a>
      , Tinubu's former Deputy Governor, in the run-up to the gubernatorial elections in 2007. It is believed that there was a dispute over Fashola's 2.1 billion Naira worth of commission with the former governor,
      <a href="/wiki/Bola_Tinubu" title="Bola Tinubu">
       Bola Ahmed Tinubu
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      although both Fashola and the
      <a href="/wiki/Lagos_State_House_of_Assembly" title="Lagos State House of Assembly">
       Lagos State House of Assembly
      </a>
      had earlier denied such plot ever took place.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      But in late January 2010, the Lagos State House of Assembly launched an investigation into possible mishandling of tax payers' money by Fashola, mainly on the ongoing mega city project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LagosGoSpoil_24-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-LagosGoSpoil-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The accusations range from importing of palm trees from
      <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">
       Niger
      </a>
      , for gardening projects within a rain forest state, to improper usage of contract money involving one of Lagos State's contractors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The group carrying out the investigation was known as the "True Face of Lagos". The final reports were due to be submitted on 15 February 2010,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LagosGoSpoil_24-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-LagosGoSpoil-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the True Face of Lagos was later abolished. There were attempts to re-launch the probes through a petition,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
      but they were again being turned down by the court.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      THE Action Congress of Nigeria (ACN) finally ended months of speculation over the second term ambition of the Lagos State Governor Babatunde Raji Fashola (SAN), when it endorsed him as the party's sole candidate for the 2011 governorship election.
     </p>
     <p>
      On Wednesday, at the party's secretariat, in Ikeja Lagos, Fashola's predecessor, Alhaji Bola Ahmed Tinubu who people perceived to be at the centre of the uncertainty over the political future of Fashola announced the decision of the party.
     </p>
     <p>
      Before thousands of the party supporters, Tinubu, who flew into Lagos from Abuja because of the emergency stakeholders' meeting raised Babatunde Fashola's hand and said: “Today, by the power conferred on me by the ACN, in consultation and in agreement with all our party leaders throughout the state and at the national level, I am announcing to you, the final endorsement of Raji Babatunde Fashola for the 2011 election.”
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      He was re-elected on 26 April 2011 by a massive landslide victory. In Lagos, South-West Nigeria and Nigeria as a whole, Babatunde Raji Fashola remains a reference point in 21st century Governance.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tribune20110428_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tribune20110428-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
