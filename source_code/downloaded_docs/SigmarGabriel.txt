     <p>
      <b>
       Sigmar Gabriel
      </b>
      (born 12 September 1959) is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">
       German
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Germany" title="Politics of Germany">
       politician
      </a>
      who serves as the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Economic_Affairs_and_Energy_(Germany)" title="Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (Germany)">
       Minister for Economic Affairs and Energy
      </a>
      and since 2013 as the 17th
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice_Chancellor_of_Germany" title="Vice Chancellor of Germany">
       Vice Chancellor of Germany
      </a>
      . Since 2009 he has been
      <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">
       chairman
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">
       Social Democratic Party of Germany
      </a>
      (SPD). He was the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Environment,_Nature_Conservation_and_Nuclear_Safety" title="Federal Ministry for Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety">
       Minister of the Environment
      </a>
      from 2005 to 2009. From 1999 to 2003 Gabriel was the 9th
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister-President_of_Lower_Saxony" title="Minister-President of Lower Saxony">
       Minister President of Lower Saxony
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      He is noted for being from the liberal wing of the SPD, which shares many similarities with Tony Blair's New Labour and Scandinavian social democrat parties.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">
       Early life and education
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Gabriel was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Goslar" title="Goslar">
       Goslar
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">
       West Germany
      </a>
      , the son of divorced parents, a public worker and a nurse. According to his own account, his father Walter was a fanatic Nazi "until his dying breath."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Sigmar Gabriel attended the public schools of Goslar, and completed two years of service in the Army.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He attended the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen" title="University of Göttingen">
       University of Göttingen
      </a>
      and was educated as a grammar school teacher specializing in German, politics and sociology.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">
       Political career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Minister-President_of_Lower_Saxony">
       Minister-President of Lower Saxony
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minister-President of Lower Saxony">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On 15 December 1999, after the resignation of
      <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Glogowski" title="Gerhard Glogowski">
       Gerhard Glogowski
      </a>
      , who had succeeded
      <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Schr%C3%B6der" title="Gerhard Schröder">
       Gerhard Schröder
      </a>
      in office, Gabriel became
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister-President_of_Lower_Saxony" title="Minister-President of Lower Saxony">
       Minister-President of Lower Saxony
      </a>
      and served until 4 March 2003. During these years, he was widely presented as a protégé of Schröder, and even as a possible successor.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After being voted out of office in 2003, Gabriel became the SPD's "Representative for Pop Culture and Pop Discourse" from 2003 to 2005, for which he was bestowed the nickname
      <i>
       Siggi Pop
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zeitde2010-02_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-zeitde2010-02-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Minister_for_the_Environment.2C_Nature_Conservation_and_Nuclear_Safety.2C_2005.E2.80.932009">
       Federal Minister for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety, 2005–2009
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Federal Minister for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety, 2005–2009">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      From 2005 to 2009 Gabriel was the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Environment,_Nature_Conservation_and_Nuclear_Safety_(Germany)" title="Federal Ministry for Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety (Germany)">
       Federal Minister for the Environment, Nature Conservation and Nuclear Safety
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/First_Merkel_cabinet" title="First Merkel cabinet">
       first cabinet
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">
       Angela Merkel
      </a>
      (CDU).
     </p>
     <p>
      During his time in office, Gabriel promoted the
      <a href="/wiki/International_Renewable_Energy_Agency" title="International Renewable Energy Agency">
       International Renewable Energy Agency
      </a>
      . He also led the German delegation to the
      <a href="/wiki/2006_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2006 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2006 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">
       Nairobi
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2007, when Germany held the
      <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_the_Council_of_the_European_Union" title="Presidency of the Council of the European Union">
       presidency of the Council of the European Union
      </a>
      , he led the negotiations between European Union environment ministers on an ambitious effort to cut
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" title="Greenhouse gases">
       greenhouse gas emissions
      </a>
      to 20 percent below 1990 levels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      That same year, he accompanied Merkel on a two-day visit to
      <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">
       Greenland
      </a>
      to see the
      <a href="/wiki/Ilulissat_Icefjord" title="Ilulissat Icefjord">
       Ilulissat Icefjord
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">
       UNESCO world heritage site
      </a>
      , and the
      <a href="/wiki/Jakobshavn_Glacier" title="Jakobshavn Glacier">
       Sermeq Kujalleq
      </a>
      glacier in order to get a firsthand look at the effects of global warming.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Opposition_leader.2C_2009.E2.80.932013">
       Opposition leader, 2009–2013
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Opposition leader, 2009–2013">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Following the SPD's defeat in the
      <a href="/wiki/German_federal_election,_2009" title="German federal election, 2009">
       federal election of 2009
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Franz_M%C3%BCntefering" title="Franz Müntefering">
       Franz Müntefering
      </a>
      resigned from the position of party chairman of the Social Democratic Party. Gabriel was nominated as his successor and was elected on 13 November 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was re-elected as party chairman for a further two years at the SPD party conference in Berlin on 5 December 2011, receiving 91.6 percent of the vote.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During his early years as chairman, Gabriel pushed through internal party reforms. He abolished the party steering committee in favor of an expanded executive committee and led the regular party conventions, the most important meetings for the party.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He also played a critical role in founding the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progressive_Alliance_(political_international)" title="Progressive Alliance (political international)">
       Progressive Alliance
      </a>
      in 2013 by cancelling the SPD payment of its £100,000 yearly membership fee to the
      <a href="/wiki/Socialist_International" title="Socialist International">
       Socialist International
      </a>
      in January 2012. Gabriel had been critical of the Socialist International's admittance and continuing inclusion of undemocratic "despotic" political movements into the organization.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      For the
      <a href="/wiki/German_federal_election,_2013" title="German federal election, 2013">
       2013 federal election
      </a>
      , Gabriel was considered a possible candidate to challenge incumbent Chancellor
      <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">
       Angela Merkel
      </a>
      but deemed too “unpopular and undisciplined” at the time.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      As a consequence, he and the other members of the party’s leadership agreed to nominate
      <a href="/wiki/Peer_Steinbr%C3%BCck" title="Peer Steinbrück">
       Peer Steinbrück
      </a>
      after
      <a href="/wiki/Frank-Walter_Steinmeier" title="Frank-Walter Steinmeier">
       Frank-Walter Steinmeier
      </a>
      , the party’s parliamentary leader, withdrew from the contest.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      During the election campaign, Gabriel became the first SPD leader to address a party convention of
      <a href="/wiki/Alliance_%2790/The_Greens" title="Alliance '90/The Greens">
       Alliance '90/The Greens
      </a>
      ; in his speech, he called for a
      <a href="/wiki/Red%E2%80%93green_alliance" title="Red–green alliance">
       red–green alliance
      </a>
      to defeat Merkel in the elections.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Vice-Chancellor_and_Federal_Minister_of_Economic_Affairs_and_Energy.2C_2013.E2.80.93present">
       Vice-Chancellor and Federal Minister of Economic Affairs and Energy, 2013–present
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Vice-Chancellor and Federal Minister of Economic Affairs and Energy, 2013–present">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Angela_Merkel,_Sigmar_Gabriel,_Frank-Walter_Steinmeier,_Christian_Schmidt,_Ursula_von_der_Leyen_(Tobias_Koch).jpg">

        </div>
        Sigmar Gabriel with
        <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">

        <a href="/wiki/Frank-Walter_Steinmeier" title="Frank-Walter Steinmeier">

     </div>
     <p>
      In 2013, Gabriel turned the Social Democrats’ third successive defeat to
      <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">
       Angela Merkel
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/German_federal_election,_2013" title="German federal election, 2013">
       federal election
      </a>
      into a share of government, after successfully navigating the three-month process of coalition negotiations and a ballot of about 475,000 party members, who endorsed the accord.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg.com_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-bloomberg.com-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the time, he was widely considered to have negotiated skillfully, particularly considering the relative weakness of his party, which had received just over 25 percent of the vote in the elections, against more than 41 percent for Merkel’s conservative bloc.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      At an SPD convention shortly after the elections, however, Gabriel and the other members of the party’s leadership were punished by delegates who re-elected them to their posts with reduced majorities; he received 83.6 percent of members’ ballots after 91.6 percent at the previous vote in 2011.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Gabriel, who serves as vice-chancellor in the
      <a href="/wiki/Third_Merkel_cabinet" title="Third Merkel cabinet">
       third Merkel cabinet
      </a>
      , took on responsibility for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_transition_in_Germany" title="Energy transition in Germany">
       Germany’s energy overhaul
      </a>
      as part of a newly configured Economy Ministry.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg.com_17-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-bloomberg.com-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Together with his French counterpart
      <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron" title="Emmanuel Macron">
       Emmanuel Macron
      </a>
      , he presented a joint proposal in 2015 to set up a common
      <a href="/wiki/Eurozone" title="Eurozone">
       eurozone
      </a>
      budget.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Speculation about Gabriel's future as leader of the SPD has been brewing since he registered just 74 percent
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
      in a party delegates' vote of confidence in December 2015 – the lowest for an SPD leader in 20 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Still, he is widely seen as running in the
      <a href="/wiki/German_federal_election,_2017" title="German federal election, 2017">
       2017 federal elections
      </a>
      given the lack of clear rivals and apparent reluctance among top SPD officials to take on what is viewed as an unenviable position.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In May 2016, Gabriel invited others to put themselves forward so that party members can choose their candidate.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_policy">
       Foreign policy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sigmar_Gabriel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Foreign policy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:President_Rouhani_meeting_German_Economy_Minister_Sigmar_Gabriel_in_Saadabad_Palace_139404291812348335724664.jpg">

       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">

         </a>
        </div>
        Gabriel meets Iranian President
        <a href="/wiki/Hassan_Rouhani" title="Hassan Rouhani">
         Hassan Rouhani
        </a>
        , 16 July 2015
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Gabriel has been staunchly against German soldiers remaining in
      <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">
       Afghanistan
      </a>
      . In 2010, he called for an independent assessment that would determine whether the U.S.
      <a href="/wiki/Counterinsurgency_in_Northern_Afghanistan" title="Counterinsurgency in Northern Afghanistan">
       counter-insurgency strategy
      </a>
      would succeed.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, he voted in favor of extending German participation in the
      <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">
       NATO
      </a>
      -led security mission
      <a href="/wiki/International_Security_Assistance_Force" title="International Security Assistance Force">
       ISAF
      </a>
      in 2009, 2010, 2011 and 2012.
     </p>
     <p>
      On the occasion of the sixtieth anniversary of the founding of the State of Israel, Gabriel participated in the first joint cabinet meeting of the governments of Germany and Israel in Jerusalem in March 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2012, after having visited
      <a href="/wiki/Hebron" title="Hebron">
       Hebron
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">
       Palestinian territories
      </a>
      , he called Israel an "
      <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">
       Apartheid Regime
      </a>
      ".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In one of the strongest comments by Germany to push for a federal solution for
      <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">
       Ukraine
      </a>
      , Gabriel told German weekly
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Welt_am_Sonntag" title="Welt am Sonntag">
        Welt am Sonntag
       </a>
      </i>
      in August 2014 that a federal structure was the only option to resolve
      <a href="/wiki/2014_pro-Russian_unrest_in_Ukraine" title="2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine">
       pro-Russian unrest
      </a>
      in the country. He added that Germany’s priority was to prevent direct conflict between Russia and its southern neighbour.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Commenting on the

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Financial_transaction_tax" title="Financial transaction tax">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Investment_banking" title="Investment banking">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Karel_De_Gucht" title="Karel De Gucht">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        [38]
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
       <a href="#cite_note-39">
        [39]
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        [40]
       </a>
      </sup>

       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        [41]
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Investor-state_dispute_settlement" title="Investor-state dispute settlement">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_Economic_and_Trade_Agreement" title="Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement">

      <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
       Canada
      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
       <a href="#cite_note-42">
        [42]
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        [43]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Piketty" title="Thomas Piketty">
       Thomas Piketty
      </a>
      , whose best-selling work
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Capital_in_the_Twenty-First_Century" title="Capital in the Twenty-First Century">
        Capital in the Twenty-First Century
       </a>
      </i>
      calls for a
      <a href="/wiki/Wealth_tax" title="Wealth tax">
       wealth tax
      </a>
      , Gabriel rejected such a
      <a href="/wiki/Progressive_tax" title="Progressive tax">
       progressive
      </a>
      levy on capital as “crazy” for business. He also argued that a wealth tax would generate no more than 8 billion euros ($9.9 billion) a year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
       <a href="#cite_note-44">
        [44]
       </a>
      </sup>

     <p>
      Following the

      <a href="/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency" title="International Atomic Energy Agency">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
       <a href="#cite_note-45">
        [45]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">

      <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">
       renewable sources
      </a>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Handelsblatt" title="Handelsblatt">

       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
       <a href="#cite_note-46">
        [46]
       </a>
      </sup>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Arms_exports">
       Arms exports
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Early in his tenure as Federal Minister of Economic Affairs and Energy, Gabriel vowed a much more cautious approach to licensing arms exports, unnerving the sizeable defense industry and signaling a change in policy from the
      <a href="/wiki/Second_Merkel_cabinet" title="Second Merkel cabinet">
       previous coalition government
      </a>
      under which sales rose.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
       <a href="#cite_note-48">
        [48]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In August 2014, he withdrew permission for
      <a href="/wiki/Rheinmetall" title="Rheinmetall">
       Rheinmetall
      </a>
      to build a military training center east of Moscow.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
       <a href="#cite_note-49">
        [49]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Gabriel is bound by pledges to his SPD to reduce arms sales to states that abuse human rights and the rule of law or where such sales may contribute to political instability. He has stated that controls over the final destination of small arms sold to such nations are still insufficient.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">
       <a href="#cite_note-50">
        [50]
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, he also indicated that the government would not universally block deals with countries outside of Germany's traditional alliances. Deals with such countries could be approved because of "special foreign-policy or security interests."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">
       <a href="#cite_note-51">
        [51]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He suggested that in future the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Office_(Germany)" title="Foreign Office (Germany)">
       Federal Foreign Office
      </a>
      may be a more appropriate body for deciding whether to allow exports, and called for common European arms exports.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52">
       <a href="#cite_note-52">
        [52]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In late 2015, his ministry approved a merger of German tank maker
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krauss-Maffei_Wegmann" title="Krauss-Maffei Wegmann">
       Krauss-Maffei Wegmann
      </a>
      (KMW) with the French armoured vehicle maker
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexter" title="Nexter">
       Nexter
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">
       <a href="#cite_note-53">
        [53]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
