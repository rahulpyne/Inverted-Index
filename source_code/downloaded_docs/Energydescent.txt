     <p>
      <b>
       Energy descent
      </b>
      is the post-
      <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">
       peak oil
      </a>
      transitional phase, when humankind is predicted to move from the ascending use of energy that has occurred since the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" title="Industrial revolution">
       industrial revolution
      </a>
      to a descending use of energy.
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">
       Summary
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_descent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Energy descent refers to retraction of oil use after the
      <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">
       peak oil
      </a>
      availability. Planning and preparing for this
      <a href="/wiki/Peak_oil" title="Peak oil">
       peak oil
      </a>
      energy descent period has been recently promoted by
      <a href="/wiki/David_Holmgren" title="David Holmgren">
       David Holmgren
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rob_Hopkins" title="Rob Hopkins">
       Rob Hopkins
      </a>
      of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transition_Towns" title="Transition Towns">
       Transition Towns
      </a>
      movement, and
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Heinberg" title="Richard Heinberg">
       Richard Heinberg
      </a>
      in the 2004 book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Power_Down:_Options_and_Actions_for_a_Post-Carbon_World" title="Power Down: Options and Actions for a Post-Carbon World">
        Power down
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      That oil reserves are dwindling is now becoming acknowledged more widely, especially after the International Energy Agency released the 2008 World Energy Outlook report.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Between 2007 and 2008 the IEA changed its figures for projected rate of decline in world energy supply from 3.7% a year (2007) to a projected rate of decline of 6.7% a year (2008).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2008 several major companies including Arup, Yahoo and Virgin created the UK Industry Taskforce on Peak Oil and Energy Security (ITPOES) and released a report, The Oil Crunch,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oil_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Oil-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      which calls for 'collaborative contingency planning' by government and industry in the face of dwindling oil reserves.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oil_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Oil-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      An Energy Descent Action Plan (EDAP) – is a local plan for planning and preparing for energy descent. It goes well beyond issues of energy supply, to look at across-the-board creative adaptations in the realms of health, education, economy and much more. Energy Descent Planning is a process developed by the Transition Towns Movement.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">
       Criticism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_descent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criticism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Some techno-optimists, such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julian_Lincoln_Simon" title="Julian Lincoln Simon">
       Julian Simon
      </a>
      , have disputed energy projections such as this, arguing that as oil becomes more expensive, humanity will tend to diversify its energy sources away from a reliance on oil, thus avoiding undesired global reductions in energy usage.
     </p>
