     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Moura Photovoltaic Power Station
      </b>
      (also known as
      <b>
       Amareleja Photovoltaic Power Station
      </b>
      ) is a large
      <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_power_station" title="Photovoltaic power station">
       photovoltaic power station
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Amareleja" title="Amareleja">
       Amareleja
      </a>
      , in the municipality of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moura_Municipality" title="Moura Municipality">
       Moura
      </a>
      , Portugal. It is one of the largest power stations of its kind, and is built in one of the sunniest regions in Europe.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IPS_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-IPS-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Its construction involved two stages: stage 1 was completed in 2008 after 13 months, and stage 2 was completed in 2010. The entire project topped a total cost of
      <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">
       €
      </a>
      250 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Stage 2 of the project involved the construction of a further 20 MW of solar panels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-photon_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-photon-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It will occupy an area of 618 acres (250 ha), and will be capable of producing 93
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GWh" title="GWh">
       GWh
      </a>
      of electrical energy annually
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-acciona_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-acciona-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      (10 MW average - equivalent to the electricity consumption of 15,000 Europeans).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-min-economia_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-min-economia-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The power station has an installed capacity of 62
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watt-peak" title="Watt-peak">
       MWp
      </a>
      , with more than 376,000
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_panel" title="Solar panel">
       solar panels
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-min-economia_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-min-economia-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Approximately 190,000 panels (32 MW) are fitted on fixed structures, and 52,000 panels (10 MW) are fixed on
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_tracker" title="Solar tracker">
       single-axis trackers
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      A €7.6 million solar panel factory, located in Moura, was constructed by
      <a href="/wiki/Acciona" title="Acciona">
       Acciona
      </a>
      , which provided panels for Stage 2 of the station construction. Its future production will be targeted at the international market, with a capacity of producing 24 MW of solar panels annually.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
