     <p>
      <b>
       Carbon finance
      </b>
      is a new branch of
      <a href="/wiki/Environmental_finance" title="Environmental finance">
       Environmental finance
      </a>
      . Carbon finance explores the financial implications of living in a carbon-constrained world, a world in which emissions of carbon dioxide and other
      <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">
       greenhouse gases
      </a>
      (GHGs) carry a price.
     </p>
     <p>
      Financial risks and opportunities impact corporate balance sheets, and market-based instruments are capable of transferring environmental risk and achieving environmental objectives. Issues regarding climate change and GHG emissions must be addressed as part of strategic management decision-making.
     </p>
     <p>
      The general term is applied to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investments" title="Investments">
       investments
      </a>
      in GHG emission reduction projects and the creation (origination) of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Financial_instruments" title="Financial instruments">
       financial instruments
      </a>
      that are tradeable on the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_market" title="Carbon market">
       carbon market
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_Implementation_and_Clean_Development_Mechanism">
       Joint Implementation and Clean Development Mechanism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_finance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Joint Implementation and Clean Development Mechanism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Clean_Development_Mechanism" title="Clean Development Mechanism">
       Clean Development Mechanism
      </a>
      (CDM), is recognised through the
      <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">
       Kyoto Protocol
      </a>
      , allowing the offset of emissions in developed countries by the investment in emission reduction projects in developing countries like
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
       Latin America
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Joint_Implementation" title="Joint Implementation">
       Joint Implementation
      </a>
      (JI), is another mechanism, allowing investments in developed countries to generate emission credit for the same or another developed country.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Market_value">
       Market value
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_finance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Market value">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The market for the purchase of carbon has grown exponentially since its conception in 1996.
     </p>
     <p>
      The following is the estimated size of the worldwide
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_market" title="Carbon market">
       carbon market
      </a>
      according to the
      <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">
       World Bank
      </a>
      :
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Volume (millions metric tonnes, MtCO2)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       2005: 718 (330 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 388 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
      <li>
       2006: 1,745 (1,134 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 611 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
      <li>
       2007: 2,983 (2,109 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 874 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Dollars (millions of USD)
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       2005: 10,908 (7,971 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 2,937 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
      <li>
       2006: 31,235 (24,699 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 6,536 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
      <li>
       2007: 64,035 (50,394 in Main Allowances Markets &amp; 13,641 in Project based transactions)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="World_Bank">
       World Bank
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_finance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World Bank">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">
       World Bank
      </a>
      has created the World Bank Carbon Finance Unit (CFU). The World Bank CFU uses money contributed by governments and companies in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">
       OECD
      </a>
      countries to purchase project-based
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emission" title="Greenhouse gas emission">
       greenhouse gas emission
      </a>
      reductions in developing countries and countries with economies in transition. The emission reductions are purchased through one of the CFU's carbon funds on behalf of the contributor, and within the framework of the Kyoto Protocol's Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) or Joint Implementation (JI).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The World Bank is particularly supportive of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Program_of_Activities_(PoA)" title="Program of Activities (PoA)">
       Program of Activities (PoA)
      </a>
      development.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
