     <p>
      <b>
       Post-Kyoto negotiations
      </b>
      refers to high level talks attempting to address
      <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">
       global warming
      </a>
      by limiting
      <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">
       greenhouse gas
      </a>
      emissions. Generally part of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">
       United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
      </a>
      (UNFCCC), these talks concern the period after the first "commitment period" of the
      <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">
       Kyoto Protocol
      </a>
      , which expired at the end of 2012. Negotiations have been mandated by the adoption of the
      <a href="/wiki/Bali_Road_Map" title="Bali Road Map">
       Bali Road Map
      </a>
      and Decision 1/CP.13 ("The Bali Action Plan").
     </p>
     <p>
      UNFCCC negotiations are conducted within two subsidiary bodies, the Ad Hoc Working Group on Long-term Cooperative Action under the Convention (AWG-LCA) and the Ad Hoc Working Group on Further Commitments for Annex I Parties under the Kyoto Protocol (AWG-KP) and were expected to culminate in the United Nations Climate Change Conference taking place in December 2009 in Copenhagen (
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/COP-15" title="COP-15">
       COP-15
      </a>
      ); negotiations are supported by a number of external processes, including the G8 process, a number of regional meetings and the Major Economies Forum on Energy and Climate that was launched by US
      <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Barack_Obama" title="Presidency of Barack Obama">
       President Barack Obama
      </a>
      in March 2009. High level talks were held at the meeting of the
      <a href="/wiki/G8%2B5" title="G8+5">
       G8+5 Climate Change Dialogue
      </a>
      in February 2007
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC070828_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC070828-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and at a number of subsequent G8 meetings, most recently leading to the adoption of the G8 leaders declaration "Responsible Leadership for a Sustainable Future" during the G8 summit in L´Aquila, Italy, in July 2009.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: February 2007 Washington Declaration">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>

     <p>
      In the non-binding "Washington Declaration" on February 16, 2007, the
      <a href="/wiki/G8%2B5" title="G8+5">

      <a href="/wiki/Emissions_trading" title="Emissions trading">

      </a>
      system that would apply to both industrialized nations and
      <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">
       developing countries
      </a>
      , which they hoped would be in place by 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC070828_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC070828-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <i>
          <a class="external text" href="http://www.globeinternational.org/content.php?id=2:9:0:48:0" rel="nofollow">
           Official G8+5 Climate Change Dialogue Web site
          </a>
         </i>
        </dd>
       </dl>
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="33rd_G8_summit">
       33rd G8 summit
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 33rd G8 summit">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:33rdG8Leaders.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="322" data-file-width="514" height="110" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/33rdG8Leaders.jpg/175px-33rdG8Leaders.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/33rdG8Leaders.jpg/263px-33rdG8Leaders.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/33rdG8Leaders.jpg/350px-33rdG8Leaders.jpg 2x" width="175"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:33rdG8Leaders.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Leaders of the 33rd G8 summit
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      On June 7, 2007, leaders at the
      <a href="/wiki/33rd_G8_summit" title="33rd G8 summit">
       33rd G8 summit
      </a>
      issued a non-binding
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communiqu%C3%A9" title="Communiqué">
       communiqué
      </a>
      announcing that the G8 nations would "aim to at least halve global
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" title="Carbon emissions">
       CO
       <sub>
        2
       </sub>
       emissions
      </a>
      by 2050". The details enabling this to be achieved would be negotiated by environment ministers within the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change in a process that would also include the major
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emerging_economies" title="Emerging economies">
       emerging economies
      </a>
      . Groups of countries would also be able to reach additional agreements on achieving the goal outside and in parallel with the United Nations process.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G8-070607_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-G8-070607-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The G8 also announced their desire to use the proceeds from the
      <a href="/wiki/Emissions_trading" title="Emissions trading">
       auction of emission rights
      </a>
      and other financial tools to support
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_global_warming" title="Mitigation of global warming">
       climate protection projects
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">
       developing countries
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G8-070607_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-G8-070607-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The agreement was welcomed by British Prime Minister
      <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">
       Tony Blair
      </a>
      as "a major, major step forward".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      French president
      <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">
       Nicolas Sarkozy
      </a>
      would have preferred a binding figure for emissions reduction to have been set.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      This was apparently blocked by U.S. President
      <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">
       George W. Bush
      </a>
      until the other major
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_carbon_dioxide_emissions" title="List of countries by carbon dioxide emissions">
       greenhouse gas emitting countries
      </a>
      , like India and China, make similar commitments.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <i>
          <a class="external text" href="http://www.g-8.de/Webs/G8/EN/Homepage/home.html" rel="nofollow">
           Official G8 Web site
          </a>
         </i>
        </dd>
       </dl>
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2007_UN_General_Assembly_plenary_debate">
       2007 UN General Assembly plenary debate
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007 UN General Assembly plenary debate">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As part of the schedule leading up to the September UN High-Level-Event, on July 31 the United Nations General Assembly opened its first-ever plenary session devoted exclusively to climate change, which also included prominent scientists and business leaders.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN070730_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN070730-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The debate, at which nearly 100 nations spoke, was scheduled to last two days but was extended for a further day to allow a greater number of "worried nations" to describe their climate-related problems.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox070803_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Fox070803-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In his opening speech,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General" title="United Nations Secretary-General">
       Secretary-General
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">
       Ban Ki-moon
      </a>
      urged Member States to work together, stating that the time had come for "decisive action on a global scale", and called for a "comprehensive agreement under the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">
       United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
      </a>
      process that tackles climate change on all fronts, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adaptation_to_global_warming" title="Adaptation to global warming">
       adaptation
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_global_warming" title="Mitigation of global warming">
       mitigation
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_economy" title="Low-carbon economy">
       clean technologies
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">
       deforestation
      </a>
      and resource mobilization".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN070731_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN070731-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In closing the conference
      <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="President of the United Nations General Assembly">
       General Assembly President
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Haya_Rashed_Al-Khalifa" title="Haya Rashed Al-Khalifa">
       Haya Rashed Al-Khalifa
      </a>
      called for an "equitable, fair and ambitious global deal to match the scale of the challenges ahead".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox070803_9-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Fox070803-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      She had earlier stressed the urgency of the situation, stating that "the longer we wait, the more expensive this will be".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN070731_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN070731-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The day after the session ended, the UN launched its
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.un.org/climatechange/" rel="nofollow">
       new climate change web site
      </a>
      detailing its activities relating to global warming.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN070803_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN070803-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <dl>
      <dd>
       <dl>
        <dd>
         <i>
          <a class="external text" href="http://www.un.org/ga/president/61/follow-up/thematic-climate.shtml" rel="nofollow">
           Official UN web site
          </a>
         </i>
        </dd>
       </dl>
      </dd>
     </dl>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2007_Vienna_Climate_Change_Talks_and_Agreement">
       2007 Vienna Climate Change Talks and Agreement
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007 Vienna Climate Change Talks and Agreement">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A round of climate change talks under the auspices of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) concluded in Austria on 31 August 2007 with agreement on key elements for an effective international response to climate change.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      A key feature of the talks was a United Nations report that showed how
      <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">
       energy efficiency
      </a>
      could yield significant cuts in emissions at low cost.
     </p>
     <p>
      The talks set the stage for the
      <a href="/wiki/2007_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      held in
      <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">
       Bali
      </a>
      in December 2007.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="September_2007_United_Nations_High-Level-Event">
       September 2007 United Nations High-Level-Event
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: September 2007 United Nations High-Level-Event">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As well as the meeting of the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">
       United Nations General Assembly
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General" title="United Nations Secretary-General">
       Secretary-General
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">
       Ban Ki-moon
      </a>
      was to hold informal high-level discussions on the post-Kyoto treaty on September 24. It was expected that these would pave the way for the United Nations Climate Change Conference, held in
      <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">
       Bali
      </a>
      in December 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN07a_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN07a-14">
        [14]
       </a>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Envoy_on_Climate_Change" title="Special Envoy on Climate Change">

      </a>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN07b_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN07b-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      held discussions with various governments to define and plan the event.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN07a_14-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN07a-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In advance of the "High-Level-Event", the Secretary-General hoped that world leaders would "send a powerful political signal to the negotiations in Bali that "business as usual" will not do and that they are ready to work jointly with others towards a comprehensive multilateral framework for action".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN07c_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UN07c-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <dd>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2007_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference_in_Bali">
       2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Bali
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Bali">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/2007_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Negotiations on a successor to the Kyoto Protocol dominated the 2007 United Nations Climate Change Conference. A meeting of environment ministers and experts held in June called on the conference to agree a road-map, timetable and "concrete steps for the negotiations" with a view to reaching an agreement by 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNCOP13_20-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UNCOP13-20">
        [20]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The conference ended with an all-night session of hard bargaining over words and their meaning.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2008_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference_in_Pozna.C5.84">
       2008 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Poznań
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Poznań">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Following preliminary talks in Bangkok, Bonn, and Accra,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]
       </a>
      </sup>
      the 2008 negotiations culminated in December with the
      <a href="/wiki/2008_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2008 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2008 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">
       Poznań
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        [23]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="September_2009_United_Nations_Secretary_General.27s_Summit_on_Climate_Change">
       September 2009 United Nations Secretary General's Summit on Climate Change
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: September 2009 United Nations Secretary General's Summit on Climate Change">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      United Nations Secretary General Ban Ki-Moon convened a high-level event on Climate Change on 22 September 2009 to which Heads of State and Government have been invited. This event was intended to build further political momentum for an ambitious Copenhagen agreed outcome to be adopted at COP-15.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2009_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference_in_Copenhagen_.28COP-15.29">
       2009 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Copenhagen (COP-15)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2009 United Nations Climate Change Conference in Copenhagen (COP-15)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote" role="note">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/2009_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2009 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2009 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg/400px-Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg/600px-Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg/800px-Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg 2x" width="400"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rundet%C3%A5rn_view_2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Copenhagen was the center of climate change negotiations in 2009.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Following preparatory talks in Bonn (in Germany), Bangkok and Barcelona, the 2009 conference was held in December 2009 in
      <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">
       Copenhagen
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
       Denmark
      </a>
      , and the treaty succeeding the
      <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">
       Kyoto Protocol
      </a>
      had been expected to be adopted there.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Some media sources claimed beforehand that the meeting would lead to empty promises without measurable goals. In a meeting of the Group of Eight
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G8" title="G8">
       G8
      </a>
      , the world top leaders agreed to halve carbon emissions by 2050; however, they did not set specific targets because they did not agree on a base year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        [25]
       </a>
      </sup>
      However members of the climate council acknowledged that action needs to happen fast. "My personal view is that the future of humanity is at stake," said
      <a href="/wiki/Tim_Flannery" title="Tim Flannery">
       Tim Flannery
      </a>
      , Professor at Macquarie University and chairman of the Copenhagen Climate Council, in an interview with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinadialogue" title="Chinadialogue">
       chinadialogue
      </a>
      .net.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
       <a href="#cite_note-26">
        [26]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      At the Conference, delegates approved a motion to "take note of the
      <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Accord" title="Copenhagen Accord">
       Copenhagen Accord
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
       <a href="#cite_note-27">
        [27]
       </a>
      </sup>
      of December 18, 2009". The motion was not unanimous, therefore it is not considered to be legally binding. The UN Secretary General Ban Ki-moon welcomed the US-backed climate deal as an "essential beginning", although it subsequently emerged that the US had 'used spying, threats and promises of aid' to gain support for the Accord, under which its emissions pledge is the lowest by any leading nation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
       <a href="#cite_note-28">
        [28]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        [29]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Copenhagen Accord recognises the scientific case for keeping temperature rises below 2 °C, but does not contain commitments for reduced emissions that would be necessary to achieve that aim, let alone 1.5 °C.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        [30]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        [31]
       </a>
      </sup>
      One part of the agreement pledges
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">
       US$
      </a>
      30 billion to the developing world over the next three years, rising to US$100 billion per year by 2020, to help poor countries adapt to climate change. Earlier proposals, that would have aimed to limit temperature rises to 1.5 °C and cut CO
      <sub>
       2
      </sub>
      emissions by 80% by 2050 were dropped. An agreement was also reached that would set up a deal to reduce
      <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">
       deforestation
      </a>
      in return for cash from developed countries.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian1912_32-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guardian1912-32">
        [32]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2011_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference">
       2011 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2011 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/2011_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2011 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2011 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      was held in
      <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">
       Durban
      </a>
      , South Africa, from 28 November to 12 December 2011 to establish a new treaty to limit carbon emissions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        [33]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The president of the conference was
      <a href="/wiki/Maite_Nkoana-Mashabane" title="Maite Nkoana-Mashabane">
       Maite Nkoana-Mashabane
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The conference agreed to a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legally_binding" title="Legally binding">
       legally binding
      </a>
      deal comprising all countries, which will be prepared by 2015, and to take effect in 2020.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_11_Dec_2011_34-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian_11_Dec_2011-34">
        [34]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2012_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference">
       2012 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2012 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/2012_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2012 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2012 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      was held in
      <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">
       Qatar
      </a>
      from 26 November to 7 December 2012. Just before the conference, New Zealand announced it would not be continuing to take part in the Kyoto Protocol.
      <a href="/wiki/Government_of_New_Zealand" title="Government of New Zealand">
       New Zealand
      </a>
      's climate minister
      <a href="/wiki/Tim_Groser" title="Tim Groser">
       Tim Groser
      </a>
      said the 15-year-old agreement was outdated, and that New Zealand was "ahead of the curve" in looking for a replacement that would include developing nations.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        [35]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The conference reached an agreement to extend the life of the Kyoto Protocol until 2020, and to reify the 2011
      <a href="/wiki/2011_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference#Durban_Platform" title="2011 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       Durban Platform
      </a>
      , meaning that a successor to the Protocol is set to be developed by 2015 and implemented by 2020.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2013_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference">
       2013 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2013 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/2013_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2013 United Nations Climate Change Conference">
       2013 United Nations Climate Change Conference
      </a>
      was the 19th yearly session of the Conference of the Parties (COP) to the 1992
      <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">
       United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
      </a>
      (UNFCCC) and the 9th session of the Meeting of the Parties (CMP) to the 1997
      <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">
       Kyoto Protocol
      </a>
      (the protocol having been developed under the UNFCCC's charter). The conference was held in
      <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">
       Warsaw
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
       Poland
      </a>
      from 11 to 22 November 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        [36]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_Summit_2014">
       Climate Summit 2014
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Post%E2%80%93Kyoto_Protocol_negotiations_on_greenhouse_gas_emissions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Climate Summit 2014">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      On 23 September 2014, the UN
      <a href="/wiki/Climate_Summit" title="Climate Summit">
       Climate Summit
      </a>
      2014 was held.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        [37]
       </a>
      </sup>
      India, Russia, Canada and Australia (all of whom are on the top 15 of the countries with the most GHG emissions) did not attend the meeting. 125 other countries did attend. France promised to deposit 750 million into the UN climate fund. Perhaps the biggest announcement came from outside the Climate Summit, and was done by the
      <a href="/wiki/Rockefeller_Brothers_Fund" title="Rockefeller Brothers Fund">
       Rockefeller Brothers Fund
      </a>
      . They announced to withdraw from investing in the
      <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">
       fossil fuel
      </a>
      industry, more specifically from coal and tar sands. According to
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arabella_Advisors&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Arabella Advisors (page does not exist)">
       Arabella Advisors
      </a>
      , 50 billion USD was withdrawn from this industry.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        [38]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It hence marks the beginning of private investors and large companies withdrawing from polluting industries, at a time when the political motivation for reducing GHG emissions is starting to stall.
     </p>
