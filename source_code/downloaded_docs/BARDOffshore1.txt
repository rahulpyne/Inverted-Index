     <p>
      <b>
       BARD Offshore 1
      </b>
      is a 400
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">
       megawatt
      </a>
      (MW)
      <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">
       North Sea
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_farm" title="Offshore wind farm">
       offshore wind farm
      </a>
      with 80 BARD 5.0 turbines. Construction was finished in July 2013
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the wind farm was officially inaugurated in August 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The wind farm is located 100 kilometres (62 mi) northwest of the isle
      <a href="/wiki/Borkum" title="Borkum">
       Borkum
      </a>
      in 40 metres (130 ft) deep water.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Laying of cables to connect the wind farm started on 23 July 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 200 km connection is the longest of its kind in the world. It is also the first connection of an offshore wind park realized as
      <a href="/wiki/HVDC_BorWin1" title="HVDC BorWin1">
       HVDC
      </a>
      -transmission. Construction of the wind turbines began in March 2010.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The first turbine became operational at the beginning of December 2010. Construction was assisted by the purpose-built
      <i>
       Wind Lift 1
      </i>
      barge / platform, which placed the 470 ton, 21 meter foundations on the sea bed.
     </p>
     <p>
      The project has run into serious and unclear problems, including being three years behind schedule and, at a cost of €3 billion, run significantly over budget. A
      <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">
       diver
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and a worker died during construction.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pressemitteilung_27._Januar_2012_BARD_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Pressemitteilung_27._Januar_2012_BARD-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mitteilung_der_DGzRS.2C_abgerufen_am_4._Februar_2012_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Mitteilung_der_DGzRS.2C_abgerufen_am_4._Februar_2012-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The farm was supposed to go online in August 2013, but a series of setbacks, including a fire at a transmission station in March 2014, have delayed its activation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      BARD went
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bankrupt" title="Bankrupt">
       bankrupt
      </a>
      in November 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Problems include
      <a href="/wiki/Overvoltage" title="Overvoltage">
       overvoltage
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Harmonics_(electrical_power)" title="Harmonics (electrical power)">
       harmonics
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      between BARD and the
      <a href="/wiki/HVDC_BorWin1" title="HVDC BorWin1">
       BorWin 1 grid link
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/BorWin_Alpha" title="BorWin Alpha">
       BorWin Alpha HVDC converter platform
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpm2015-05-29_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-wpm2015-05-29-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      BARD's original owner had decided to make all components within the company, and troubles from the custom transformer are unrelated to standard equipment used elsewhere.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As of January 2015
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=BARD_Offshore_1&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      , most of the turbines are not supplying power to shore, costing ratepayers €2million per day.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In September 2015, StatKraft and Ocean Breeze extended their contract for two years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      By May 2016, the company website stated that the wind farm had produced 3 TWh, and was running stable at full capacity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        [16]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
