     <p>
      <b>
       Personal development
      </b>
      covers activities that
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-improvement" title="Self-improvement">
       improve
      </a>
      awareness and identity, develop talents and potential, build
      <a href="/wiki/Human_capital" title="Human capital">
       human capital
      </a>
      and facilitate
      <a href="/wiki/Employability" title="Employability">
       employability
      </a>
      , enhance the
      <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">
       quality of life
      </a>
      and contribute to the realization of dreams and aspirations. Personal development takes place over the course of a person's entire life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Not limited to
      <a href="/wiki/Self-help" title="Self-help">
       self-help
      </a>
      , the concept involves formal and informal activities for developing others in roles such as teacher, guide, counselor, manager, life coach or mentor. When personal development takes place in the context of
      <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">
       institutions
      </a>
      , it refers to the methods, programs, tools, techniques, and assessment systems that support
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_development_(psychology)" title="Human development (psychology)">
       human development
      </a>
      at the individual level in
      <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">
       organizations
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Personal development may include the following activities:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       improving
       <a href="/wiki/Self-awareness" title="Self-awareness">
        self-awareness
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       improving
       <a href="/wiki/Self-knowledge_(psychology)" title="Self-knowledge (psychology)">
        self-knowledge
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       improving
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skills" title="Skills">
        skills
       </a>
       and/or learning new ones
      </li>
      <li>
       building or renewing
       <a href="/wiki/Identity_(social_science)" title="Identity (social science)">
        identity
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Self-esteem" title="Self-esteem">
        self-esteem
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       developing strengths or
       <a href="/wiki/Skill" title="Skill">
        talents
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       improving
       <a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">
        wealth
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       identifying or improving
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_potential_movement" title="Human potential movement">
        potential
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       building
       <a href="/wiki/Employability" title="Employability">
        employability
       </a>
       or (alternatively)
       <a href="/wiki/Human_capital" title="Human capital">
        human capital
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       enhancing
       <a href="/wiki/Lifestyle_(sociology)" title="Lifestyle (sociology)">
        lifestyle
       </a>
       and/or the
       <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">
        quality of life
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       improving
       <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">
        health
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       fulfilling
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aspiration_(long-term_hope)" title="Aspiration (long-term hope)">
        aspirations
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       initiating a
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Life_enterprise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Life enterprise (page does not exist)">
        life enterprise
       </a>
       or (alternatively)
       <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Personal_autonomy_(disambiguation)" title="Personal autonomy (disambiguation)">
        personal autonomy
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       defining and executing
       <a href="/wiki/Personal_development_planning" title="Personal development planning">
        personal development plans
       </a>
       (PDPs)
      </li>
      <li>
       improving
       <a href="/wiki/Social_cognition" title="Social cognition">
        social abilities
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Personal development can also include developing other people. This may take place through roles such as those of a
      <a href="/wiki/Teacher" title="Teacher">
       teacher
      </a>
      or mentor, either through a personal competency (such as the skill of certain
      <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">
       managers
      </a>
      in developing the potential of employees) or through a professional service (such as providing training, assessment or coaching).
     </p>
     <p>
      Beyond improving oneself and developing others, "personal development" labels a field of practice and research. As a field of practice, it includes personal development methods, learning programs,
      <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/assessment" title="wikt:assessment">
       assessment
      </a>
      systems, tools, and techniques. As a field of research, personal development topics increasingly
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
         <span title="This term requires quantification. (August 2015)">
          quantify
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      appear in scientific journals, higher education reviews, management journals and
      <a href="/wiki/Business_sector" title="Business sector">
       business
      </a>
      books.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Any sort of development—whether economic, political, biological, organizational or personal—requires a
      <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Framework_(disambiguation)" title="Framework (disambiguation)">
       framework
      </a>
      if one wishes to know whether a change has actually occurred.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      In the case of personal development, an individual often functions as the primary judge of improvement or of regression, but validation of objective improvement requires assessment using standard criteria. Personal-development frameworks may include goals or benchmarks that define the end-points, strategies or plans for reaching goals, measurement, and assessment of progress, levels or stages that define milestones along a development path, and a feedback system to provide information on changes.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_industry">
       As an industry
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: As an industry">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Personal development as an
      <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">
       industry
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      has several business relationship formats of operating. The main ways are
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business-to-consumer" title="Business-to-consumer">
       business-to-consumer
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Business-to-business" title="Business-to-business">
       business-to-business
      </a>
      . However, there are two newer ways increasing in prevalence:
      <a href="/wiki/Consumer-to-business" title="Consumer-to-business">
       consumer-to-business
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consumer-to-consumer" title="Consumer-to-consumer">
       consumer-to-consumer
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Business-to-consumer_market">
       Business-to-consumer market
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business-to-consumer market">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The business-to-consumer market involves selling books, courses and techniques to individuals, such as:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       newly invented offerings such as:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Physical_fitness" title="Physical fitness">
          fitness
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Cosmetology" title="Cosmetology">
          beauty enhancement
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Weight_loss" title="Weight loss">
          weight loss
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
       traditional practices such as:
       <ul>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">
          yoga
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">
          martial arts
         </a>
        </li>
        <li>
         <a href="/wiki/Meditation" title="Meditation">
          meditation
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Some programs are delivered online and many include
      <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">
       tools
      </a>
      sold with a program, such as motivational books for self-help, recipes for weight-loss or technical manuals for yoga and martial-arts programs.
     </p>
     <p>
      A partial list of personal development offerings on the business-to-individual market might include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       books
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Motivational_speaker" title="Motivational speaker">
        motivational speaking
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E-Learning" title="E-Learning">
        e-Learning
       </a>
       programs
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Workshop_(disambiguation)" title="Workshop (disambiguation)">
        workshops
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       individual
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_counseling_topics" title="List of counseling topics">
        counseling
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_coaching" title="Life coaching">
        life coaching
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Management" title="Time Management">
        Time Management
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Business-to-business_market">
       Business-to-business market
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      Major religions – such as the

      <a href="/wiki/Indian_religions" title="Indian religions">

      <a href="/wiki/Prayer" title="Prayer">

      </a>

       music
      </a>

      </a>

       sports
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Martial_arts" title="Martial arts">
       martial arts
      </a>
      . These practices have various functions, such as health or aesthetic satisfaction, but they may also link
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>

      </sup>

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/The_good_life" title="The good life">
       the good life
      </a>
      (compare
      <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">
       philosophy
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Michel_Foucault" title="Michel Foucault">
       Michel Foucault

      </i>

       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]

      </i>

      <a href="/wiki/Dieting" title="Dieting">
       dieting
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">
       Christianity
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In yoga, a discipline originating in
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>

     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Acupuncture" title="Acupuncture">
       acupuncture
      </a>
      .

      <a href="/wiki/Prayer" title="Prayer">

      <a href="/wiki/Tazkiah" title="Tazkiah">

     <p>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Aristotle_and_the_Western_tradition">
       Aristotle and the Western tradition
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     <p>
      The Greek philosopher
      <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">
       Aristotle
      </a>
      (384 BCE – 322 BCE) influenced theories
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">

          which?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      of personal development in the West. In his
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Nicomachean_Ethics" title="Nicomachean Ethics">
        Nicomachean Ethics
       </a>
      </i>
      , Aristotle defined personal development as a category of
      <a href="/wiki/Phronesis" title="Phronesis">
       phronesis
      </a>
      or practical wisdom, where the practice of virtues (
      <i>
       arête
      </i>
      ) leads to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Eudaimonia" title="Eudaimonia">
        eudaimonia
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      commonly translated as "happiness" but more accurately understood as "human flourishing" or "living well".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Aristotle continues to influence the Western concept of personal development to this day
      <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
       <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit">
        [update]
       </a>
      </sup>
      , particularly in the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Economics_of_human_development&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Economics of human development (page does not exist)">
       economics of human development
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and in
      <a href="/wiki/Positive_psychology" title="Positive psychology">
       positive psychology
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Confucius_and_the_East_Asian_tradition">
       Confucius and the East Asian tradition
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Confucius and the East Asian tradition">

       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h3>
     <p>
      In Chinese tradition,
      <a href="/wiki/Confucius" title="Confucius">
       Confucius
      </a>
      (around 551 BCE – 479 BCE) founded an ongoing philosophy. His ideas continue to influence
      <a href="/wiki/Family_values" title="Family values">
       family values
      </a>
      , education and
      <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">
       management
      </a>
      in China and East Asia. In his
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Learning" title="Great Learning">
        Great Learning
       </a>
      </i>
      Confucius wrote:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       The ancients who wished to illustrate illustrious virtue throughout the kingdom, first ordered well their own states. Wishing to order well their states, they first regulated their families. Wishing to regulate their families, they first cultivated their persons. Wishing to cultivate their persons, they first rectified their hearts. Wishing to rectify their hearts, they first sought to be sincere in their thoughts. Wishing to be sincere in their thoughts, they first extended to the utmost their knowledge. Such extension of knowledge lay in the investigation of things.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Psychology">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">
       Psychology
      </a>
      became linked to personal development starting
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">

       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      with
      <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Adler" title="Alfred Adler">
       Alfred Adler
      </a>
      (1870–1937) and
      <a href="/wiki/Carl_Jung" title="Carl Jung">
       Carl Jung
      </a>
      (1875–1961).
     </p>
     <p>
      Adler refused to limit psychology to analysis, making the important point that aspirations look forward and do not limit themselves to unconscious drives or to childhood experiences.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
      He also originated the concepts of
      <a href="/wiki/Lifestyle_(sociology)" title="Lifestyle (sociology)">
       lifestyle
      </a>
      (1929—he defined "lifestyle" as an individual's characteristic approach to life, in facing problems) and of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self_image" title="Self image">
       self image
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      a concept that influenced management under the heading of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Work-life_balance" title="Work-life balance">
       work-life balance
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
         <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (October 2010)">
          clarification needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Carl Gustav Jung made contributions to personal development with his concept of
      <a href="/wiki/Individuation" title="Individuation">
       individuation
      </a>
      , which he saw as the drive of the individual to achieve the wholeness and balance of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self_(psychology)" title="Self (psychology)">
       Self
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Levinson" title="Daniel Levinson">
       Daniel Levinson
      </a>
      (1920–1994) developed Jung's early concept of "
      <a href="/wiki/Developmental_stage_theories" title="Developmental stage theories">
       life stages
      </a>
      " and included a sociological perspective. Levinson proposed that personal development come under the influence—throughout
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_life" title="Personal life">
       life
      </a>
      —of aspirations, which he called "the Dream":
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       Whatever the nature of his Dream, a young man has the developmental task of giving it greater definition and finding ways to live it out. It makes a great difference in his growth whether his initial life structure is consonant with and infused by the Dream, or opposed to it. If the Dream remains unconnected to his life it may simply die, and with it his sense of aliveness and purpose.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Levinson's model of seven life-stages has been considerably modified
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (March 2009)">
          by whom?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      due to sociological changes
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2010)">
          which?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Biological_life_cycle" title="Biological life cycle">
       lifecycle
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        [17]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Research on success in reaching goals, as undertaken by
      <a href="/wiki/Albert_Bandura" title="Albert Bandura">
       Albert Bandura
      </a>
      (born 1925), suggested that
      <a href="/wiki/Self-efficacy" title="Self-efficacy">
       self-efficacy
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        [18]
       </a>
      </sup>
      best explains why people with the same level of knowledge and skills get very different results. According to Bandura
      <a href="/wiki/Self-confidence" title="Self-confidence">
       self-confidence
      </a>
      functions as a powerful predictor of success because:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        [19]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <ol>
      <li>
       it makes you expect to succeed
      </li>
      <li>
       it allows you take risks and set challenging goals
      </li>
      <li>
       it helps you keep trying if at first you don't succeed
      </li>
      <li>
       it helps you control emotions and fears when the going gets rough
      </li>
     </ol>
     <p>
      In 1998
      <a href="/wiki/Martin_Seligman" title="Martin Seligman">
       Martin Seligman
      </a>
      won election to a one-year term as President of the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Psychological_Association" title="American Psychological Association">
       American Psychological Association
      </a>
      and proposed a new focus: on healthy individuals
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      rather than on pathology (he created the "
      <a href="/wiki/Positive_psychology" title="Positive psychology">
       positive psychology
      </a>
      " current)
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       We have discovered that there is a set of human strengths that are the most likely buffers against mental illness: courage, optimism, interpersonal skill, work ethic, hope, honesty and perseverance. Much of the task of prevention will be to create a science of human strength whose mission will be to foster these virtues in young people.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <h3>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <p>

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">

         </span>

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

         </span>

       </i>
       ]
      </sup>

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">

         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_von_Humboldt" title="Wilhelm von Humboldt">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_University_of_Berlin" title="Humboldt University of Berlin">

      </a>
      ) in 1810, made a statement interpretable
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">

      </sup>

      <i>

      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        [21]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        [22]

      </li>

       <a href="/wiki/Emotion" title="Emotion">
        emotions
       </a>
      </li>

       <a href="/wiki/Autonomy" title="Autonomy">

      </li>
      <li>
       developing
       <a href="/wiki/Integrity" title="Integrity">

       </a>
      </li>

     <p>
      In the UK, personal development took a central place in university policy
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      in 1997 when the
      <a href="/wiki/Dearing_Report" title="Dearing Report">
       Dearing Report
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        [24]
       </a>

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">

      </sup>
      for universities to enhance personal development as:
     </p>

      <i>
       * a structured and supported process undertaken by an individual to reflect upon their own learning, performance and / or achievement and to plan for their personal, educational and career development;
      </i>
      <br/>
      <i>
       * objectives related explicitly to student development; to improve the capacity of students to understand what and how they are learning, and to review, plan and take responsibility for their own learning

      </a>
      set up the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Quality_Improvement_System" title="European Quality Improvement System">

      </a>

      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_workplace">
       The workplace
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: The workplace">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Maslow" title="Abraham Maslow">
       Abraham Maslow
      </a>
      (1908–1970), proposed a
      <a href="/wiki/Maslow%27s_hierarchy_of_needs" title="Maslow's hierarchy of needs">
       hierarchy of needs
      </a>
      with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self_actualization" title="Self actualization">
       self actualization
      </a>
      at the top, defined as:
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        [29]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       … the desire to become more and more what one is, to become everything that one is capable of becoming.
      </i>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since Maslow himself believed that only a small minority of people
      <a href="/wiki/Self-actualization" title="Self-actualization">
       self-actualize
      </a>
      —he estimated one percent
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        [30]
       </a>
      </sup>
      —his hierarchy of needs had the consequence that organizations came to regard self-actualization or personal development as occurring at the top of the organizational pyramid, while job security and good working conditions would fulfill the needs of the mass of employees.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As organizations and labor markets became more global,
      <a href="/wiki/Moral_responsibility" title="Moral responsibility">
       responsibility
      </a>
      for development shifted from the company to the individual.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
         <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2010)">
          clarification needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      In 1999 management thinker
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Drucker" title="Peter Drucker">
       Peter Drucker
      </a>
      wrote in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_Review" title="Harvard Business Review">
        Harvard Business Review
       </a>
      </i>
      :
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       We live in an age of unprecedented opportunity: if you've got ambition and smarts, you can rise to the top of your chosen profession, regardless of where you started out. But with opportunity comes responsibility. Companies today aren't managing their employees' careers;
       <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_worker" title="Knowledge worker">
        knowledge workers
       </a>
       must, effectively, be their own chief executive officers. It's up to you to carve out your place, to know when to change course, and to keep yourself engaged and productive during a work life that may span some 50 years.
       <i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
         <a href="#cite_note-31">
          [31]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </i>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Management professors
      <a href="/wiki/Sumantra_Ghoshal" title="Sumantra Ghoshal">
       Sumantra Ghoshal
      </a>
      of the London Business School and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christopher_Bartlett" title="Christopher Bartlett">
       Christopher Bartlett
      </a>
      of the Harvard Business School wrote in 1997 that companies must manage people individually and establish a new work contract.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        [32]
       </a>
      </sup>
      On the one hand the company must allegedly recognize that personal development creates economic value: "market performance flows not from the omnipotent wisdom of top managers but from the initiative, creativity and skills of all employees".
     </p>
     <p>
      On the other hand, employees should recognize that their work includes personal development and "... embrace the invigorating force of continuous learning and personal development".
     </p>
     <p>
      The 1997 publication of Ghoshal's and Bartlett's
      <i>
       Individualized Corporation
      </i>
      corresponded to a change in
      <a href="/wiki/Career_development" title="Career development">
       career development
      </a>
      from a system of predefined paths defined by companies, to a strategy defined by the individual and matched to the needs of organizations in an open landscape of possibilities.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Another contribution to the study of career development came with the recognition that women's careers show specific personal needs and different development paths from men. The 2007 study of women's careers by Sylvia Ann Hewlett
      <i>
       Off-Ramps and On-Ramps
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        [33]
       </a>
      </sup>
      had a major impact on the way companies view careers.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Further work on the career as a personal development process came from study by Herminia Ibarra in her
      <i>
       Working Identity
      </i>
      on the relationship with career change and identity change,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        [34]
       </a>
      </sup>
      indicating that priorities of
      <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">
       work
      </a>
      and lifestyle continually develop through life.
     </p>
     <p>
      Personal development programs in companies fall into two categories: the provision of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employee_benefit" title="Employee benefit">
       employee benefits
      </a>
      and the fostering of development strategies.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Employee_benefit" title="Employee benefit">
       Employee benefits
      </a>
      have the purpose of improving satisfaction, motivation and loyalty.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Employee surveys may help organizations find out personal-development needs, preferences and problems, and they use the results to design benefits programs.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Typical programs in this category include:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Work-life_balance" title="Work-life balance">
        work-life balance
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Time_management" title="Time management">
        time management
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Stress_management" title="Stress management">
        stress management
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">
        health
       </a>
       programs
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counseling" title="Counseling">
        counseling
       </a>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Many such programs resemble programs that some employees might conceivably pay for themselves outside work: yoga, sports, martial arts, money-management, positive psychology,
      <a href="/wiki/Neuro-linguistic_programming" title="Neuro-linguistic programming">
       NLP
      </a>
      , etc.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As an investment, personal development programs have the goal of increasing
      <a href="/wiki/Human_capital" title="Human capital">
       human capital
      </a>
      or improving
      <a href="/wiki/Productivity" title="Productivity">
       productivity
      </a>
      , innovation or quality. Proponents actually see such programs not as a cost but as an investment with results linked to an organization's strategic development goals. Employees gain access to these investment-oriented programs by selection according to the value and future potential of the employee, usually defined in a
      <a href="/wiki/Talent_management" title="Talent management">
       talent management
      </a>
      architecture including populations such as new hires, perceived high-potential employees, perceived key employees, sales staff, research staff and perceived future
      <a href="/wiki/Leadership" title="Leadership">
       leaders
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      Organizations may also offer other (non-investment-oriented) programs to many or even all employees. Typical programs
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2009)">
          which?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      focus on career-development,
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_effectiveness" title="Personal effectiveness">
       personal effectiveness
      </a>
      , teamwork, and competency-development. Personal development also forms an element in
      <a href="/wiki/Management" title="Management">
       management
      </a>
      tools such as
      <a href="/wiki/Personal_development_planning" title="Personal development planning">
       personal development planning
      </a>
      , assessing one's level of ability using a
      <a href="/wiki/Competence_(human_resources)" title="Competence (human resources)">
       competency
      </a>
      grid, or getting feedback from a
      <a href="/wiki/360-degree_feedback" title="360-degree feedback">
       360 questionnaire
      </a>
      filled in by colleagues at different levels in the organization.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">
       Criticism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Personal_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Criticism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Scholars have targeted self-help claims as misleading and incorrect. In 2005,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Salerno&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steve Salerno (page does not exist)">
       Steve Salerno
      </a>
      portrayed the American self-help movement—he uses the acronym
      <i>
       SHAM:
      </i>
      <i>
       The Self-Help and
       <a href="/wiki/Self-actualization" title="Self-actualization">
        Actualization
       </a>
