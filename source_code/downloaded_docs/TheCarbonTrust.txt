     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Carbon Trust
      </b>
      is a not-for-dividend company that helps organisations and companies reduce their carbon emissions and become more resource efficient. Its stated mission is to accelerate the move to a sustainable,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_carbon_economy" title="Low carbon economy">
       low carbon economy
      </a>
      . It reinvests surpluses from its group commercial activities into its mission.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust helps companies and organisations reduce carbon emissions and increase resource efficiency through providing specialist help, support and advice. As of December 2014 the Carbon Trust had saved its customers £5.5bn in costs and 60Mt
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">
       CO
       <sub>
        2
       </sub>
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It operates globally and has offices in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.K." title="U.K.">
       U.K.
      </a>
      , China, Mexico,
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
       Brazil
      </a>
      and United States. It is also active in many other countries including
      <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
       South Korea
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">
       Malaysia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
       Singapore
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
       Indonesia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">
       Namibia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
       Peru
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
       Chile
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
       Panama
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Services">
       Services
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Business_advice">
       Business advice
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business advice">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust looks at current and future
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">
       sustainability
      </a>
      challenges and works with business and organisations to develop sustainable strategies to deliver savings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_footprinting.2C_verification_and_Carbon_Trust_standard">
       Carbon footprinting, verification and Carbon Trust standard
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Carbon footprinting, verification and Carbon Trust standard">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust provides voluntary carbon certification services and carbon labelling schemes – it verifies organisation and product carbon footprint data and provides marks of quality to organisations to demonstrate standards have been met.
     </p>
     <p>
      From February 2013 the Carbon Trust introduced the water standard to go alongside its carbon standard.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In November 2013 it announced the launch of the world's first international standard for organisational waste reduction.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2015 it launched the Carbon Trust Supply Chain Standard to look at carbon footprints across the
      <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">
       supply chain
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Developing_clean_technology">
       Developing clean technology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Developing clean technology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust works with governments, innovators and corporates with the aim of accelerating the commercialisation of low carbon technologies, and leads projects to deliver commercial partnerships and develop low carbon technologies.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is particularly active in the areas of offshore wind, marine energy, fuel cell development and industrial energy efficiency. One such project is the Offshore Wind Accelerator, which is aimed at reducing the cost of wind power through projects focused in the North Sea. The Offshore Wind Accelerator is a partnership between industry and governments.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_and_Markets">
       Policy and Markets
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Policy and Markets">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust provides analysis on
      <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">
       sustainability
      </a>
      issues to help businesses, investors and policy makers with their roles in reducing carbon and saving energy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It works with companies and governments across the world and is presently developing a range of policy and market focused projects including in Mexico, South Africa, Scotland, Brazil and the United Kingdom.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_footprinting.2C_verification_and_Carbon_Trust_standard_2">
       Carbon footprinting, verification and Carbon Trust standard
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Carbon footprinting, verification and Carbon Trust standard">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_Trust_standards">
       Carbon Trust standards
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Carbon Trust standards">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust runs a series of environmental standards that certify measurement and reduction. Currently these cover carbon, water and waste and have been awarded to hundreds of leading companies and organisations across the world.
     </p>
     <p>
      In June 2008 the Carbon Trust introduced the Carbon Trust Carbon Standard to address what it describes as business
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenwash" title="Greenwash">
       greenwash
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Carbon Trust Carbon Standard is only awarded to companies and organisations who measure and reduce their carbon emissions year on year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Examples of organisations who hold the Carbon Standard include
      <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">
       Sky
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">
       Aldi
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Eurotunnel" title="Eurotunnel">
       Eurotunnel
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bupa" title="Bupa">
       Bupa
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pricewaterhousecoopers" title="Pricewaterhousecoopers">
       Pricewaterhousecoopers
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">
       Samsung Electronics
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angus_Council" title="Angus Council">
       Angus Council
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Capital_%26_Regional" title="Capital &amp; Regional">
       Capital &amp; Regional
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telefonica_Europe" title="Telefonica Europe">
       O2
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/RWE_npower" title="RWE npower">
       RWE npower
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Credit_Suisse" title="Credit Suisse">
       Credit Suisse
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">
       Scottish Government
      </a>
      and the UK
      <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/DECC" title="DECC">
       Department of Energy and Climate Change
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In February 2013 the Carbon Trust introduced the Carbon Trust Water Standard to recognise those companies reducing their water use year on year. The first four companies to receive the Water Standard were
      <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury's">
       Sainsbury's
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coca_Cola" title="Coca Cola">
       Coca Cola
      </a>
      Enterprises Ltd, Sunlight Services Group, and Branston.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In July 2013 the Carbon Trust introuduced the Carbon Trust Waste Standard.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In November 2013 the waste standard was awarded to the first wave of organisations, which included
      <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">
       the Football Association
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Renishaw_plc" title="Renishaw plc">
       Renishaw plc
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Whitbread" title="Whitbread">
       Whitbread
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pricewaterhousecoopers" title="Pricewaterhousecoopers">
       Pricewaterhousecoopers
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/AkzoNobel" title="AkzoNobel">
       AkzoNobel
      </a>
      Decorative Paints. These last three became the first in the world to gain the triple crown of reaching the carbon, water and waste standard.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2015 the Carbon Trust launched the Carbon Trust Supply Chain Standard to look at carbon footprints across the supply chain. It is the world’s first independent certification for organisations that are measuring, managing and reducing greenhouse gas (CO2e) emissions in their supply chains.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2016 the Carbon Trust launched the Carbon Trust Zero Waste to Landfill Standard.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_footprint_label">
       Carbon footprint label
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Carbon footprint label">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust helps companies to measure the carbon emissions associated with their products (embodied emissions) and also provides a label for these products
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">
       carbon footprint
      </a>
      . Measuring the embodied emissions of products enables reductions to be identified and achieved across the supply chain. The label demonstrates a commitment by the product owner to reduce that footprint every two years. The
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emission_label" title="Carbon emission label">
       Carbon Reduction Label
      </a>
      was introduced in the UK in March 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Examples of products that have featured their carbon footprint are
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingsmill_(bread)" title="Kingsmill (bread)">
       Kingsmill
      </a>
      bread,
      <a href="/wiki/Walkers_(snack_foods)" title="Walkers (snack foods)">
       Walkers
      </a>
      Crisps, a range of own brand products in
      <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">
       Tesco
      </a>
      supermarkets, Halifax (
      <a href="/wiki/HBOS" title="HBOS">
       HBOS
      </a>
      ) bank accounts,
      <a href="/wiki/Dyson_(company)" title="Dyson (company)">
       Dyson
      </a>
      airblades,
      <a href="/wiki/Marshalls" title="Marshalls">
       Marshalls
      </a>
      building products,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quaker_oats" title="Quaker oats">
       Quaker oats
      </a>
      , Silver Spoon sugar,
      <a href="/wiki/Tate_%26_Lyle" title="Tate &amp; Lyle">
       Tate &amp; Lyle
      </a>
      sugar,
      <a href="/wiki/Lafarge_(company)" title="Lafarge (company)">
       Lafarge
      </a>
      cement,
      <a href="/wiki/Quorn" title="Quorn">
       Quorn
      </a>
      foods and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pompeian_(olive_oil)" title="Pompeian (olive oil)">
       Pompeian
      </a>
      Olive Oil.
     </p>
     <p>
      The standards behind carbon labelling are now formally recognised through the PAS 2050 developed by the Carbon Trust in conjunction with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Standards_Institution" title="British Standards Institution">
       BSI
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defra" title="Defra">
       Defra
      </a>
      . This methodology is now gaining international acceptance following its launch in October 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        [13]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Funds_and_financing">
       Funds and financing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Funds and financing">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Carbon Trust also supports the development and deployment of low carbon technologies and is actively engaged in the
      <a href="/wiki/Fuel_cell" title="Fuel cell">
       fuel cell
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wave_energy" title="Wave energy">
       wave energy
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_energy" title="Wind energy">
       wind energy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       solar energy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">
       biomass
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biofuels" title="Biofuels">
       biofuels
      </a>
      sectors. It has a particularly active role in the Offshore Wind sector through its Offshore Wind Accelerator programme.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        [14]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      It is involved in a number of finance schemes – including an Energy efficiency financing scheme across the UK in partnership with
      <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">
       Siemens
      </a>
      Financial Services worth around £550 million
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        [15]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and interest-free loans for
      <a href="/wiki/Small_business" title="Small business">
       small and medium-sized enterprises
      </a>
      available for energy-efficient equipment in Northern Ireland, in conjunction with Invest Northern Ireland.
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2016 it launched its Green Business Fund offering a range of services for
      <a href="/wiki/Small_and_medium-sized_enterprises" title="Small and medium-sized enterprises">
       SMEs
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
       Great Britain
      </a>
      including up to £10,000 towards buying energy efficient equipment.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Subsidiary_companies">
       Subsidiary companies
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Subsidiary companies">

        ]
       </span>
      </span>

     <p>
      The Carbon Trust has created a number of successful subsidiary companies such as Partnership for Renewables which develops
      <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">

      on
      <a href="/wiki/Public_land" title="Public land">
       public sector land
      </a>
      and Low Carbon Workplace which funds and develops the low carbon refurbishment of buildings; and Future Blends which converts biomass to transport fuel.
     </p>
