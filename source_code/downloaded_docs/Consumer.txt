     <p>
      A
      <b>
       consumer
      </b>
      is a person or organization that uses
      <a href="/wiki/Service_(economics)" title="Service (economics)">
       economic services
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Commodity" title="Commodity">
       commodities
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Economic_system" title="Economic system">
       economic systems
      </a>
      <i>
       consumers
      </i>
      are
      <a href="/wiki/Utility" title="Utility">
       utilities
      </a>
      expressed in the decision to
      <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">
       trade
      </a>
      or not.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Economics_and_marketing">
       Economics and marketing
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Economics and marketing">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <i>
       consumer
      </i>
      is the one who pays to consume goods and services produced. As such,
      <i>
       consumers
      </i>
      play a vital role in the
      <a href="/wiki/Economic_system" title="Economic system">
       economic system
      </a>
      of a
      <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">
       nation
      </a>
      . Without consumer
      <a href="/wiki/Demand" title="Demand">
       demand
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Production_(economics)" title="Production (economics)">
       producers
      </a>
      would lack one of the key motivations to produce:
      <a href="/wiki/Sales" title="Sales">
       to sell
      </a>
      to consumers. The
      <i>
       consumer
      </i>
      also forms part of the
      <a href="/wiki/Distribution_(economics)" title="Distribution (economics)">
       chain of distribution
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Recently in
      <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">
       marketing
      </a>
      instead of marketers generating broad
      <a href="/wiki/Demographic_profile" title="Demographic profile">
       demographic profiles
      </a>
      and Fisio-graphic profiles of
      <a href="/wiki/Market_segmentation" title="Market segmentation">
       market segments
      </a>
      , marketers have started to engage in
      <a href="/wiki/Personalized_marketing" title="Personalized marketing">
       personalized marketing
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Permission_marketing" title="Permission marketing">
       permission marketing
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Mass_customization" title="Mass customization">
       mass customization
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Largely due the rise of the
      <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">
       Internet
      </a>
      , consumers are shifting more and more towards becoming "
      <a href="/wiki/Prosumer" title="Prosumer">
       prosumers
      </a>
      " – consumers that are also producers (often of information and media on the
      <a href="/wiki/Social_web" title="Social web">
       social web
      </a>
      ) or influence the products created (e.g. by customization,
      <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">
       crowdfunding
      </a>
      or publishing their preferences) or actively participate in the production process or use interactive products.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_politics">
       Law and politics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Law and politics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The law primarily uses the notion of the consumer in relation to
      <a href="/wiki/Consumer_protection" title="Consumer protection">
       consumer protection
      </a>
      laws, and the definition of consumer is often restricted to living persons (i.e. not corporations or businesses) and excludes commercial users.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      A typical legal rationale for protecting the consumer is based on the notion of policing market failures and inefficiencies, such as inequalities of bargaining power between a consumer and a business.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      As of all potential voters are also consumers, consumer protection takes on a clear political significance.
     </p>
     <p>
      Concern over the interests of consumers has also spawned activism, as well as incorporation of consumer education into school curricula.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      There are also various non-profit publications, such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Which%3F" title="Which?">
        Which?
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Reports" title="Consumer Reports">
        Consumer Reports
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choice_Magazine" title="Choice Magazine">
        Choice Magazine
       </a>
      </i>
      , dedicated to assist in consumer education and decision making.
     </p>
     <p>
      In India,
      <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Protection_Act,_1986" title="Consumer Protection Act, 1986">
       the Consumer Protection Act 1986
      </a>
      differentiates the consummation of a commodity or service for personal use or to earn a livelihood. Only consumers are protected per this act and any person, entity or organization purchasing a commodity for commercial reasons are exempted from any benefits of this act.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Public_reaction">
       Public reaction
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public reaction">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      While use of the term
      <i>
       consumer
      </i>
      is widespread among governmental, business and media organisations, many individuals and groups find the label objectionable because it assigns a limited and passive role to their activities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
