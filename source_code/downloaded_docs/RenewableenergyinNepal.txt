     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_energy">
       Solar energy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Solar energy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Power cuts with an average of 10 hours per day
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      have been common in Nepal and Nepal Electricity Authority
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      publishes a time table for power cuts.
      <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">
       Solar energy
      </a>
      can be seen as a more reliable source of energy in Nepal than the traditional electricity. Private installations of solar panels are more frequent in Nepal. The US Embassy has installed the solar panels.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Places such as Madi, Chitwan where the Electricity Authority do not provide electricity
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      because of
      <a href="/wiki/Chitwan_National_Park" title="Chitwan National Park">
       Chitwan National Park
      </a>
      , people there have been relying on solar power
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      for several years.
     </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <p>
      Electric powered
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      public three wheeler have been in use to reduce
      <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">
       carbon dioxide (CO
       <sub>
        2

       )

      emissions.
