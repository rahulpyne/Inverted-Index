     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Shepherds Flat Wind Farm
      </b>
      is an 845
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">
       megawatt
      </a>
      (MW)
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">
       wind farm
      </a>
      in the U.S. state of
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">
       Oregon
      </a>
      . The facility is located in
      <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Oregon" title="Eastern Oregon">
       Eastern Oregon
      </a>
      in both
      <a href="/wiki/Morrow_County,_Oregon" title="Morrow County, Oregon">
       Morrow
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Gilliam_County,_Oregon" title="Gilliam County, Oregon">
       Gilliam
      </a>
      counties, near
      <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Oregon" title="Arlington, Oregon">
       Arlington
      </a>
      . Approved in 2008 by state regulators, groundbreaking came in 2009. The wind farm was built by Caithness Energy using
      <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">
       General Electric
      </a>
      (GE) 2.5 MW
      <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">
       wind turbines
      </a>
      , and it supplies electricity to
      <a href="/wiki/Southern_California_Edison" title="Southern California Edison">
       Southern California Edison
      </a>
      . The wind farm is estimated to have an economic impact of $16 million annually for Oregon. It is one of the largest land-based wind farms in the world. It officially opened in September 2012.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Details">
       Details
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shepherds_Flat_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Details">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The wind farm is located in
      <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Oregon" title="Eastern Oregon">
       Eastern Oregon
      </a>
      in both
      <a href="/wiki/Morrow_County,_Oregon" title="Morrow County, Oregon">
       Morrow
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Gilliam_County,_Oregon" title="Gilliam County, Oregon">
       Gilliam
      </a>
      counties, with the majority of the turbines to be in Gilliam County.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EO_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-EO-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Oregon provided tax incentives to the developer to help land the facility,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EO_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-EO-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the project is expected to provide Gilliam County with about $5 million annually in taxes and fees when it is operating.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EO_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-EO-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Shepherds Flat is entirely on private property approximately 5 miles (8.0 km) southeast of the city of Arlington between
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Route_19" title="Oregon Route 19">
       Oregon Route 19
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Route_74" title="Oregon Route 74">
       Oregon Route 74
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pbj_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pbj-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Construction on the project is expected to employ 400 people to build 90 miles (140 km) of power lines and 85 miles (137 km) of roads on the 30-square-mile (78 km
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) wind farm.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Plans for the project first came in 2002 for a 105 megawatt project, with the plan changed to larger project in 2004.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        [5]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The project was then submitted to the state in 2006 with plans to have the first phase online in 2008.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Early plans called for 303 turbines and a generating capacity of 909
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">
       megawatts
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-renrewable_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-renrewable-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the time, it would have doubled the capacity for wind-generated power in Oregon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The farm is divided into several sections, with the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Willow_Creek_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Willow Creek Wind Farm (page does not exist)">
       Willow Creek Wind Farm
      </a>
      between two of the larger segments.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Approved in 2008 by state regulators,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pbj_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-pbj-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
      groundbreaking came in 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EO_1-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-EO-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The project design now includes 338
      <a href="/wiki/GE_Wind_Energy#GE_2.5MW" title="GE Wind Energy">
       GE2.5XL
      </a>
      turbines built by
      <a href="/wiki/GE_Wind_Energy" title="GE Wind Energy">
       GE Wind Energy
      </a>
      , each with a capacity to produce 2.5 megawatts,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      and used for the first time in USA.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfDoe_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-sfDoe-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      These turbines and a ten-year service contract cost a total of $1.4 billion, the largest expense on the estimated $2 billion project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      In December 2010, the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">
       Department of Energy
      </a>
      (DOE) offered a $1.3 billion
      <a href="/wiki/Loan_guarantee" title="Loan guarantee">
       loan guarantee
      </a>
      for the project. The insurance fee for the loan guarantee would be paid from federal stimulus money through the Financial Institution Partnership Program.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfDoe_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-sfDoe-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Generating capacity will be 845 megawatts, producing an estimated 2 billion
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KWh" title="KWh">
       kWh
      </a>
      each year, enough to provide electricity to 235,000 homes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Once operational, the facility will have about 35 permanent employees.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_4-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn-4">
        [4]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The wind farm is estimated to have an economic impact of $16 million annually for Oregon.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Itochu" title="Itochu">

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Concerns">
       Concerns
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shepherds_Flat_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Concerns">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      There are concerns that have been raised by taxpayer watchdog groups and various blogs. They point out that large private ranch owners in the area will receive up to $12,000 annually per turbine leased on their land while they have already received tens of millions of dollars in U.S. government subsidies over the years and then benefiting from taxpayer subsidies of the wind energy industry. Some local ranchers due to benefit or already benefiting from the partially government subsidized wind energy projects were published as receiving nearly $1 million in government agricultural subsidies in 2008 alone.

       <a href="#cite_note-15">
